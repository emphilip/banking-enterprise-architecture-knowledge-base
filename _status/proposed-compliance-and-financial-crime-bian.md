# Proposed Compliance & Financial Crime Business Capabilities

Deeper L3 / L4 business capabilities extending the existing L1 (Financial Crime
Compliance), the three L2s (KYC Management, AML Monitoring, Regulatory
Compliance) and one new L2 (Financial Crime Risk Compliance), plus the three
already-existing L3s (Transaction Monitoring, Sanctions Screening, Regulatory
Reporting). Every name was checked against `glossary/_canonical-names.md` for
global uniqueness across all sections (capabilities, processes, tech,
sub-capabilities, systems, glossary).

Disambiguators applied to avoid collisions with prior domains:
- "AML Customer Due Diligence" (not "Customer Due Diligence", which is an existing
  Customer Management L3) and "AML Case Investigation" (not "Case Management",
  an existing Customer Management L3).
- "SAR Management" / "Suspicious Activity Detection" (the process "Suspicious
  Activity Reporting" already exists; these capabilities are distinct).
- "Bribery & Corruption Risk Assessment" (not "Fraud Risk Assessment", an
  existing Risk Management L3) and "Conduct Risk Management" (not the existing
  "Complaint Management").
- "Compliance Policy Management" (not the existing tech "Policy & Control
  Management") and "Compliance Risk Assessment" (not the existing Risk caps).
- "Watchlist Management" / "Adverse Media Screening" extend AML Monitoring beyond
  the existing "Sanctions Screening" and "Transaction Monitoring".

Grounded in: FATF 40 Recommendations (R.10 CDD, R.12/22 PEPs, R.16 wire transfers,
R.20/21 STR, R.24/25 beneficial ownership), US BSA/AML 5 Pillars and FinCEN CDD /
Beneficial Ownership Rule, OFAC 2019 Framework for Sanctions Compliance
Commitments, Wolfsberg Group CBDDQ/FCCQ and monitoring guidance, EU AMLD5/AMLD6
(Dir (EU) 2024/1640), UK MLR 2017, and BIAN Regulatory Compliance / Regulatory
Reporting / Party Authentication service domains.
