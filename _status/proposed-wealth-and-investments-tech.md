# Proposed Wealth & Investments Technology Decomposition

Scope: the Wealth & Investments business domain currently has **no dedicated
top-level technology capability** in the registry (only the business caps
`Wealth Management` (L1), `Portfolio Management`, `Investment Advisory` and
`Brokerage & Trading` (L2), plus the `Wealth Onboarding` process exist). This
proposal therefore introduces **3 NEW top-level technology capabilities** in the
`Core Processing` technology domain to enable the Wealth business capabilities,
decomposes them into **11 L2 sub-capabilities** and **3 optional L3
sub-capabilities**, and performs a full **systems refresh** (the registry has no
wealth systems today) pairing legacy and modern investment platforms with
same-capability `supersedes` links.

Grounding: wealth/investment-management reference architectures and vendor docs —
front-to-back investment platforms (BlackRock Aladdin unifying risk analytics,
order management, portfolio management, performance attribution, compliance and
accounting; Charles River IMS integrated OMS/EMS, portfolio construction,
position keeping and performance attribution; SimCorp One/Dimension portfolio
accounting and real-time performance/attribution book-of-record; SS&C Advent
Geneva/APX portfolio accounting and Moxy OMS; Bloomberg AIM buy-side OMS with
order modelling, rebalancing and pre-trade compliance; Broadridge investment
book-of-record and portfolio/order management), reporting/aggregation platforms
(Addepar with the "Advent Converter" migration path), trust/portfolio accounting
(FIS Global Plus, SunGard Asset Arena), and digital-advisory platforms (Temenos
WealthSuite robo-advisor with client profiling, asset allocation, rebalancing
and goal-based investing; InvestCloud / NaviPlan goal-based and cashflow
planning; Envestnet unified managed accounts with rebalancing, cash management
and tax-loss harvesting). Robo-advisory / goal-based / risk-profiling component
patterns and the buy-side OMS lifecycle (order raise -> route -> execute (EMS)
-> allocate -> settle, with pre/post-trade compliance and SSIs) per industry
references; cloud-native event-driven service patterns per CNCF.

Naming verified globally unique against `glossary/_canonical-names.md`. Tech
names are deliberately kept distinct from the Wealth business capabilities and
the `Wealth Onboarding` process, e.g.:
- tech "Portfolio Management System" vs business cap "Portfolio Management"
- tech "Order Management System" vs business cap "Brokerage & Trading"
- tech "Investment Advisory Platform" vs business cap "Investment Advisory"
- tech "Trade Allocation Engine" vs payments tech "Settlement Instruction Engine"
- tech "Securities Settlement Processor" vs cards tech "Clearing & Settlement Processor" and business cap "Payment Clearing & Settlement"
- tech "Pre-Trade Compliance Engine" vs business caps "Regulatory Compliance" / "Transaction Monitoring"
Also checked against existing Core Processing tech sub-caps (e.g. "Transaction
Posting Engine", "Product & Pricing Engine", "Settlement Instruction Engine",
"Card Authorization Engine") — no clashes.

Relationship verbs used (of the allowed 7): `derived_from`, `defined_in`,
`depends_on`, `related_to`, `supersedes`.
