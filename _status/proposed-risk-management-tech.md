# Proposed Risk Management Technology Decomposition

Scope: the **Risk Management** business domain (Enterprise Risk Management and its
children: Credit Risk Management, Market Risk Management, Operational Risk
Management, Liquidity Risk Management, Fraud Management) has **no dedicated
top-level technology capability** in the registry. Only Fraud Analytics, Machine
Learning Platform, Analytics Platform and Threat Detection serve it indirectly.

This proposal therefore introduces **4 NEW top-level (L1) technology
capabilities** plus **L2/L3 decomposition** and a **systems refresh** of risk
platforms. Grounded in risk-technology reference architectures and vendor docs
(SAS Risk Engine / Risk Management for Banking, Oracle OFSAA, Moody's Analytics
RiskAuthority / RiskFrontier / RiskConfidence, IBM Algorithmics Algo Risk, Murex
MX.3 Enterprise Risk, FIS Enterprise Risk Suite / Adaptiv, MSCI RiskMetrics
RiskManager, Numerix Oneview, Quantifi, IBM OpenPages, ServiceNow IRM,
MetricStream, Archer, ValidMind, ModelOp, Quantexa), Basel/BCBS 239 risk data
aggregation principles, SR 11-7 / SR 26-2 model risk management guidance, and
CNCF cloud-native patterns.

Naming note: all canonical names were checked against
`glossary/_canonical-names.md` for global uniqueness and are deliberately kept
distinct from existing **business** capabilities/processes and from existing tech
caps (Fraud Analytics, Machine Learning Platform, Analytics Platform, Threat
Detection, Regulatory Reporting Engine). Examples of the tech-vs-business split:
tech **Risk Analytics Engine** vs business *Enterprise Risk Management* / process
*Risk Assessment*; tech **Stress Testing Engine** vs any business *Stress
Testing* process; tech **Model Risk Management Platform** vs any *Model Risk
Management* business capability; tech **Governance Risk & Compliance Platform** vs
business *Regulatory Compliance*. Relationships use only the 7 verbs:
`depends_on`, `derived_from`, `supersedes`, `defined_in`, `related_to`, `causes`,
`mentions`.
