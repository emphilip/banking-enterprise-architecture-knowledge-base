# Risk Identification & Assessment

**Definition.** Risk Identification & Assessment is the process of identifying risks across categories and assessing inherent and residual risk (likelihood times impact), capturing them in the Risk Register, evaluating residual risk against appetite, and assigning treatment and owners under COSO ERM.
**Also known as:** Risk Assessment, Risk ID & Assessment.

## Relationships
- Risk Identification & Assessment is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Identification & Assessment depends on the Operational Risk Management capability.

## Details
Risk Identification & Assessment draws on business activities, loss events, control inventories and the Risk Appetite Statement to produce a populated Risk Register, residual risk ratings and treatment plans. Controls include COSO ERM identification and assessment, inherent-versus-residual scoring, residual-versus-appetite evaluation, risk-owner assignment, and first and second line ownership under the three lines of defence.

## Flow
- Identify Risk Event causes Register Risk Item.
- Register Risk Item causes Score Inherent Risk.
- Score Inherent Risk causes Evaluate Residual Risk.
- Evaluate Residual Risk causes Assign Risk Treatment.
- Assign Risk Treatment causes Track Risk Remediation.
