# Funds Transfer Pricing

**Definition.** Funds Transfer Pricing is the ALM process that builds the matched-maturity transfer-pricing curve and liquidity-premium add-ons, applies transfer rates to assets and liabilities, attributes net interest margin to business units, and governs methodology and curves through ALCO.
**Also known as:** FTP, Liquidity Transfer Pricing, Internal Funds Pricing, Transfer Pricing (Treasury).

## Relationships
- Funds Transfer Pricing is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Funds Transfer Pricing depends on the Asset Liability Management capability.

## Details
Funds Transfer Pricing is run by the ALM Analyst. Inputs are market funding curves, behavioural assumptions, balance-sheet positions and ALCO policy; outputs are the FTP Curve, transfer-priced margins and NIM attribution. Controls include matched-maturity methodology governance, behavioural-assumption review, FTP curve governance and ALCO approval, premium-range limits and margin-attribution reconciliation.

## Flow
- Assemble Market Curves causes Build FTP Curve.
- Build FTP Curve causes Set Behavioural Assumptions.
- Set Behavioural Assumptions causes Approve FTP Methodology.
- Approve FTP Methodology causes Apply Transfer Rates.
- Apply Transfer Rates causes Attribute Net Margin.
- Attribute Net Margin causes Report FTP Outcome.
