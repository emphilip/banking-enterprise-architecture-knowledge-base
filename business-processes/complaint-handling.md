# Complaint Handling

**Definition.** Complaint Handling is the process of capturing, investigating, resolving, and reporting customer complaints in line with conduct and fair-treatment obligations.
**Also known as:** Complaints Process.

## Relationships
- Complaint Handling is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Complaint Handling depends on the Customer Servicing capability.
- Complaint Handling depends on the Case Management capability.

## Details
Complaint Handling logs the complaint, classifies and prioritizes it, investigates root cause, communicates resolution and any redress, and feeds learnings into conduct reporting. Actors include contact center agents, complaints specialists, and conduct teams. Systems involved include a case management platform, contact center tooling, and notification services.

## Flow
- Log Complaint causes Acknowledge Complaint.
- Acknowledge Complaint causes Triage Complaint.
- Triage Complaint causes Investigate Complaint.
- Investigate Complaint causes Determine Outcome.
- Determine Outcome causes Issue Final Response.
- Issue Final Response causes Conduct Root Cause.
- Conduct Root Cause causes Close Complaint.
