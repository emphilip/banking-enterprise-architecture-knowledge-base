# Regulatory Change Management

**Definition.** Regulatory Change Management is the end-to-end process for managing regulatory change: horizon scanning of emerging rules, mapping new requirements to internal Regulatory Obligations, impact assessment, remediation and control implementation, and attestation that the change has been embedded, with full traceability.
**Also known as:** Reg Change Management, RCM, Regulatory Change Tracking.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Change Management is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Regulatory Change Management depends on the Regulatory Compliance capability.

## Details
Regulatory Change Management scans regulatory feeds and publications for emerging or finalised change, maps each change to affected internal Regulatory Obligations, policies, controls and owners, assesses multi-dimension business and operational impact, plans and implements remediation, and attests that the change is embedded and operating. The lead actor is the Regulatory Change Manager. Outputs include the updated Regulatory Obligation, the remediation plan and the attestation record.
