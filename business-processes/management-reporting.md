# Management Reporting

**Definition.** Management Reporting is the planning-and-analysis process that builds the budget and forecast, measures actuals against plan, analyses variances, captures management commentary and produces the management accounts and board MI pack for decision-making.
**Also known as:** FP&A Process, Performance Management Reporting, Management Accounting, Board Reporting.

## Relationships
- Management Reporting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Management Reporting depends on the Financial Reporting capability.

## Details
Management Reporting is led by the FP&A Manager. Inputs are the approved Trial Balance, the budget/plan, actuals and business commentary; outputs are the Management Accounts, the board MI pack and variance analysis. Controls include budget approval, actual-vs-budget reconciliation, variance commentary governance, segment KPI integrity and CFO/board MI sign-off.

## Flow
- Build Operating Budget causes Approve Budget Plan.
- Approve Budget Plan causes Roll Forward Forecast.
- Roll Forward Forecast causes Capture Actual Results.
- Capture Actual Results causes Analyse Budget Variance.
- Analyse Budget Variance causes Compile Management Accounts.
- Compile Management Accounts causes Present Board Pack.
