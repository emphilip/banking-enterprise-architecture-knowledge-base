# Ledger Close & Reconciliation

**Definition.** Ledger Close & Reconciliation closes subledgers to the general ledger, posts period-end adjusting journals and reconciles accounts to produce an adjusted trial balance.
**Also known as:** Subledger Close, GL Close, Close & Recon.

## Relationships
- Ledger Close & Reconciliation is derived from Financial Close.
- Ledger Close & Reconciliation is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Ledger Close & Reconciliation depends on the General Ledger Accounting capability.

## Details
The sub-process is performed by the General Ledger Accountant. Inputs are subledger balances, accruals and reconciliations; outputs are an adjusted Trial Balance. Controls include period-end cut-off, journal approval and segregation of duties, and account reconciliation.
