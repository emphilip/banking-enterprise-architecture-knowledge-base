# Suspicious Activity Reporting

**Definition.** Suspicious Activity Reporting is the process of detecting, reviewing, and filing reports of suspicious financial activity to regulators in line with anti-money-laundering obligations.
**Also known as:** SAR Filing.

## Relationships
- Suspicious Activity Reporting is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Suspicious Activity Reporting depends on the Transaction Monitoring capability.
- Suspicious Activity Reporting depends on the Transaction Monitoring Platform capability.

## Details
Suspicious Activity Reporting reviews alerts from transaction monitoring, investigates and documents the rationale, and files reports such as SARs or STRs with the relevant financial intelligence unit. Actors include AML analysts and the money laundering reporting officer. Systems involved include a transaction monitoring platform, case management, and regulatory filing channels.
