# Cash & Liquidity Management

**Definition.** Cash & Liquidity Management is the treasury operations process that aggregates nostro and central-bank balances, projects the intraday and short-term liquidity position, monitors LCR/NSFR buffers, decides and executes funding actions, and reconciles end-of-day cash.
**Also known as:** Liquidity Operations, Treasury Cash Operations.

## Relationships
- Cash & Liquidity Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Cash & Liquidity Management depends on the Liquidity Management capability.

## Details
Cash & Liquidity Management is run by the Treasury Analyst. Inputs are nostro and central-bank balances, payment flows and regulatory liquidity limits; outputs are the liquidity position, funding decisions and the intraday cash report. Controls include nostro reconciliation, intraday liquidity monitoring, LCR/NSFR limit governance, funding-authority limits, treasurer escalation and end-of-day cash sign-off.

## Flow
- Aggregate Cash Balances causes Project Liquidity Position.
- Project Liquidity Position causes Assess Liquidity Buffers.
- Assess Liquidity Buffers causes Decide Funding Action.
- Decide Funding Action causes Execute Funding Action.
- Execute Funding Action causes Reconcile End-Of-Day Cash.
- Reconcile End-Of-Day Cash causes Report Liquidity Position.
