# Financial Planning

**Definition.** Financial Planning is the end-to-end advisory process that profiles the client, assesses suitability and best interest, formulates an investment plan and asset allocation, and produces and agrees the Investment Policy Statement and suitability report.
**Also known as:** Wealth Planning, Advice Process.

## Relationships
- Financial Planning is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Financial Planning depends on the Investment Advisory capability.

## Details
Financial Planning gathers client goals and financials, determines risk tolerance, formulates a target allocation, runs suitability and best-interest checks, and codifies the agreed plan in an Investment Policy Statement. Controls include MiFID II / ESMA suitability, Reg BI care and disclosure obligations, KYC for investors, and conflict-of-interest checks. Actors include the Financial Advisor.

## Flow
- Capture Client Goals causes Determine Risk Tolerance.
- Determine Risk Tolerance causes Formulate Investment Plan.
- Formulate Investment Plan causes Confirm Suitability.
- Confirm Suitability causes Draft Investment Policy Statement.
- Draft Investment Policy Statement causes Agree Investment Plan.
