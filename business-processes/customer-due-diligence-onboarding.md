# Customer Due Diligence Onboarding

**Definition.** Customer Due Diligence Onboarding is the risk-based due-diligence process performed at onboarding: Customer Identification Program (CIP) data capture, beneficial-ownership identification, sanctions/PEP/adverse-media screening, customer risk rating, and the accept/EDD/reject decision under FATF Recommendations 10 and 12.
**Also known as:** CDD Onboarding, Diligence Onboarding, AML Onboarding.

## Relationships
- Customer Due Diligence Onboarding is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Customer Due Diligence Onboarding depends on the Customer Due Diligence capability.
- Customer Due Diligence Onboarding depends on the Sanctions Screening capability.

## Details
Customer Due Diligence Onboarding captures the CIP minimum data set, identifies ultimate beneficial owners at the 25% threshold and control persons, screens parties against sanctions, PEP and adverse-media lists, assigns a customer risk score and triggers Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk customers. The accept/reject/condition decision is made under four-eyes. The lead actor is the AML Compliance Officer. Outputs include the verified KYC Profile, the Customer Risk Score and the CDD decision.
