# Stress Testing

**Definition.** Stress Testing is the forward-looking process of assessing capital and loss resilience under adverse macro scenarios: scenario design, model execution under SR 11-7 governance, capital and loss projection, and board sign-off and regulatory submission under CCAR/DFAST and EBA.
**Also known as:** Capital Stress Testing, CCAR Process, Scenario Analysis.

## Relationships
- Stress Testing is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Stress Testing depends on the Market Risk Management capability.

## Details
Stress Testing takes supervisory and internal scenarios, validated models and portfolio data and produces capital projections, the Stress Test Result and a capital plan submission. Controls include scenario design (baseline, adverse and severely adverse), SR 11-7 and OCC 2011-12 model validation, board sign-off, and CCAR/DFAST regulatory submission.

## Flow
- Design Stress Scenario causes Validate Risk Models.
- Validate Risk Models causes Project Capital Impact.
- Project Capital Impact causes Assess Capital Adequacy.
- Assess Capital Adequacy causes Obtain Board Approval.
- Obtain Board Approval causes Submit Capital Plan.
