# Risk Appetite Setting

**Definition.** Risk Appetite Setting is the process of articulating the aggregate level and types of risk the bank is willing to accept to meet its objectives: drafting the Risk Appetite Statement, cascading qualitative statements into quantitative limits and tolerances, and obtaining board and CRO approval under the Risk Appetite Framework.
**Also known as:** RAF Process, Appetite Setting.

## Relationships
- Risk Appetite Setting is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Appetite Setting depends on the Enterprise Risk Management capability.

## Details
Risk Appetite Setting begins from the strategy and business plan, prior Risk Appetite Statement and an assessment of risk capacity. It produces an approved Risk Appetite Statement and a cascade of risk limits, following FSB Principles for an Effective Risk Appetite Framework. Controls include board approval of the RAS, CRO/CFO collaboration, the cascade into quantitative limits, and three-lines-of-defence ownership.

## Flow
- Define Risk Capacity causes Draft Appetite Statement.
- Draft Appetite Statement causes Cascade Risk Limits.
- Cascade Risk Limits causes Approve Risk Appetite.
- Approve Risk Appetite causes Embed Appetite Limits.
