# Conduct Risk Management

**Definition.** Conduct Risk Management oversees market-conduct, consumer-protection and culture obligations to ensure fair customer outcomes and regulatory conduct compliance, aligning with the BIAN Regulatory Compliance service domain.
**Also known as:** Conduct Compliance, Market Conduct Management.

## Relationships
- Conduct Risk Management is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Conduct Risk Management is derived from Regulatory Compliance.
- Conduct Risk Management depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.
- Conduct Risk Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
The FCA Consumer Duty (Principle 12 and PRIN 2A, in force from July 2023) requires firms to deliver good outcomes for retail customers across products and services, price and value, consumer understanding and consumer support, and to evidence those outcomes with data. Conduct Risk Management governs treating-customers-fairly controls, conflicts of interest, market-abuse conduct and sales-practice monitoring, producing the board-level outcomes monitoring the FCA expects.
