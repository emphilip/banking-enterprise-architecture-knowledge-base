# Counterparty Credit Risk

**Definition.** Counterparty Credit Risk measures exposure and capital for derivative and securities-financing counterparties, including CVA and SA-CCR add-ons, grounded in the Basel SA-CCR and credit valuation adjustment frameworks.
**Also known as:** CCR Management, CVA Risk, SA-CCR Exposure.

## Relationships
- Counterparty Credit Risk is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Counterparty Credit Risk is derived from Market Risk Management.
- Counterparty Credit Risk depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Counterparty Credit Risk depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Counterparty Credit Risk computes exposure-at-default for OTC derivatives, exchange-traded derivatives and securities-financing transactions using SA-CCR (CRE52): EAD = 1.4 × (RC + PFE) with asset-class-specific add-ons and recognition of netting and margin. It quantifies wrong-way risk, manages CVA capital under the BCBS d325 framework to cover mark-to-market losses from counterparty credit-quality deterioration, and supports central-counterparty exposure treatment for cleared trades.
