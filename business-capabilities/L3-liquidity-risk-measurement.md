# Liquidity Risk Measurement

**Definition.** Liquidity Risk Measurement quantifies funding gaps, liquidity buffers and survival horizons under business-as-usual and stress, realised through the BIAN Liquidity Risk Models service domain under the Basel III liquidity framework.
**Also known as:** Liquidity Gap Analysis, Survival Horizon Measurement.

## Relationships
- Liquidity Risk Measurement is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Liquidity Risk Measurement is derived from Liquidity Risk Management.
- Liquidity Risk Measurement depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Liquidity Risk Measurement depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Liquidity Risk Measurement builds contractual and behavioural maturity ladders, computes net cumulative cash-flow gaps across time buckets, and measures the survival horizon — the number of days the counterbalancing capacity covers stressed net outflows. It applies the BCBS d188 "Principles for Sound Liquidity Risk Management and Supervision" expectations on cash-flow projection and buffer adequacy, and produces the inputs to LCR/NSFR steering and intraday monitoring under multiple idiosyncratic and market-wide stress assumptions.
