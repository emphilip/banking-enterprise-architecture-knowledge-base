# AML Monitoring

**Definition.** AML Monitoring is the business capability that detects potential money laundering by analyzing customer behavior and transactions against typologies and risk indicators. It aligns with the BIAN Financial Crime service domain and FATF AML standards.
**Also known as:** Anti-Money Laundering.

## Relationships
- AML Monitoring is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- AML Monitoring is derived from Financial Crime Compliance.
- AML Monitoring depends on the Transaction Monitoring Platform capability.

## Details
AML Monitoring combines rules-based scenarios, segmentation, and machine-learning anomaly detection to surface alerts for investigation. It decomposes into transaction monitoring and sanctions screening, with modern platforms such as Hawk AI and ComplyAdvantage superseding legacy engines to lower false-positive rates and support explainable alert disposition.
