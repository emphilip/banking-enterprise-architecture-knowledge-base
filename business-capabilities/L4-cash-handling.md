# Cash Handling

**Definition.** Cash Handling is the Channels & Engagement capability that performs counter deposit, withdrawal and cash-recycler operations and till balancing, supporting the BIAN Point of Service and Branch Currency Management service domains.
**Also known as:** Till Operations, Counter Cash Handling.

## Relationships
- Cash Handling is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Cash Handling is derived from Teller Operations.
- Cash Handling depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
Cash Handling supports BIAN Point of Service and Branch Currency Management at the till: accepting and dispensing notes and coin, operating cash recyclers, and balancing the drawer against the core ledger at shift end. Cash transactions over the USD 10,000 threshold require a Currency Transaction Report and ID capture under the Bank Secrecy Act, and structuring patterns are flagged for monitoring. Counterfeit detection, dual control for over-limit withdrawals, and sealed-bag buy/sell with the vault are the core controls. Cash recyclers shrink manual counting and feed real-time till positions back for reconciliation.
