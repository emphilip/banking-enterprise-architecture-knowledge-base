# Capital Stress Forecasting

**Definition.** Capital Stress Forecasting projects capital ratios under baseline and stress macro scenarios to inform the capital plan, complementing Risk-domain stress testing.
**Also known as:** Capital Projection, Stressed Capital Forecast, Capital Scenario Forecasting.

## Relationships
- Capital Stress Forecasting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Capital Stress Forecasting is derived from Capital Planning.
- Capital Stress Forecasting depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Capital Stress Forecasting projects CET1 and total-capital ratios over a multi-year horizon under severely adverse scenarios, modelling stressed losses, pre-provision net revenue, RWA migration and dividend constraints to confirm the bank stays above its minimum-plus-buffer requirement. It produces the finance-owned capital projections underpinning supervisory exercises such as the Federal Reserve's CCAR/DFAST, demonstrating that planned distributions are sustainable even after a stress draws down capital.
