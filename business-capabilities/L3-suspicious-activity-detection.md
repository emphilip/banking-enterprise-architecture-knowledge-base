# Suspicious Activity Detection

**Definition.** Suspicious Activity Detection identifies unusual or potentially illicit behaviour through scenarios, rules and analytics for triage into alerts, per FATF Recommendation 20 and the US Bank Secrecy Act suspicious-activity framework.
**Also known as:** Suspicious Behaviour Detection, AML Detection.

## Relationships
- Suspicious Activity Detection is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Suspicious Activity Detection is derived from AML Monitoring.
- Suspicious Activity Detection depends on the Transaction Monitoring Platform capability.
- Suspicious Activity Detection depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.

## Details
FATF Recommendation 20 requires that, where an institution suspects funds are proceeds of crime or related to terrorist financing, it must promptly report to the financial intelligence unit. Under the BSA, banks must monitor for and detect suspicious activity such as structuring just below the $10,000 CTR threshold, rapid movement of funds, and transactions inconsistent with the customer's known profile. Detection scenarios encode typologies (e.g. layering, smurfing) and behavioural thresholds to generate candidate alerts.
