# Third-Party Integrity Due Diligence

**Definition.** Third-Party Integrity Due Diligence screens agents, intermediaries and suppliers for corruption risk, the principal FCPA exposure for multinationals, supporting the UK Bribery Act 2010 controls.
**Also known as:** Intermediary Due Diligence, Vendor Integrity Screening.

- Third-Party Integrity Due Diligence depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
A large majority of FCPA enforcement actions involve bribes paid through third-party intermediaries, so the DOJ/SEC FCPA Resource Guide expects risk-based due diligence on agents and business partners, including ownership, qualifications, references and red flags such as unusual commissions or payments to offshore accounts. The UK Bribery Act's "due diligence" principle requires proportionate checks on persons who perform services for or on behalf of the organisation. Findings gate onboarding, contractual anti-bribery clauses and audit rights.
