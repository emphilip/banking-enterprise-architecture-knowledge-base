# Profitability Analytics

**Definition.** Profitability Analytics measures product, customer and business-unit profitability and cost allocation, supporting the BIAN Management Accounting service domain.
**Also known as:** Cost & Profitability Analysis, Product Profitability, Customer Profitability.

## Relationships
- Profitability Analytics is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Profitability Analytics is derived from Internal Management Reporting.
- Profitability Analytics depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Profitability Analytics computes multi-dimensional risk-adjusted profitability — by product, customer, segment and business unit — by combining net interest income (after FTP funding charges/credits), fee income, direct and allocated costs (activity-based costing), and expected credit-loss/capital charges. Aligned to APQC management-accounting measures, it isolates where the bank truly makes money once funding cost and risk are charged back, informing pricing, portfolio and cost-to-serve decisions rather than gross relationship revenue.
