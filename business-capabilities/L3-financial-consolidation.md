# Financial Consolidation

**Definition.** Financial Consolidation aggregates subsidiary results and eliminates intra-group balances to produce group accounts under IFRS 10 Consolidated Financial Statements, supporting APQC PCF "Perform consolidations and process eliminations".
**Also known as:** Group Consolidation, Consolidation & Eliminations, IFRS 10 Consolidation.

## Relationships
- Financial Consolidation is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Financial Consolidation is derived from Financial Reporting.
- Financial Consolidation depends on the Financial Consolidation Platform capability.
- Financial Consolidation depends on the Data Warehousing capability.

## Details
Financial Consolidation applies the IFRS 10 control model: entities the parent controls are consolidated line-by-line, intra-group items eliminated in full, and non-controlling interests presented within equity. The capability collects entity trial balances on a common group chart of accounts, translates foreign-currency books (IAS 21), runs elimination and adjustment journals, and produces the consolidated balance sheet, P&L and equity. Group structure and ownership percentages drive the consolidation method (full consolidation, equity method for associates).
