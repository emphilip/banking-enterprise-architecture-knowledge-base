# SAR Filing & Tracking

**Definition.** SAR Filing & Tracking submits Suspicious Activity Reports to the financial intelligence unit within mandated timeframes and tracks acknowledgements and continuing-activity reviews, per FinCEN rules.
**Also known as:** STR Filing, SAR Submission.

## Relationships
- SAR Filing & Tracking is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- SAR Filing & Tracking is derived from SAR Management.
- SAR Filing & Tracking depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.
- SAR Filing & Tracking depends on the Document Management capability.

## Details
FinCEN requires SARs (FinCEN Form 111) to be filed electronically through the BSA E-Filing System within 30 calendar days of initial detection, or 60 days where no suspect is identified. Where suspicious activity continues, a follow-up SAR is filed at least every 90 days. This capability formats and validates the filing, transmits it, captures the BSA Identifier acknowledgement, and tracks deadlines and continuing-activity timers. Late or missing filings are a frequent enforcement basis, so the tracking ledger evidences timeliness to examiners.
