# Channel Experience Management

**Definition.** Channel Experience Management is the Channels & Engagement capability that governs UX content, journeys and A/B optimisation across digital channels, an omnichannel CX practice supporting the BIAN Channel Activity History analytics service domain.
**Also known as:** CX Management, Journey Optimisation.

## Relationships
- Channel Experience Management is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Channel Experience Management is derived from Digital Engagement & Personalisation.
- Channel Experience Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Channel Experience Management governs the design and continuous optimisation of digital journeys: content management, A/B and multivariate testing, funnel analytics and behavioural session replay, all reading BIAN Channel Activity History for the unified interaction record. It measures task completion, drop-off and effort across web, mobile and assisted channels to drive omnichannel consistency. Conduct regimes (FCA Consumer Duty) require evidence that journeys avoid sludge and foreshorten harm, and any experimentation on consumers must respect consent and avoid discriminatory treatment. Tooling includes Adobe Analytics, Glassbox and Optimizely-class platforms.
