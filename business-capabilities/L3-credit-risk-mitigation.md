# Credit Risk Mitigation

**Definition.** Credit Risk Mitigation recognises and manages collateral, guarantees, netting and hedges that reduce credit exposure and capital, aligned with the Basel credit risk mitigation framework and the BIAN Collateral Asset Administration service domain.
**Also known as:** CRM Techniques, Collateral Risk Mitigation, Netting Recognition.

## Relationships
- Credit Risk Mitigation is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Credit Risk Mitigation is derived from Credit Risk Management.
- Credit Risk Mitigation depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Credit Risk Mitigation depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Credit Risk Mitigation applies the Basel CRE22 techniques: funded protection (eligible financial collateral under the comprehensive approach with supervisory or own-estimate haircuts) and unfunded protection (guarantees and credit derivatives via substitution of the protection provider's risk weight). It enforces legal certainty, maturity-mismatch and currency-mismatch adjustments, and recognises netting agreements. Outputs reduce EAD or LGD inputs to capital, with collateral eligibility and revaluation managed through the collateral administration domain.
