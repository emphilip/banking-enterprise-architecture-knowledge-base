# Statutory Reporting

**Definition.** Statutory Reporting produces the primary financial statements for legal and external filing under IAS 1 Presentation of Financial Statements (transitioning to IFRS 18 from 2027), aligning to the BIAN Financial Control service domain.
**Also known as:** External Financial Reporting, Statutory Accounts, IAS 1 Reporting.

## Relationships
- Statutory Reporting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Statutory Reporting is derived from Financial Reporting.
- Statutory Reporting depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.

## Details
Statutory Reporting prepares the IAS 1 complete set of financial statements — statement of financial position, profit or loss and other comprehensive income, changes in equity, cash flows and notes — applying the going-concern, accrual and comparative-information requirements. It drives jurisdictional statutory accounts for each legal entity and the consolidated group, reconciled to the ledger and consolidation outputs. IFRS 18 (effective 1 January 2027) restructures the income statement into defined categories and adds management-defined performance measures, which this capability will adopt.
