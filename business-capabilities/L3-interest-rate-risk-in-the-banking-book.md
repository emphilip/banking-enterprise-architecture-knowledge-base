# Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book

**Definition.** Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book measures and manages the sensitivity of net interest income and economic value of equity to interest-rate movements under the Basel BCBS 368 IRRBB standard.
**Also known as:** IRRBB Management, Banking Book Rate Risk, EVE & NII Management.

## Relationships
- Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book is derived from Asset Liability Management.
- Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book depends on the Asset Liability Management Engine capability.

## Details
Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book applies BCBS d368, which requires banks to measure both the economic-value-of-equity (EVE) and net-interest-income (NII) impact of rate shocks, including the six prescribed standardised interest-rate shock scenarios, and to apply the supervisory outlier test (e.g. EVE decline exceeding 15% of Tier 1 capital). It captures repricing gaps, basis and optionality risk across the banking book and feeds the bank's ALCO limits and Pillar 2 IRRBB disclosures.
