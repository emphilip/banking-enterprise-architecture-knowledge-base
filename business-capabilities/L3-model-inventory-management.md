# Model Inventory Management

**Definition.** Model Inventory Management maintains a complete register of models with tiering, ownership, validation status and usage to support effective challenge, expected under US Federal Reserve guidance SR 11-7.
**Also known as:** Model Register, Model Tiering, Model Catalogue.

## Relationships
- Model Inventory Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Model Inventory Management is derived from Model Risk Management.
- Model Inventory Management depends on the Model Risk Management Platform capability.
- Model Inventory Management depends on the Data Warehousing capability.

## Details
Model Inventory Management satisfies the SR 11-7 expectation that firms maintain a comprehensive inventory of models in use, under development, or recently retired, with key attributes: model owner, developer, validator, materiality tier, version, input data, downstream consumers, last validation date and finding status. It captures model interdependencies (a credit PD model feeding ECL and capital), flags models past their revalidation due date, and provides the population over which model risk aggregate reporting is produced.
