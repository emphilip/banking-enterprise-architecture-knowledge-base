# Collateral Perfection

**Definition.** Collateral Perfection is the capability that registers, secures and maintains the legal enforceability of charges over pledged assets, mapping to the BIAN Collateral Asset Administration service domain.
**Also known as:** Lien Perfection, Charge Registration.

## Relationships
- Collateral Perfection is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Collateral Perfection is derived from Collateral Management.
- Collateral Perfection depends on the Document Management capability.
- Collateral Perfection depends on the Workflow Orchestration capability.

## Details
The BIAN Collateral Asset Administration service domain records the security interest the bank holds, which Collateral Perfection makes legally enforceable against third parties. A concrete banking specific: in the US a security interest in personal property is perfected by filing a UCC-1 financing statement under Article 9 of the Uniform Commercial Code, establishing the bank's priority over later creditors.
