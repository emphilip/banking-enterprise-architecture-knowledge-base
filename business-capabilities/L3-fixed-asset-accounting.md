# Fixed Asset Accounting

**Definition.** Fixed Asset Accounting maintains the asset register, capitalisation, depreciation and disposal of property, plant and equipment in line with IAS 16, supporting APQC PCF "Manage fixed-asset project accounting".
**Also known as:** Asset Register Management, Depreciation Accounting, Capital Asset Accounting.

## Relationships
- Fixed Asset Accounting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Fixed Asset Accounting is derived from General Ledger Accounting.
- Fixed Asset Accounting depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
Fixed Asset Accounting applies IAS 16 Property, Plant and Equipment: assets are capitalised at cost, depreciated systematically over useful life, tested for impairment and derecognised on disposal with gain/loss to P&L. For a bank this covers premises, ATMs, hardware and capitalised software, plus right-of-use assets under IFRS 16 leases. The capability maintains parallel depreciation books (IFRS, local GAAP and tax) over a single asset register so that period depreciation posts consistently to the ledger.
