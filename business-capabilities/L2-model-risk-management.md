# Model Risk Management

**Definition.** Model Risk Management governs the model lifecycle — development, validation, independent challenge, inventory and ongoing monitoring — to control errors in quantitative models, framed by US Federal Reserve guidance SR 11-7.
**Also known as:** Model Governance, Model Validation, MRM.

## Relationships
- Model Risk Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Model Risk Management is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Model Risk Management depends on the Model Risk Management Platform capability.
- Model Risk Management depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.

## Details
Model Risk Management implements the SR 11-7 (and OCC 2011-12) supervisory guidance on model risk: the "three core elements" of robust model development and implementation, effective validation, and sound governance/policies/controls. Validation covers conceptual soundness review, ongoing monitoring, and outcomes analysis (backtesting and benchmarking). It enforces effective challenge by parties independent of model development, tiers models by materiality, and tracks remediation of validation findings across the firm-wide model inventory.
