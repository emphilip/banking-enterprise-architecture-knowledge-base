# Operational Risk Management

**Definition.** Operational Risk Management is the business capability that identifies and mitigates losses arising from failed internal processes, people, systems, and external events, including cyber and third-party risk. It corresponds to the BIAN Operational Risk service domain.
**Also known as:** Operational Risk.

## Relationships
- Operational Risk Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Operational Risk Management is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Operational Risk Management depends on the Threat Detection capability.

## Details
Operational Risk Management runs risk-and-control self-assessments, loss event capture, key risk indicators, and scenario analysis aligned to the Basel standardized approach. It integrates with threat detection, business continuity, and third-party risk programs, and increasingly uses RPA and analytics to automate control testing and operational resilience monitoring.
