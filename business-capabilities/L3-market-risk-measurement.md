# Market Risk Measurement

**Definition.** Market Risk Measurement quantifies exposure to moves in interest-rate, FX, equity, credit-spread and commodity factors using sensitivities and risk metrics, realised through the BIAN Market Risk Models service domain under the Basel FRTB standard.
**Also known as:** Market Risk Metrics, Sensitivity Measurement.

## Relationships
- Market Risk Measurement is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Market Risk Measurement is derived from Market Risk Management.
- Market Risk Measurement depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Market Risk Measurement depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Market Risk Measurement produces the risk-factor sensitivities and capital metrics required by the Basel FRTB (d457) Standardised Approach: the Sensitivities-Based Method (delta, vega, curvature aggregated under prescribed correlations), the Default Risk Charge, and the Residual Risk Add-On. It maps positions to the seven risk classes (GIRR, CSR non-securitisation, CSR securitisation, CSR correlation-trading, equity, commodity, FX) and feeds both standardised and internal-model capital streams.
