# Typology Modelling

**Definition.** Typology Modelling encodes money-laundering and terrorist-financing typologies into detection logic, drawing on FATF typology and methods-and-trends reports.
**Also known as:** ML Typology Modelling, Risk Typology Library.

## Relationships
- Typology Modelling is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Typology Modelling is derived from Suspicious Activity Detection.
- Typology Modelling depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- Typology Modelling depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.

## Details
FATF publishes methods-and-trends and typology reports describing how criminals launder funds — for example trade-based money laundering, structuring/smurfing, funnel accounts, money mules and the misuse of legal persons. Typology Modelling translates these patterns and FinCEN advisories into concrete detection scenarios and red-flag indicators, maintaining a typology library that maps each typology to monitoring rules. As new predicate-offence trends emerge (e.g. ransomware, sanctions evasion), the library and downstream scenarios are updated to keep detection coverage current.
