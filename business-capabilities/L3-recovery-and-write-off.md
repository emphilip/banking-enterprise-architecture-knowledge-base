# Recovery & Write-Off

**Definition.** Recovery & Write-Off is the capability that manages late-stage recovery, repossession, charge-off and debt sale of non-performing accounts, mapping to the BIAN Collection/Recovery service domain.
**Also known as:** Debt Recovery, Charge-Off Management.

## Relationships
- Recovery & Write-Off is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Recovery & Write-Off is derived from Collections & Recovery.
- Recovery & Write-Off depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.
- Recovery & Write-Off depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
The BIAN Collection/Recovery service domain pursues recovery on defaulted exposures, which Recovery & Write-Off operates. A concrete banking specific: US banking regulators' Uniform Retail Credit Classification policy generally requires open-end (revolving) credit to be charged off at 180 days past due and closed-end instalment loans at 120 days, after which the bank pursues recovery, repossession or debt sale.
