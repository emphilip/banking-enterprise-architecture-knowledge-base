# Adverse Media Screening

**Definition.** Adverse Media Screening searches negative news and open-source intelligence for financial-crime risk indicators on customers and counterparties, supporting the FATF Recommendation 10 risk-based diligence and ongoing-monitoring obligations.
**Also known as:** Negative News Screening, Media Monitoring.

## Relationships
- Adverse Media Screening is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Adverse Media Screening is derived from AML Monitoring.
- Adverse Media Screening depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- Adverse Media Screening depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.

## Details
FATF R.10 requires understanding the nature of the customer relationship and conducting ongoing due diligence; adverse media is a key risk signal feeding customer risk rating and EDD triggers. Screening matches customers and beneficial owners against news, enforcement, litigation and sanctions-related reporting categorised by predicate offence (fraud, corruption, trafficking, terrorism). Natural-language processing reduces false positives from name ambiguity and stale articles, and confirmed adverse media typically escalates a customer to enhanced due diligence.
