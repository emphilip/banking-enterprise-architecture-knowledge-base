# Market Risk Management

**Definition.** Market Risk Management is the business capability that measures and controls exposure to movements in interest rates, FX, equity, and commodity prices across trading and banking books. It aligns with the BIAN Market Risk service domain.
**Also known as:** Market Risk.

## Relationships
- Market Risk Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Market Risk Management is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Market Risk Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Market Risk Management computes value-at-risk, expected shortfall, sensitivities (the Greeks), and stress and scenario losses, supporting the Fundamental Review of the Trading Book capital requirements. It depends on high-volume market data, pricing libraries, and analytics platforms for intraday and end-of-day risk aggregation and limit monitoring.
