# Treasury Management

**Definition.** Treasury Management is the top-level business capability that manages the bank's funding, liquidity, balance sheet, and market positions. It aligns with the BIAN Treasury and Asset & Liability Management service domains.
**Also known as:** Treasury.

## Relationships
- Treasury Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Treasury Management depends on the Data Warehousing capability.
- Treasury Management is related to Liquidity Management.

## Details
Treasury Management spans funding and money-market operations, liquidity management, asset-liability management, FX and interest-rate hedging, and intraday liquidity. It depends on aggregated position and cash-flow data, coordinating closely with liquidity risk and finance to optimize net interest margin and meet regulatory buffers.
