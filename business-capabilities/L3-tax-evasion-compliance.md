# Tax Evasion Compliance

**Definition.** Tax Evasion Compliance handles tax-transparency obligations (FATCA and the OECD Common Reporting Standard, customer tax classification) and facilitation-of-tax-evasion controls, mapping to the BIAN Customer Tax Handling service domain.
**Also known as:** Customer Tax Compliance, FATCA CRS Compliance.

## Relationships
- Tax Evasion Compliance is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Tax Evasion Compliance is derived from Financial Crime Risk Compliance.
- Tax Evasion Compliance depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.
- Tax Evasion Compliance depends on the Data Governance capability.

## Details
FATCA requires foreign financial institutions to identify US account holders and report them to the IRS (or local tax authority under an IGA), withholding 30% on certain US-source payments to non-compliant accounts. The OECD CRS extends automatic exchange of financial account information across 100+ participating jurisdictions. This capability governs self-certification collection, tax-residence and US-indicia determination, reportable-account identification, and annual reporting. It also covers UK CCO failure-to-prevent-facilitation-of-tax-evasion controls under the Criminal Finances Act 2017.
