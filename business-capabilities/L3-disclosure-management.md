# Disclosure Management

**Definition.** Disclosure Management assembles, controls and tags the notes and narrative disclosures of the financial statements, including XBRL/ESEF tagging required under IAS 1 and the IFRS instrument-disclosure standards.
**Also known as:** Notes & Disclosures, Disclosure Reporting, XBRL Disclosure.

## Relationships
- Disclosure Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Disclosure Management is derived from Financial Reporting.
- Disclosure Management depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.

## Details
Disclosure Management produces the note disclosures that IAS 1 mandates around the primary statements — accounting policies, judgements and estimates — plus the financial-instrument disclosures of IFRS 7 and the ECL/credit-risk disclosures arising from IFRS 9. It links each disclosed figure back to ledger and consolidation sources, manages narrative authoring and review workflow, and applies the ESEF/XBRL taxonomy so that filed statements are machine-readable and consistent across periods.
