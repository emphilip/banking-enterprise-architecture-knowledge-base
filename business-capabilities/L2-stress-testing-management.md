# Stress Testing Management

**Definition.** Stress Testing Management designs and runs firm-wide and risk-type scenarios (baseline, adverse, reverse) to assess resilience under shocks, governed by the BCBS stress-testing principles and supervisory exercises such as Fed CCAR/DFAST and the EBA EU-wide stress test.
**Also known as:** Scenario Stress Testing, Reverse Stress Testing, CCAR Stress Testing.

## Relationships
- Stress Testing Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Stress Testing Management is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Stress Testing Management depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Stress Testing Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Stress Testing Management executes the BCBS "Stress testing principles" (d450) lifecycle: governance, scenario design, model execution, and use in capital and liquidity planning. Under the Federal Reserve CCAR/DFAST programme it projects nine-quarter pre-provision net revenue, losses and post-stress CET1 ratios under supervisory baseline, adverse and severely adverse macro paths. Reverse stress testing works backward from a defined point of non-viability to the scenarios that would cause it, feeding recovery and resolution planning.
