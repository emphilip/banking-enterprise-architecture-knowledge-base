# Liquidity Management

**Definition.** Liquidity Management is the business capability that forecasts, monitors, and optimizes the bank's cash and funding positions to meet obligations efficiently. It corresponds to the BIAN Treasury and Liquidity service domains.
**Also known as:** Cash & Liquidity.

## Relationships
- Liquidity Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Liquidity Management is derived from Treasury Management.
- Liquidity Management depends on the Data Warehousing capability.

## Details
Liquidity Management covers cash-flow forecasting, nostro and intraday liquidity management, collateral and high-quality liquid asset optimization, and funding plans. It draws on aggregated transaction and position data, coordinating with payments and liquidity risk to maintain buffers and meet LCR and intraday settlement requirements.
