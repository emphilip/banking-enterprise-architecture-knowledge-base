# Collateral Valuation

**Definition.** Collateral Valuation is the capability that establishes and revalues the worth of pledged assets, mapping to the BIAN Collateral Asset Administration service domain.
**Also known as:** Asset Valuation (Lending), Security Valuation.

## Relationships
- Collateral Valuation is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Collateral Valuation is derived from Collateral Management.
- Collateral Valuation depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- Collateral Valuation depends on the Document Management capability.

## Details
The BIAN Collateral Asset Administration service domain tracks pledged assets and their values, which Collateral Valuation supplies and refreshes. A concrete banking specific: a residential mortgage requires an independent property appraisal at origination, and US appraisals must comply with USPAP and appraiser-independence requirements under the Dodd-Frank Act / Regulation Z so the loan-to-value ratio rests on a defensible value.
