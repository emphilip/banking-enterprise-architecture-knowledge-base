# Customer Lifecycle Management

**Definition.** Customer Lifecycle Management is the L1 business capability covering the end-to-end ability of a bank to acquire, identify, service, and maintain relationships with parties across their lifecycle, aligned to the BIAN Customer Management and Party reference business areas.
**Also known as:** Customer Lifecycle.

## Relationships
- Customer Lifecycle Management is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Customer Lifecycle Management depends on the Master Data Management capability.
- Customer Lifecycle Management depends on the CRM Platform capability.
- Customer Lifecycle Management is related to Marketing Management.

## Details
Customer Lifecycle Management spans BIAN service domains such as Customer Relationship Management, Party Reference Data Management, Customer Profile, and Customer Case. Customer Lifecycle Management decomposes into Customer Acquisition, Party Data Management, Customer Servicing, and Relationship Management. Typical scope includes maintaining a single trusted view of the party, orchestrating onboarding, and supporting servicing interactions across channels. In APQC PCF terms it maps to the "Market and Sell Products and Services" and "Manage Customer Service" process groups.
