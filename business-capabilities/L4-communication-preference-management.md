# Communication Preference Management

**Definition.** Communication Preference Management is the L4 business capability that maintains channel, frequency and topic preferences and suppression rules used to govern outbound contact, supporting the BIAN Customer Profile and Contact Dialogue service domains.
**Also known as:** Channel Preferences, Marketing Permissions, Contact Preferences.

## Relationships
- Communication Preference Management is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Communication Preference Management is derived from Consent & Preference Management.
- Communication Preference Management depends on the CRM Platform capability.

## Details
Communication Preference Management extends its parent Consent & Preference Management within the Customer Management domain and is grounded in BIAN Service Domain mappings and APQC PCF alignment. Communication Preference Management relies on the CRM Platform technology capability for its core processing and integrates with adjacent Customer Management capabilities. Implementations typically apply cloud-native, API-led and AI-assisted patterns to deliver Communication Preference Management at scale.
