# Negotiable Instrument Processing

**Definition.** Negotiable Instrument Processing is the Channels & Engagement capability that handles cheque deposits, drafts and money orders at the counter, supporting the BIAN Point of Service fulfilment service domain.
**Also known as:** Cheque Processing (Branch), Draft Handling.

## Relationships
- Negotiable Instrument Processing is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Negotiable Instrument Processing is derived from Teller Operations.
- Negotiable Instrument Processing depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
Negotiable Instrument Processing supports BIAN Point of Service fulfilment for paper instruments: accepting endorsed cheques, drafts and money orders, capturing the MICR line, and presenting images for clearing under Check 21 (substitute checks) and image-exchange networks. Funds availability and holds are governed by Reg CC, which sets next-day and extended-hold rules the teller applies and discloses at deposit. Endorsement verification, duplicate-presentment and altered/forged-instrument checks are the fraud controls. Captured images feed back-office clearing while the deposit posts provisionally to the core, with chargeback on return.
