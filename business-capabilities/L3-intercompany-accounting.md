# Intercompany Accounting

**Definition.** Intercompany Accounting records, matches and reconciles transactions between group entities, mapping to APQC PCF "Post and reconcile intercompany transactions" and supporting IFRS 10 consolidation.
**Also known as:** Intercompany Transactions, IC Accounting, Inter-Entity Accounting.

## Relationships
- Intercompany Accounting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Intercompany Accounting is derived from General Ledger Accounting.
- Intercompany Accounting depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
Intercompany Accounting books reciprocal payables/receivables and intra-group sales between legal entities and matches them so that mismatches are resolved before close. Because IFRS 10 requires that intra-group balances, transactions and unrealised profit be eliminated in full on consolidation, unmatched intercompany differences directly distort group accounts; this capability is the prerequisite control that makes Intercompany Elimination clean. Transfer-pricing and withholding-tax treatment of cross-border intercompany flows is also captured here.
