# Hedge Management

**Definition.** Hedge Management designates, executes and accounts for hedging relationships used to manage structural interest-rate and FX risk, applying IFRS 9 hedge-accounting requirements.
**Also known as:** Hedge Accounting Management, Treasury Hedging, Macro Hedge Management.

## Relationships
- Hedge Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Hedge Management is derived from Asset Liability Management.
- Hedge Management depends on the Treasury Management System capability.

## Details
Hedge Management designates derivatives (interest-rate swaps, FX forwards) as fair-value or cash-flow hedges and applies IFRS 9 hedge accounting, which requires a formal economic relationship between hedged item and instrument, that credit risk does not dominate value changes, and a hedge ratio consistent with risk-management intent. Qualifying relationships defer or match P&L volatility (cash-flow hedge reserve in OCI), and ineffectiveness is recognised immediately, so designation, documentation and effectiveness testing are tightly controlled.
