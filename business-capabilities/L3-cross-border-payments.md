# Cross-Border Payments

**Definition.** Cross-Border Payments is the L3 business capability for executing international transfers across currencies and jurisdictions, typically over correspondent banking and SWIFT networks with FX and compliance handling.
**Also known as:** International Payments, SWIFT Payments.

## Relationships
- Cross-Border Payments is defined in the Payments domain.
- Cross-Border Payments is derived from Payment Execution.
- Cross-Border Payments depends on the Payment Orchestration capability.
- Cross-Border Payments is related to Domestic Payments.

## Details
Cross-Border Payments aligns to BIAN Correspondent Bank Operations and Payment Execution service domains. Typical sub-functions include FX conversion, correspondent routing, SWIFT gpi tracking, sanctions screening integration, and fee and charging handling. ISO 20022 migration (e.g. CBPR+) and payment orchestration hubs are central to modernising international flows.
