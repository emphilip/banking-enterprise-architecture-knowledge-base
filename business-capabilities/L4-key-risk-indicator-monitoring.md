# Key Risk Indicator Monitoring

**Definition.** Key Risk Indicator Monitoring defines, thresholds and tracks KRIs that give early warning of rising operational risk and control failure, a Basel operational-risk management tool.
**Also known as:** KRI Monitoring, Risk Indicator Tracking.

## Relationships
- Key Risk Indicator Monitoring is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Key Risk Indicator Monitoring is derived from Risk & Control Self-Assessment.
- Key Risk Indicator Monitoring depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.
- Key Risk Indicator Monitoring depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Key Risk Indicator Monitoring operationalises the BCBS d292 monitoring-and-reporting principle by tracking metrics — failed-trade rates, system downtime, staff turnover, complaint volumes, ageing of open audit issues — against green/amber/red thresholds. Breaches trigger escalation and link to the RCSA control assessment. Indicators are designed to be leading rather than lagging so that deteriorating control environments are detected before loss events crystallise, and trends feed the operational-risk profile and board reporting.
