# Reconciliation & Close

**Definition.** Reconciliation & Close performs period-end ledger reconciliation and orchestrates the financial close, aligning to APQC PCF "Reconcile general ledger accounts" and the BIAN Financial Control service domain.
**Also known as:** Period-End Close, GL Reconciliation, Financial Close.

## Relationships
- Reconciliation & Close is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Reconciliation & Close is derived from General Ledger Accounting.
- Reconciliation & Close depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
Reconciliation & Close reconciles GL balances to sub-ledgers and external sources, posts adjusting entries and locks the period. APQC tracks "cycle time in days to perform the annual close" as the headline efficiency measure; leading banks compress this through pre-close reconciliations and continuous accounting. Under SOX/ICFR the close is a controlled process: balance-sheet accounts must be substantiated and certified, reconciling items aged and cleared, and the period sign-off evidenced before results are released.
