# Intraday Liquidity Monitoring

**Definition.** Intraday Liquidity Monitoring tracks intraday payment and settlement flows, available intraday liquidity and timing of obligations against the Basel intraday liquidity monitoring tools.
**Also known as:** Intraday Liquidity Management, Intraday Position Monitoring.

## Relationships
- Intraday Liquidity Monitoring is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Intraday Liquidity Monitoring is derived from Liquidity Risk Measurement.
- Intraday Liquidity Monitoring depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Intraday Liquidity Monitoring depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Intraday Liquidity Monitoring reports against the seven BCBS d248 monitoring tools: daily maximum intraday liquidity usage, available intraday liquidity at the start of the business day, total payments, time-specific obligations, value of customer payments made on behalf of correspondents, intraday credit lines extended, and intraday throughput. It tracks net debit positions in RTGS and CHIPS/CLS settlement, manages timing of large outflows against incoming liquidity, and produces the supervisory monitoring returns for direct and correspondent participants.
