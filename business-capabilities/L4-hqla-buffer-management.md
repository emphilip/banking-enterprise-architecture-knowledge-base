# HQLA Buffer Management

**Definition.** HQLA Buffer Management classifies, values and manages the eligibility and haircuts of high-quality liquid assets held to meet the LCR, defined by the Basel III liquidity coverage ratio standard.
**Also known as:** Liquid Asset Buffer, HQLA Eligibility Management.

## Relationships
- HQLA Buffer Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- HQLA Buffer Management is derived from LCR & NSFR Management.
- HQLA Buffer Management depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- HQLA Buffer Management depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.

## Details
HQLA Buffer Management categorises the liquidity buffer per BCBS d238 into Level 1 (cash, central-bank reserves, 0% haircut), Level 2A (certain sovereigns and covered bonds, 15% haircut, capped at 40% of the buffer) and Level 2B (lower-rated corporate bonds and equities, 25-50% haircut, capped at 15%). It enforces operational requirements — unencumbered, monetisable, under treasury control, free of legal or operational impediments — and verifies eligibility, concentration and currency-matching of the buffer used in the LCR numerator.
