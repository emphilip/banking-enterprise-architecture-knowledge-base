# Promise-To-Pay Management

**Definition.** Promise-To-Pay Management is the capability that records, monitors and follows up borrower repayment commitments during collections, mapping to the BIAN Delinquent Account Handling service domain.
**Also known as:** PTP Management, Repayment Promise Tracking.

## Relationships
- Promise-To-Pay Management is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Promise-To-Pay Management is derived from Delinquency Management.
- Promise-To-Pay Management depends on the Workflow Orchestration capability.
- Promise-To-Pay Management depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
The BIAN Delinquent Account Handling service domain tracks arrangements that Promise-To-Pay Management captures and monitors. A concrete banking specific: a recorded promise-to-pay (amount and date) suppresses further dunning until the date, and a broken promise re-escalates the account and is a key input to roll-rate and cure-rate collections analytics.
