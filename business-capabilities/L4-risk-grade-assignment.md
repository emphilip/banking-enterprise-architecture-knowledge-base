# Risk-Grade Assignment

**Definition.** Risk-Grade Assignment is the capability that assigns internal risk ratings and probability-of-default grades to applicants, mapping to the BIAN Customer Credit Rating service domain.
**Also known as:** Credit Grading, Rating Assignment.

## Relationships
- Risk-Grade Assignment is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Risk-Grade Assignment is derived from Affordability Assessment.
- Risk-Grade Assignment depends on the Credit Decisioning Engine capability.
- Risk-Grade Assignment depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
The BIAN Customer Credit Rating service domain holds the bank's internal rating, which Risk-Grade Assignment produces from scorecards and PD models. A concrete banking specific: banks on the Internal Ratings-Based (IRB) approach under the Basel framework assign each exposure a probability of default (PD) grade that feeds regulatory risk-weighted-asset and capital calculations.
