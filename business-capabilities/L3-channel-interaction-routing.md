# Channel Interaction Routing

**Definition.** Channel Interaction Routing is the Channels & Engagement capability that matches contacts to the best-suited servicing resource using skills and priority rules, realising the BIAN Contact Routing service domain; it is named to avoid the Interaction Routing alias under Customer Management.
**Also known as:** Intent Routing, Channel Routing Capability.

## Relationships
- Channel Interaction Routing is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Channel Interaction Routing is derived from Contact Center.
- Channel Interaction Routing depends on the Contact Center Platform capability.

## Details
Channel Interaction Routing realises BIAN Contact Routing: it classifies intent (often from IVR or NLU), evaluates customer value and priority, and selects the agent or queue with the matching skill and availability. Skills-based and value-based routing, callback offers and overflow rules balance service level against cost. Routing reads omnichannel context so a customer mid-dispute is steered to the handling team, and accessibility routing can prioritise relay or assisted lines. Real-time queue analytics and SLA breach prediction feed dynamic re-routing across voice and digital channels.
