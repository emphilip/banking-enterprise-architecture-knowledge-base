# Cash-in-Transit Coordination

**Definition.** Cash-in-Transit Coordination is the Channels & Engagement capability that schedules and reconciles armoured carrier collections and deliveries for branches and ATMs, supporting the BIAN Branch Currency Management and ATM Network Operations service domains.
**Also known as:** CIT Coordination, Armoured Carrier Management.

## Relationships
- Cash-in-Transit Coordination is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Cash-in-Transit Coordination is derived from Cash & Vault Management.
- Cash-in-Transit Coordination depends on the Notification Services capability.

## Details
Cash-in-Transit Coordination supports BIAN Branch Currency Management and ATM Network Operations by booking armoured-carrier (Brinks, Loomis, G4S) runs, tracking sealed-bag chain of custody, and reconciling manifests against vault and ATM cash positions on collection and delivery. It manages SLAs, route windows and contingency for missed runs, and surfaces discrepancies (short bags, count differences) for investigation as a loss control. Notifications confirm scheduled stops, exceptions and proof of delivery to branch and treasury operations. Coordination with ATM Cash Management aligns replenishment runs to forecast demand to minimise visits and idle cash.
