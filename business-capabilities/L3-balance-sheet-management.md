# Balance Sheet Management

**Definition.** Balance Sheet Management optimises the structure, margin and risk of the bank's assets and liabilities under ALCO oversight, aligning to the BIAN Asset & Liability Management service domain.
**Also known as:** Structural Balance Sheet Management, ALCO Management, Balance Sheet Optimisation.

## Relationships
- Balance Sheet Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Balance Sheet Management is derived from Asset Liability Management.
- Balance Sheet Management depends on the Asset Liability Management Engine capability.

## Details
Balance Sheet Management is the ALCO-governed discipline that sets the target asset/liability mix, net-interest-margin trajectory and structural rate/liquidity profile, trading off NII, IRRBB EVE sensitivity, LCR/NSFR liquidity ratios and capital/RWA consumption simultaneously. It runs forward balance-sheet simulations across rate and growth scenarios, allocates limits to business lines, and recommends hedging and funding actions so the bank earns target returns within its risk appetite and regulatory constraints.
