# Portfolio Accounting

**Definition.** Portfolio Accounting maintains positions, valuations, cost basis, income, and the book-of-record for investment portfolios; it supports the BIAN Securities Position Management service domain.
**Also known as:** Investment Bookkeeping, Investment Book of Record Capability.

## Relationships
- Portfolio Accounting is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Portfolio Accounting is derived from Portfolio Management.
- Portfolio Accounting depends on the Portfolio Management System capability.
- Portfolio Accounting depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
The BIAN Securities Position Management service domain tracks holdings and movements. Portfolio Accounting maintains the Investment Book of Record (IBOR) on a trade-date and settlement-date basis, applies corporate-action adjustments, accrues income, and reconciles to custodians. Under the US T+1 settlement cycle, accounting must reflect positions and cash one business day after trade date, tightening intraday reconciliation.
