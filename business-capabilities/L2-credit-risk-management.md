# Credit Risk Management

**Definition.** Credit Risk Management is the business capability that assesses, quantifies, and controls the risk of borrower default across lending and counterparty exposures. It corresponds to the BIAN Credit Risk Models and Credit Management service domains.
**Also known as:** Credit Risk.

## Relationships
- Credit Risk Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Credit Risk Management is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Credit Risk Management depends on the Credit Decisioning Engine capability.

## Details
Credit Risk Management covers PD, LGD, and EAD modeling, IFRS 9 and CECL expected credit loss provisioning, limit and exposure management, and portfolio concentration analysis. Model governance, backtesting, and explainable machine-learning scorecards are central to regulatory acceptance under Basel internal ratings-based approaches.
