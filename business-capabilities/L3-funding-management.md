# Funding Management

**Definition.** Funding Management plans and executes wholesale and deposit funding to meet the bank's liquidity needs, aligning to the BIAN Funding service domain, distinct from the Risk-domain Funding Risk Management capability.
**Also known as:** Funding Execution, Wholesale Funding Management, Treasury Funding.

## Relationships
- Funding Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Funding Management is derived from Liquidity Management.
- Funding Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Funding Management decides the funding mix across retail/corporate deposits, money-market borrowing, repo, certificates of deposit and term debt issuance to fund the balance sheet at acceptable cost and stability. It is shaped by the Basel III Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR, BCBS d295), which requires available stable funding to cover required stable funding over a one-year horizon, steering the bank toward longer-tenor and more stable liabilities and away from over-reliance on short-term wholesale funding.
