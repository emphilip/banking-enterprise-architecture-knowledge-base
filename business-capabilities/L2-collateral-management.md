# Collateral Management

**Definition.** Collateral Management is the L2 business capability for capturing, valuing, and monitoring security pledged against credit exposures, including perfection, revaluation, and release, aligned to the BIAN Collateral Administration service domain.
**Also known as:** Collateral & Security.

## Relationships
- Collateral Management is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Collateral Management is derived from Lending.
- Collateral Management depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.
- Collateral Management is related to Credit Risk Management.

## Details
Collateral Management maps to BIAN Collateral Administration and Collateral Asset Administration service domains. Typical sub-functions include collateral registration and perfection, valuation and revaluation, allocation to facilities, covenant monitoring, and release. It underpins the Mortgage Origination and Trade Finance Processing business processes and feeds loss-given-default inputs to Credit Risk Management.
