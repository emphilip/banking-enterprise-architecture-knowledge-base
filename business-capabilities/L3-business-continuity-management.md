# Business Continuity Management

**Definition.** Business Continuity Management maintains continuity and recovery plans, impact analyses and resilience testing so critical operations survive disruption, aligned with the BCBS Principles for Operational Resilience.
**Also known as:** BCM, Operational Resilience, Disaster Recovery Planning.

## Relationships
- Business Continuity Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Business Continuity Management is derived from Operational Risk Management.
- Business Continuity Management depends on the Threat Detection capability.
- Business Continuity Management depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
Business Continuity Management implements the BCBS d516 Principles for Operational Resilience: mapping critical operations and their interconnections, setting impact tolerances for disruption, and conducting business impact analysis with recovery time and recovery point objectives. It maintains continuity, disaster recovery and crisis-management plans, runs severe-but-plausible scenario testing including third-party dependency failure, and links to the BCBS d292 operational-risk programme. Cyber-induced disruption is monitored through threat detection.
