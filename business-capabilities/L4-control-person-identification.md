# Control Person Identification

**Definition.** Control Person Identification identifies the individual with significant managerial responsibility or control of a legal entity, per the FinCEN CDD/Beneficial Ownership Rule control prong.
**Also known as:** Controlling Party Identification, Control Prong.

## Relationships
- Control Person Identification is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Control Person Identification is derived from Beneficial Ownership Identification.
- Control Person Identification depends on the Data Governance capability.
- Control Person Identification depends on the Document Management capability.

## Details
The FinCEN CDD Rule's control prong requires identifying at least one individual with significant responsibility to control, manage, or direct a legal entity customer — for example a CEO, CFO, COO, managing member, general partner, or president — regardless of equity ownership. This complements the 25% ownership prong: even where no individual reaches the ownership threshold, a control person must always be identified and verified. This ensures a responsible natural person is on record for every legal-entity account.
