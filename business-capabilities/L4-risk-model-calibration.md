# Risk Model Calibration

**Definition.** Risk Model Calibration tunes the factors, weights and thresholds of customer risk-rating models to maintain a defensible risk-based approach per FATF Recommendation 1.
**Also known as:** Rating Model Tuning, Risk Factor Weighting.

## Relationships
- Risk Model Calibration is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Risk Model Calibration is derived from Customer Risk Rating.
- Risk Model Calibration depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.
- Risk Model Calibration depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
FATF R.1 requires institutions to apply a risk-based approach and to keep risk assessments current; a customer-risk model that is not periodically recalibrated drifts from actual risk and becomes indefensible to examiners. Calibration revisits factor selection (geography, product, channel, PEP status), reweights factors against observed SAR/typology outcomes, and resets risk-tier thresholds. Changes are validated and documented under model-governance discipline so that risk tiering driving CDD/EDD and KYC refresh cadence remains evidence-based.
