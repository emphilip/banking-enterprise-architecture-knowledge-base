# AML Case Investigation

**Definition.** AML Case Investigation gathers evidence, analyses networks and reaches a disposition on escalated alerts to support SAR/STR decisions, per FATF Recommendation 20 and US Bank Secrecy Act expectations.
**Also known as:** Financial Crime Investigation, AML Case Handling.

## Relationships
- AML Case Investigation is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- AML Case Investigation is derived from AML Monitoring.
- AML Case Investigation depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- AML Case Investigation depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.

## Details
Under the BSA, an institution that detects activity it knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect is suspicious must investigate and, where the conclusion is positive, file a SAR within 30 calendar days of initial detection. Investigation consolidates transaction history, KYC data, prior alerts and adverse media into a case, applies network/link analysis to expose related parties and accounts, and documents the rationale for either filing a SAR or closing the case. FATF R.20 obliges prompt reporting once suspicion is formed.
