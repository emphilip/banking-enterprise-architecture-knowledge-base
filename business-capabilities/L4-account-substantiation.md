# Account Substantiation

**Definition.** Account Substantiation evidences and certifies balance-sheet account balances against supporting documentation, mapping to APQC PCF "Reconcile general ledger accounts" activity.
**Also known as:** Balance Sheet Substantiation, Account Certification, Recon Substantiation.

## Relationships
- Account Substantiation is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Account Substantiation is derived from Reconciliation & Close.
- Account Substantiation depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
Account Substantiation requires each balance-sheet account owner to prove the GL balance with supporting evidence (sub-ledger detail, bank statements, schedules), classify and age reconciling items, and certify the account at period-end. It is a frontline SOX/ICFR control over financial-statement integrity: risk-rank accounts so high-risk balances get deeper scrutiny, escalate aged or unexplained items, and retain the certification and documentation as audit evidence that asset and liability balances are real and complete.
