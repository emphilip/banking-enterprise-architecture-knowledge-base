# Chart Of Accounts Management

**Definition.** Chart Of Accounts Management defines and governs the account hierarchy, ledger structures and posting rules of the bank, supporting the BIAN Financial Accounting service domain and APQC PCF "Manage and process general ledger" measures.
**Also known as:** COA Management, Account Structure Governance, Ledger Hierarchy Management.

## Relationships
- Chart Of Accounts Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Chart Of Accounts Management is derived from General Ledger Accounting.
- Chart Of Accounts Management depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
Chart Of Accounts Management maintains the segmented account structure (natural account, cost centre, profit centre, legal entity) and the operating, statutory and consolidation ledgers built on it. APQC benchmarks the "number of accounts in the chart of accounts" because excess accounts inflate close effort; this capability rationalises that structure and controls which dimensions post where. A single group COA with mapped local variants underpins consistent IFRS 10 consolidation and multi-GAAP reporting.
