# Liquidity Risk Management

**Definition.** Liquidity Risk Management is the business capability that monitors and controls the bank's ability to meet obligations as they fall due under normal and stressed conditions. It aligns with the BIAN Liquidity Risk service domain and Basel LCR and NSFR standards.
**Also known as:** Liquidity Risk.

## Relationships
- Liquidity Risk Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Liquidity Risk Management is derived from Risk Management.
- Liquidity Risk Management depends on the Data Warehousing capability.

## Details
Liquidity Risk Management computes the liquidity coverage ratio, net stable funding ratio, cash-flow gap analysis, and intraday liquidity monitoring. It depends on aggregated position and cash-flow data, runs survival-horizon stress tests, and coordinates closely with treasury and asset-liability management to maintain high-quality liquid asset buffers.
