# Beneficial Ownership Identification

**Definition.** Beneficial Ownership Identification determines and verifies the natural persons who ultimately own or control a legal entity per FATF Recommendations 24/25, the FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Rule and EU AMLD6.
**Also known as:** UBO Identification, Ownership Verification.

## Relationships
- Beneficial Ownership Identification is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Beneficial Ownership Identification is derived from KYC Management.
- Beneficial Ownership Identification depends on the Data Governance capability.
- Beneficial Ownership Identification depends on the KYC Onboarding Platform capability.

## Details
The FinCEN CDD/Beneficial Ownership Rule requires identifying each individual who owns 25% or more of the equity of a legal entity customer (the ownership prong) plus one individual with significant managerial control (the control prong). FATF R.24/R.25 require accurate, up-to-date beneficial-ownership information for legal persons and arrangements held in registries or otherwise accessible. AMLD6 reinforces central beneficial-ownership registers and verification across the EU.
