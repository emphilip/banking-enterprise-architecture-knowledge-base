# Financial Crime Risk Compliance

**Definition.** Financial Crime Risk Compliance covers bribery and corruption, tax-evasion and other predicate-offence controls beyond money laundering and sanctions, reflecting the EU 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (Directive (EU) 2018/1673) harmonised predicate-offence list and the Wolfsberg Group FCCQ scope.
**Also known as:** FinCrime Risk Compliance, Wider Financial Crime.

## Details
The EU 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (Directive (EU) 2018/1673) harmonised a list of 22 predicate offences for money laundering across the EU, extending compliance scope beyond core AML/CFT to corruption, tax crimes, environmental crime and cybercrime, and introduced criminal liability for legal persons. (The separate 2024 AML package — Directive (EU) 2024/1640, the AMLR and the AMLA — governs the institutional and supervisory framework.) The Wolfsberg Financial Crime Compliance Questionnaire (FCCQ) frames an institution's wider financial-crime control environment, sitting alongside the CBDDQ. Programs must demonstrate a risk-based approach across all predicate offences, not only laundering and sanctions evasion.
