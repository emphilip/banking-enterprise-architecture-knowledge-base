# Cash Flow Forecasting

**Definition.** Cash Flow Forecasting projects expected treasury cash inflows and outflows across time horizons to drive funding and investment decisions, supporting the BIAN Treasury service domain.
**Also known as:** Treasury Cash Forecasting, Liquidity Cash Forecasting, Cash Projection.

## Relationships
- Cash Flow Forecasting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Cash Flow Forecasting is derived from Treasury Cash Positioning.
- Cash Flow Forecasting depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Cash Flow Forecasting builds short-term (intraday/daily) and structural (weeks/months) projections of contractual and behavioural cash flows — maturing wholesale funding, loan drawdowns, deposit churn, settlement obligations — so treasury can pre-position funding and invest surpluses. The forecast horizon and granularity underpin the Basel III LCR net-cash-outflow estimation (BCBS d238) and the survival-horizon view; AI/ML forecasting of behavioural deposit and receivable flows materially improves accuracy over deterministic schedules.
