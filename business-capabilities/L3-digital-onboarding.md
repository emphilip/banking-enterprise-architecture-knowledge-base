# Digital Onboarding

**Definition.** Digital Onboarding is the Channels & Engagement capability that lets prospects apply for and activate products entirely through digital channels, fronting the BIAN Party Authentication and eBranch service domains while orchestrating identity, consent and account set-up; it is distinct from the Customer Onboarding process and the Customer Acquisition capability.
**Also known as:** Digital Account Opening, Self-Service Onboarding.

## Relationships
- Digital Onboarding is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Digital Onboarding is derived from Digital Banking.
- Digital Onboarding depends on the Digital Channel Platform capability.
- Digital Onboarding depends on the Customer Identity capability.

## Details
Digital Onboarding realises the in-channel front of BIAN Party Authentication and eBranch, sequencing remote identity proofing, electronic consent and first funding into a single self-service journey. Under PSD2 it must apply Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) at enrolment, and electronic identification must satisfy eIDAS/AML eKYC standards (document plus biometric liveness). Drop-off is the dominant design risk, so journeys use progressive disclosure, pre-fill and save-and-resume, and they feed downstream Customer Due Diligence rather than re-collecting it. Cloud-native engagement platforms (Backbase, Temenos, ebankIT) expose onboarding as orchestrated micro-journeys over core and KYC APIs.
