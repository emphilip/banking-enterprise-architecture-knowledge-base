# Capital Adequacy Reporting

**Definition.** Capital Adequacy Reporting prepares and submits regulatory capital, leverage-ratio and own-funds returns (e.g. COREP) under the Basel III framework, distinct from the Risk-domain Regulatory Capital Adequacy assessment.
**Also known as:** Capital Reporting, COREP Reporting, Own Funds Reporting.

## Relationships
- Capital Adequacy Reporting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Capital Adequacy Reporting is derived from Regulatory Capital Management.
- Capital Adequacy Reporting depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.

## Details
Capital Adequacy Reporting populates the supervisory own-funds and capital-requirement templates — in the EU the EBA COREP framework (own funds, credit/market/operational risk RWA, large exposures, leverage ratio) submitted under the ITS on supervisory reporting. It reconciles regulatory own funds (CET1, AT1, Tier 2 after deductions) against accounting equity, applies the Basel III ratio definitions, and validates the return against EBA plausibility/cross-checks before filing through the regulatory submission channel.
