# Collateral Optimisation

**Definition.** Collateral Optimisation allocates and substitutes eligible collateral across obligations to minimise funding cost and maximise reuse, supporting the BIAN Reservation Administration service domain.
**Also known as:** Collateral Allocation, Collateral Substitution, Treasury Collateral Optimisation.

## Relationships
- Collateral Optimisation is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Collateral Optimisation is derived from Collateral & Reserve Management.
- Collateral Optimisation depends on the Treasury Management System capability.

## Details
Collateral Optimisation solves the "cheapest-to-deliver" allocation problem — posting the least valuable eligible asset that satisfies each counterparty/CCP/central-bank requirement after eligibility schedules and haircuts, while reserving high-quality liquid assets to preserve the Basel III LCR buffer (BCBS d238). It substitutes collateral as availability and prices change, tracks encumbrance and reuse rights, and reduces the cost of funding margin and the drag of over-collateralisation across the firm's pledged-asset inventory.
