# Hedge Effectiveness Testing

**Definition.** Hedge Effectiveness Testing assesses prospective and ongoing hedge effectiveness to qualify and maintain hedge accounting under IFRS 9.
**Also known as:** Effectiveness Testing, Hedge Qualification Testing, IFRS 9 Effectiveness.

## Relationships
- Hedge Effectiveness Testing is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Hedge Effectiveness Testing is derived from Hedge Management.
- Hedge Effectiveness Testing depends on the Asset Liability Management Engine capability.

## Details
Hedge Effectiveness Testing evaluates the three IFRS 9 qualifying criteria — an economic relationship between hedged item and hedging instrument, that credit risk does not dominate value changes, and that the designated hedge ratio matches the actual quantities used. Unlike the old IAS 39 80-125% bright line, IFRS 9 requires a forward-looking economic-relationship assessment and measurement of any ineffectiveness to P&L. The capability runs prospective tests at designation, monitors the relationship each period, and triggers rebalancing or de-designation when criteria fail.
