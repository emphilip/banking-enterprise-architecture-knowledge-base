# Party Identifier Management

**Definition.** Party Identifier Management is the L4 business capability that allocates, maintains and cross-references unique party identifiers and external keys across systems, realising the BIAN Party Reference Data Directory service domain.
**Also known as:** Party ID Management, Identifier Cross-Referencing.

## Relationships
- Party Identifier Management is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Party Identifier Management is derived from Party Reference Data.
- Party Identifier Management depends on the Master Data Management capability.

## Details
Party Identifier Management extends its parent Party Reference Data within the Customer Management domain and is grounded in BIAN Service Domain mappings and APQC PCF alignment. Party Identifier Management relies on the Master Data Management technology capability for its core processing and integrates with adjacent Customer Management capabilities. Implementations typically apply cloud-native, API-led and AI-assisted patterns to deliver Party Identifier Management at scale.
