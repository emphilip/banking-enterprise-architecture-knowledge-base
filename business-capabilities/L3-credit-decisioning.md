# Credit Decisioning

**Definition.** Credit Decisioning is the L3 business capability for evaluating creditworthiness and rendering approve, decline, or refer decisions using scorecards, policy rules, and predictive models.
**Also known as:** Credit Assessment, Underwriting Decision.

## Relationships
- Credit Decisioning is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Credit Decisioning is derived from Loan Origination.
- Credit Decisioning depends on the Credit Decisioning Engine capability.
- Credit Decisioning depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.

## Details
Credit Decisioning aligns to BIAN Credit Management and Customer Credit Rating service domains, and underpins the Credit Underwriting and Loan Origination Workflow business processes. Typical sub-functions include data aggregation, credit scoring, affordability and policy-rule evaluation, pricing, and decision recording with audit. Modern AI decisioning engines apply machine learning to expand credit access while maintaining explainability and fairness controls.
