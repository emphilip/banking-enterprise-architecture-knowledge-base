# Overdraft Management

**Definition.** Overdraft Management is the L2 business capability for granting and administering arranged and unarranged overdraft facilities on current accounts, including limit setting, fee and interest handling, and excess management.
**Also known as:** Overdrafts.

## Relationships
- Overdraft Management is defined in the Deposits & Accounts domain.
- Overdraft Management is derived from Deposits.
- Overdraft Management depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.
- Overdraft Management is related to Account Servicing.

## Details
Overdraft Management maps to BIAN Current Account and Credit Facility service domains. Typical sub-functions include overdraft limit assessment and setup, arranged versus unarranged handling, overdraft interest and fee calculation, excess and returned-item management, and customer notifications. It intersects with Credit Decisioning for limit assessment and with Account Servicing for posting. Core banking platforms enforce limits and compute overdraft charges in real time.
