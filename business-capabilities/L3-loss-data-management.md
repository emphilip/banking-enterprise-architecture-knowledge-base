# Loss Data Management

**Definition.** Loss Data Management captures, classifies and reconciles internal operational-loss events and integrates external loss data for measurement and benchmarking, aligned with the Basel operational-risk principles and the ORX loss data exchange.
**Also known as:** Internal Loss Data, Loss Event Management, External Loss Data.

## Relationships
- Loss Data Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Loss Data Management is derived from Operational Risk Management.
- Loss Data Management depends on the Data Warehousing capability.
- Loss Data Management depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
Loss Data Management maintains the internal loss event database that underpins the Basel Standardised Approach for operational risk, applying the de-minimis collection threshold (EUR 20,000 for the Internal Loss Multiplier and a ten-year history) and mapping each event to the Basel event-type and business-line matrices. It reconciles gross loss, recoveries and provisions to the general ledger, supports near-miss and boundary-event capture, and consumes ORX external/consortium data to benchmark frequency and severity of rare tail events.
