# Risk Appetite Management

**Definition.** Risk Appetite Management sets, cascades and monitors the board-approved risk appetite statement and tolerance limits across risk types, aligned with the COSO ERM framework's risk appetite and strategy component.
**Also known as:** Risk Appetite Framework, Tolerance Setting, RAS Management.

## Relationships
- Risk Appetite Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Appetite Management is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Risk Appetite Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- Risk Appetite Management depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.

## Details
Risk Appetite Management operationalises the COSO ERM "Strategy and Objective-Setting" and "Performance" components by translating a qualitative risk appetite statement into quantitative tolerance and limit thresholds (e.g. capital-at-risk, earnings volatility, concentration caps) per risk type. It follows the BCBS Corporate Governance Principles for Banks (BCBS d328), under which the board approves the appetite, management cascades it into business-unit limits, and breaches escalate through a defined governance route. Utilisation against appetite is reported alongside ICAAP outputs.
