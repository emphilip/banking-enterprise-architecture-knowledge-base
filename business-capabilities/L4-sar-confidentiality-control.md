# SAR Confidentiality Control

**Definition.** SAR Confidentiality Control enforces tipping-off prohibitions and restricted access to SAR information per FATF Recommendation 21 and US Bank Secrecy Act confidentiality provisions.
**Also known as:** Tipping-Off Prevention, SAR Access Control.

## Relationships
- SAR Confidentiality Control is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- SAR Confidentiality Control is derived from SAR Management.
- SAR Confidentiality Control depends on the Document Management capability.
- SAR Confidentiality Control depends on the Data Governance capability.

## Details
FATF Recommendation 21 protects staff who report in good faith from liability and prohibits tipping off the customer that a report has been or may be filed. The BSA makes unauthorised disclosure of a SAR (including its existence) a federal offence, with safe-harbor protection for the filing institution. This capability restricts SAR access to need-to-know roles, suppresses any indication of a filing in customer-facing systems, and audits access, ensuring investigators and relationship managers cannot inadvertently disclose a report to the subject.
