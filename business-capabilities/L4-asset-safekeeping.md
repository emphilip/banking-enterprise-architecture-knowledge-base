# Asset Safekeeping

**Definition.** Asset Safekeeping records and protects book-entry and physical securities holdings and reconciles against depositories; it supports the BIAN Custody Administration service domain.
**Also known as:** Safekeeping Records, Depository Reconciliation.

## Relationships
- Asset Safekeeping is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Asset Safekeeping is derived from Custody & Safekeeping.
- Asset Safekeeping depends on the Order Management System capability.
- Asset Safekeeping depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
Most US securities are immobilised in book-entry form at DTC and held through nominee name, so Asset Safekeeping maintains the custodian's beneficial-ownership records and reconciles them daily to the depository's position to detect breaks. It segregates client assets from firm assets per SEC custody rules and tracks any physical certificates in vault, supporting the BIAN Custody Administration service domain.
