# KYC Refresh Management

**Definition.** KYC Refresh Management keeps customer records current through periodic and event-driven (perpetual) review based on risk, per UK Money Laundering Regulations 2017 regulation 28 ongoing-monitoring duties and FATF Recommendation 10.
**Also known as:** Perpetual KYC, Periodic Review, KYC Remediation.

## Relationships
- KYC Refresh Management is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- KYC Refresh Management is derived from KYC Management.
- KYC Refresh Management depends on the Document Management capability.
- KYC Refresh Management depends on the KYC Onboarding Platform capability.

## Details
UK MLR 2017 regulation 28 requires ongoing monitoring of business relationships, including keeping CDD documents, data and information up to date. FATF R.10 likewise mandates ongoing due diligence on the business relationship and scrutiny of transactions. Refresh cadence is risk-based: high-risk and PEP customers are typically reviewed annually, medium-risk every two to three years, low-risk up to five years, while perpetual KYC replaces fixed cycles with trigger events (e.g. ownership change, adverse media, unusual activity) that drive event-driven reviews.
