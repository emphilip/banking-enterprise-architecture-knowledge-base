# Risk Tolerance Profiling

**Definition.** Risk Tolerance Profiling captures and scores a client's capacity and willingness to bear loss to drive suitable advice; it is grounded in MiFID II suitability requirements and the BIAN Customer Investment Profile service domain.
**Also known as:** Risk Profiling, Risk Capacity Assessment.

## Relationships
- Risk Tolerance Profiling is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Risk Tolerance Profiling is derived from Suitability Assessment.
- Risk Tolerance Profiling depends on the Investment Advisory Platform capability.
- Risk Tolerance Profiling depends on the CRM Platform capability.

## Details
The ESMA MiFID II suitability guidelines require firms to distinguish a client's risk capacity (objective ability to absorb losses, from their financial situation) from risk tolerance (subjective willingness), and warn against questionnaires that conflate the two. Risk Tolerance Profiling administers a structured questionnaire, scores both dimensions, reconciles inconsistent answers, and writes the resulting risk profile to the BIAN Customer Investment Profile service domain.
