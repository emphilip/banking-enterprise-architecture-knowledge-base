# Digital Self-Service

**Definition.** Digital Self-Service is the Channels & Engagement capability that lets customers perform enquiries, transfers and account actions without assisted support, realising the BIAN Customer Access Entitlement and Customer Workbench service domains in a self-directed digital context.
**Also known as:** Self-Service Banking, Digital Self Service.

## Relationships
- Digital Self-Service is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Digital Self-Service is derived from Digital Banking.
- Digital Self-Service depends on the Digital Channel Platform capability.
- Digital Self-Service depends on the Customer Identity capability.

## Details
Digital Self-Service surfaces BIAN Customer Workbench actions and Customer Access Entitlement permissions so customers self-direct balances, transfers, payee setup and document retrieval. Sensitive actions require PSD2 Strong Customer Authentication (two-factor, with transaction risk analysis exemptions), and authorisation is scoped per-entitlement so joint and delegated parties see only permitted accounts. Deflecting contacts from branch and call centre is the core value driver; analytics on completion and abandonment tune the journeys. WCAG 2.2 conformance keeps these flows operable for assistive technology users.
