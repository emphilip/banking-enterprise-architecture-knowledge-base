# Asset Liability Management

**Definition.** Asset Liability Management is the business capability that manages the structural interest-rate and funding risk of the balance sheet to balance profitability and risk. It aligns with the BIAN Asset & Liability Management service domain.
**Also known as:** ALM.

## Relationships
- Asset Liability Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Asset Liability Management is derived from Treasury Management.
- Asset Liability Management depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Asset Liability Management measures interest-rate risk in the banking book, net interest income and economic value of equity sensitivity, behavioral modeling of deposits, and funds transfer pricing. It depends on analytics platforms for scenario and simulation analysis, informing hedging, pricing, and balance-sheet strategy under IRRBB guidelines.
