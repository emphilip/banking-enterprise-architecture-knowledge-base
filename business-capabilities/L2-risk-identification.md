# Risk Identification

**Definition.** Risk Identification discovers, classifies and registers risks across the enterprise into a taxonomy and risk register, drawing on internal events, external data and emerging-risk scanning, supporting the COSO ERM performance component.
**Also known as:** Risk Taxonomy Management, Emerging Risk Scanning, Risk Register Management.

## Relationships
- Risk Identification is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Identification is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Risk Identification depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- Risk Identification depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
Risk Identification implements the COSO ERM "Performance" component step of identifying and assessing risk severity, maintaining a level-1/level-2 risk taxonomy (credit, market, liquidity, operational, conduct, model and emerging risks) and a firm-wide risk register. It feeds Risk & Control Self-Assessment and scenario design, and supports the BCBS d328 expectation that the board and senior management understand the full risk profile. Emerging-risk scanning captures horizon risks (climate, cyber, geopolitical) not yet crystallised in loss data.
