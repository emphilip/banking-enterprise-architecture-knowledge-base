# Asset Allocation

**Definition.** Asset Allocation sets and maintains the target mix of asset classes for a portfolio against client objectives and capital market expectations; it supports the BIAN Investment Portfolio Planning service domain.
**Also known as:** Strategic Asset Allocation, SAA/TAA.

## Relationships
- Asset Allocation is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Asset Allocation is derived from Portfolio Management.
- Asset Allocation depends on the Portfolio Management System capability.
- Asset Allocation depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Per CFA Institute practice, Asset Allocation distinguishes strategic asset allocation (the long-run policy mix anchored to the IPS) from tactical asset allocation (short-run deviations). It uses forward-looking capital market assumptions — expected return, volatility, and correlation by asset class — to derive target weights, which then drive the BIAN Investment Portfolio Planning service domain's portfolio design.
