# Source Of Wealth Verification

**Definition.** Source Of Wealth Verification establishes the origin of a customer's overall wealth and the funds used in the relationship for high-risk and PEP relationships, per FATF Recommendations 12 and 10.
**Also known as:** SoW Verification, Source Of Funds Verification.

## Relationships
- Source Of Wealth Verification is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Source Of Wealth Verification is derived from Enhanced Due Diligence Management.
- Source Of Wealth Verification depends on the Document Management capability.
- Source Of Wealth Verification depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
FATF Recommendation 12 explicitly requires firms to take reasonable measures to establish the source of wealth and source of funds of PEPs, and UK MLR 2017 regulation 33 extends this to other high-risk situations. Source of wealth explains how the customer's total net worth was accumulated (business sale, inheritance, salary), while source of funds explains the origin of the specific monies transacted. Verification relies on corroborating evidence — tax returns, audited accounts, sale contracts — not customer assertion alone.
