# Teller Operations

**Definition.** Teller Operations is the Channels & Engagement capability that performs over-the-counter cash and transaction services at the branch, realising the BIAN Point of Service service domain for in-branch fulfilment; it is distinct from the Branch Operations process.
**Also known as:** Counter Operations, Cashier Operations.

## Relationships
- Teller Operations is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Teller Operations is derived from Branch Banking.
- Teller Operations depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
Teller Operations realises BIAN Point of Service: counter deposits, withdrawals, cheque encashment, transfers and balance enquiries posted in real time to the core ledger. Branches in the US must file a Currency Transaction Report (CTR) for cash transactions over USD 10,000 and watch for structuring under the Bank Secrecy Act, so the teller workstation enforces threshold prompts and ID capture. Dual control and end-of-day till balancing against the cash drawer are the core operational controls. Cash recyclers and assisted self-service kiosks increasingly automate the physical cash handling behind the counter.
