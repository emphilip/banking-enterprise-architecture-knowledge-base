# Risk & Control Self-Assessment

**Definition.** Risk & Control Self-Assessment lets business units identify operational risks, assess inherent and residual risk and rate control effectiveness, a core Basel operational-risk tool benchmarked by ORX and supporting the BIAN Operational Risk Models service domain.
**Also known as:** RCSA, Control Self-Assessment.

## Relationships
- Risk & Control Self-Assessment is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Risk & Control Self-Assessment is derived from Operational Risk Management.
- Risk & Control Self-Assessment depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.
- Risk & Control Self-Assessment depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Risk & Control Self-Assessment is one of the operational-risk management tools set out in the BCBS d292 "Principles for the Sound Management of Operational Risk". Business units score inherent risk (likelihood × impact before controls), assess design and operating effectiveness of mitigating controls, and derive residual risk against appetite, raising action plans for unacceptable gaps. ORX RCSA practice benchmarks standardise rating scales and refresh cadence. Outputs link to Key Risk Indicator Monitoring and scenario analysis.
