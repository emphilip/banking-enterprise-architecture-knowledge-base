# Digital Servicing

**Definition.** Digital Servicing is the Channels & Engagement capability that handles in-channel service requests, maintenance and case raising originated through digital touchpoints, aligning to the BIAN Servicing Issue and Session Dialogue service domains.
**Also known as:** In-App Servicing, Digital Service Requests.

## Relationships
- Digital Servicing is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Digital Servicing is derived from Digital Banking.
- Digital Servicing depends on the Digital Channel Platform capability.
- Digital Servicing depends on the CRM Platform capability.

## Details
Digital Servicing fronts BIAN Servicing Issue and Session Dialogue: a customer raises a request (address change, dispute, card freeze) in-app and the platform either auto-resolves it or opens a service case that carries omnichannel context to the contact centre. Continuity of the session record means a journey can start digitally and complete with an agent without re-keying. Servicing actions affecting payments or data sharing trigger PSD2 SCA step-up, and complaint-class requests must be logged for regulatory handling timelines (e.g. FCA DISP). The Session Dialogue domain holds the conversational state that links digital and assisted channels.
