# Servicing Resource Management

**Definition.** Servicing Resource Management is the Channels & Engagement capability that assigns agents to servicing positions and tracks availability and utilisation across sessions, applying contact-centre workforce engagement management (WEM) practice.
**Also known as:** Agent Scheduling, Position Management.

## Relationships
- Servicing Resource Management is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Servicing Resource Management is derived from Contact Center.
- Servicing Resource Management depends on the Contact Center Platform capability.
- Servicing Resource Management depends on the Workforce Engagement Management capability.

## Details
Servicing Resource Management applies contact-centre workforce engagement management (WEM): it forecasts contact volume, builds schedules and shrinkage-adjusted rosters, and binds agents to positions with the right skills and entitlements. Workforce management (Erlang-based staffing, intraday re-forecasting) targets service-level and occupancy goals while honouring labour rules. It supplies real-time agent state to Channel Interaction Routing so routing only offers contacts to available, qualified resources. Adherence, occupancy and schedule-fit analytics close the loop, and skill mappings keep regulated activities (complaints, collections) staffed only by certified agents.
