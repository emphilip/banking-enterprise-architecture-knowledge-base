# Customer Risk Rating

**Definition.** Customer Risk Rating scores each customer's money-laundering and sanctions risk to drive a risk-based approach to diligence and monitoring per FATF Recommendation 1 and Wolfsberg Group risk-assessment guidance.
**Also known as:** AML Risk Scoring, Customer Risk Profiling.

## Relationships
- Customer Risk Rating is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Customer Risk Rating is derived from KYC Management.
- Customer Risk Rating depends on the Analytics Platform capability.
- Customer Risk Rating depends on the Transaction Monitoring Platform capability.

## Details
FATF Recommendation 1 establishes the risk-based approach: institutions must identify, assess and understand their ML/TF risks and apply measures proportionate to those risks. Customer risk ratings combine factors such as customer type (PEP, legal-entity complexity), product and channel risk, and geographic exposure to high-risk jurisdictions, producing a risk tier that determines whether standard CDD or EDD applies and how frequently KYC is refreshed. Ratings must be documented and defensible to regulators.
