# Correspondent Banking Due Diligence

**Definition.** Correspondent Banking Due Diligence assesses respondent institutions using the Wolfsberg Group CBDDQ/FCCQ and FATF Recommendation 13 controls for cross-border correspondent relationships.
**Also known as:** CBDDQ Management, Respondent Bank Diligence.

## Relationships
- Correspondent Banking Due Diligence is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Correspondent Banking Due Diligence is derived from KYC Management.
- Correspondent Banking Due Diligence depends on the Document Management capability.
- Correspondent Banking Due Diligence depends on the KYC Onboarding Platform capability.

## Details
FATF Recommendation 13 requires correspondent banks to gather information on the respondent to understand its business and reputation, assess its AML/CFT controls, obtain senior-management approval before establishing relationships, and document responsibilities. It prohibits relationships with shell banks and payable-through-account arrangements lacking proper CDD. The Wolfsberg Correspondent Banking Due Diligence Questionnaire (CBDDQ) is the industry-standard instrument for collecting this information from respondents.
