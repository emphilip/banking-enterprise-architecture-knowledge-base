# Suitability Assessment

**Definition.** Suitability Assessment evaluates whether products and advice fit a client's objectives, knowledge, experience, and risk tolerance and produces suitability records; it is grounded in MiFID II suitability requirements and the BIAN Customer Investment Profile service domain.
**Also known as:** Suitability & Appropriateness, Investor Profiling.

## Relationships
- Suitability Assessment is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Suitability Assessment is derived from Investment Advisory.
- Suitability Assessment depends on the Investment Advisory Platform capability.
- Suitability Assessment depends on the CRM Platform capability.

## Details
MiFID II and the ESMA suitability guidelines require firms giving investment advice to collect information on the client's knowledge and experience, financial situation (including ability to bear losses), and investment objectives (including risk tolerance), and to recommend only suitable products. Suitability Assessment captures and scores this profile against the BIAN Customer Investment Profile service domain and blocks unsuitable recommendations.
