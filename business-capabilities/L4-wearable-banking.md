# Wearable Banking

**Definition.** Wearable Banking is the Channels & Engagement capability that delivers glanceable balances, alerts and payments on smartwatches and wearables as an extension of the digital channel estate, beyond BIAN's named domains.
**Also known as:** Smartwatch Banking, Wearable Channel.

## Relationships
- Wearable Banking is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Wearable Banking is derived from Digital Engagement & Personalisation.
- Wearable Banking depends on the Digital Channel Platform capability.

## Details
Wearable Banking extends the digital channel estate to watchOS and Wear OS devices, optimising for glanceable balances, push alerts and tap-to-pay rather than full transactional journeys. Payments run through the platform wallets (Apple Pay, Google Wallet) using network tokenisation and device biometrics (wrist-detection plus passcode) to satisfy PSD2 SCA via possession-plus-inherence factors. The constrained screen and shared-glance context shape what data is surfaced, and accessibility considers haptics and voice for the small form factor. It reuses the digital channel security model and notification pipeline rather than a separate stack.
