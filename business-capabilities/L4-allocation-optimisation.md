# Allocation Optimisation

**Definition.** Allocation Optimisation runs mean-variance and scenario optimisation to derive efficient target weights under constraints; it supports the BIAN Investment Portfolio Planning service domain.
**Also known as:** Mean-Variance Optimisation, Efficient Frontier.

## Relationships
- Allocation Optimisation is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Allocation Optimisation is derived from Asset Allocation.
- Allocation Optimisation depends on the Portfolio Management System capability.
- Allocation Optimisation depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Per CFA Institute methodology, mean-variance optimisation maximises expected return for a given level of variance using capital market assumptions, tracing the efficient frontier of optimal portfolios. Allocation Optimisation solves this under real-world constraints — long-only, asset-class bounds, turnover and transaction-cost penalties — to produce the target weights that feed Portfolio Construction within the BIAN Investment Portfolio Planning service domain.
