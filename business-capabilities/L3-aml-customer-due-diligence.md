# AML Customer Due Diligence

**Definition.** AML Customer Due Diligence is the capability to identify and verify customers, understand the purpose and intended nature of the relationship and apply risk-based diligence per FATF Recommendation 10 and the FinCEN CDD Rule.
**Also known as:** CDD Management, AML CDD, Standard Due Diligence.

## Relationships
- AML Customer Due Diligence is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- AML Customer Due Diligence is derived from KYC Management.
- AML Customer Due Diligence depends on the Customer Identity capability.
- AML Customer Due Diligence depends on the KYC Onboarding Platform capability.

## Details
FATF Recommendation 10 prohibits anonymous accounts and requires CDD when establishing relations, for occasional transactions above the designated threshold, on suspicion of ML/TF, or when prior data is doubted. The FinCEN CDD Rule (effective 2018) made identifying beneficial owners a fifth pillar and requires ongoing monitoring to maintain and update customer information. CDD comprises the four core elements: identify and verify the customer, identify and verify beneficial owners (the 25% ownership prong), understand purpose and nature, and conduct ongoing monitoring.
