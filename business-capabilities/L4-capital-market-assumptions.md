# Capital Market Assumptions

**Definition.** Capital Market Assumptions produces forward-looking risk, return, and correlation estimates by asset class to drive allocation; it supports the BIAN Investment Portfolio Analysis service domain, following CFA Institute methodology.
**Also known as:** CMA Modelling, Return Forecasting.

## Relationships
- Capital Market Assumptions is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Capital Market Assumptions is derived from Asset Allocation.
- Capital Market Assumptions depends on the Portfolio Management System capability.
- Capital Market Assumptions depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Per CFA Institute methodology, capital market assumptions are the long-horizon expected returns, volatilities, and cross-asset correlations that are the primary inputs to strategic asset allocation and optimisation. Capital Market Assumptions builds these using methods such as the building-block approach (risk-free rate plus risk premia), historical estimation, and shrinkage, then publishes them to Allocation Optimisation and the BIAN Investment Portfolio Analysis service domain.
