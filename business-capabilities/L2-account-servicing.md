# Account Servicing

**Definition.** Account Servicing is the L2 business capability for the day-to-day administration of deposit accounts, including transaction posting, balance maintenance, statements, and standing instructions, aligned to the BIAN Current Account service domain.
**Also known as:** Account Administration.

## Relationships
- Account Servicing is defined in the Deposits & Accounts domain.
- Account Servicing is derived from Deposits.
- Account Servicing depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.
- Account Servicing depends on the Notification Services capability.

## Details
Account Servicing maps to BIAN Current Account, Savings Account, and Deposit Account service domains. Typical sub-functions include transaction posting and reversal, balance and hold management, standing orders, statement generation, and account maintenance. It underpins the Statement Generation business process. Core banking platforms perform the posting and ledger functions, while notification services deliver alerts and statements. Robotic process automation handles repetitive servicing tasks.
