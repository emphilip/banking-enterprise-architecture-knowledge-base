# Wholesale Funding Execution

**Definition.** Wholesale Funding Execution issues and rolls money-market, repo and debt instruments to raise term and short-term funding, aligning to the BIAN Funding service domain.
**Also known as:** Money Market Funding, Repo Funding, Debt Issuance Funding.

## Relationships
- Wholesale Funding Execution is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Wholesale Funding Execution is derived from Funding Management.
- Wholesale Funding Execution depends on the Treasury Management System capability.

## Details
Wholesale Funding Execution transacts the funding instruments treasury uses to plug the funding gap — interbank deposits, certificates of deposit, commercial paper, repo and senior/covered debt issuance — capturing the deals, settling them and booking the resulting positions. Instrument choice is steered by the Basel III NSFR (BCBS d295), which assigns lower available-stable-funding factors to short-dated wholesale liabilities, biasing execution toward longer-tenor and more stable funding to keep NSFR above 100%.
