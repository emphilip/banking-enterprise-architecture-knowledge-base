# Risk Reporting & Governance

**Definition.** Risk Reporting & Governance aggregates risk data and produces board, management and supervisory risk reports under sound governance and data-quality controls, aligned with the BCBS 239 principles for effective risk data aggregation and risk reporting.
**Also known as:** Risk Data Aggregation & Governance, Board Risk Reporting, BCBS 239 Compliance.

## Relationships
- Risk Reporting & Governance is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Reporting & Governance is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Risk Reporting & Governance depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.
- Risk Reporting & Governance depends on the Data Warehousing capability.

## Details
Risk Reporting & Governance delivers against the 14 BCBS 239 principles, grouped into governance and infrastructure, risk data aggregation capabilities (accuracy, completeness, timeliness, adaptability), risk reporting practices (clarity, frequency, distribution), and supervisory review. It enforces a single authoritative source for risk data with end-to-end lineage, the ability to aggregate exposures rapidly during stress, and reconciliation of risk and finance figures. Reporting cadence spans board risk dashboards, RAS utilisation, and regulatory returns.
