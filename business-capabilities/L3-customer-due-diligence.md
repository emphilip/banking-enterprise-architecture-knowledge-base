# Customer Due Diligence

**Definition.** Customer Due Diligence is the L3 business capability for assessing and documenting customer risk at onboarding and on an ongoing basis, including standard and enhanced checks on identity, beneficial ownership, and source of funds.
**Also known as:** CDD, EDD.

## Relationships
- Customer Due Diligence is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Customer Due Diligence is derived from Customer Acquisition.
- Customer Due Diligence depends on the Document Management capability.
- Customer Due Diligence is related to Identity Verification.

## Details
Customer Due Diligence supports BIAN Party Lifecycle Management and Regulatory Compliance interactions, and it feeds the KYC Verification business process. Typical sub-functions include risk rating, beneficial ownership identification, PEP and adverse media review, and ongoing periodic review (EDD for higher-risk parties). It collaborates closely with KYC Management and AML Monitoring capabilities in the Compliance domain. Document management and intelligent document processing accelerate evidence capture.
