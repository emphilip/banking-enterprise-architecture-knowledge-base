# Regulatory Examination Management

**Definition.** Regulatory Examination Management coordinates regulator interactions, information requests and remediation of findings, aligning with the BIAN Regulatory Compliance and Regulatory Reporting service domains.
**Also known as:** Exam Management, Regulator Relations.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Examination Management is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Regulatory Examination Management is derived from Regulatory Compliance.
- Regulatory Examination Management depends on the Document Management capability.
- Regulatory Examination Management depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
US examiners apply the FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual procedures, scoping the exam to the institution's risk profile and testing each program pillar; deficiencies can result in matters requiring attention (MRAs), consent orders or civil money penalties. This capability manages document request lists, exam logistics, responses to findings, and the tracking of remediation commitments to closure. It also handles ongoing supervisory dialogue with regulators such as the FCA on financial-crime systems and controls.
