# Concentration Risk Analysis

**Definition.** Concentration Risk Analysis identifies and quantifies single-name, sector and geographic concentrations in the credit portfolio against appetite, aligned with the Basel large exposures framework.
**Also known as:** Single-Name Concentration, Portfolio Concentration Analysis.

## Relationships
- Concentration Risk Analysis is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Concentration Risk Analysis is derived from Credit Exposure Management.
- Concentration Risk Analysis depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Concentration Risk Analysis depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Concentration Risk Analysis computes single-name, connected-group, sector, geographic and collateral concentrations and summary metrics such as the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index and name-concentration granularity adjustment. It tests exposures against the Basel Large Exposures Framework (BCBS d283) 25%-of-Tier-1 limit and internal sub-limits, identifies the largest exposures for board reporting, and quantifies the Pillar 2 capital add-on for residual concentration not captured under Pillar 1.
