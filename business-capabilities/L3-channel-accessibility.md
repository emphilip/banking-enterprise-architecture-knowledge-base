# Channel Accessibility

**Definition.** Channel Accessibility is the Channels & Engagement capability that ensures digital experiences meet inclusive-design and WCAG conformance so all customers can transact independently; it is an accessibility-practice extension beyond BIAN's core channel domains.
**Also known as:** Digital Accessibility, Inclusive Design.

## Relationships
- Channel Accessibility is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Channel Accessibility is derived from Digital Banking.
- Channel Accessibility depends on the Digital Channel Platform capability.

## Details
Channel Accessibility governs conformance to W3C WCAG 2.2 at Level AA (perceivable, operable, understandable, robust) across web and mobile, plus native platform guidelines (iOS VoiceOver, Android TalkBack). In the US it underpins ADA Title III obligations that courts apply to bank websites and mobile apps; in the EU the European Accessibility Act (effective 2025) makes accessible banking services mandatory. Practical controls cover screen-reader labelling, colour contrast, keyboard and switch operability, captioning, and accessible authentication that does not rely solely on a cognitive test. It is tested with automated scanners and assistive-technology user testing.
