# Tax-Loss Harvesting

**Definition.** Tax-Loss Harvesting identifies and realises losses to offset gains while preserving market exposure within wash-sale rules; it complements the BIAN Investment Portfolio Management service domain, reflecting CFA Institute tax-aware practice.
**Also known as:** Tax Optimisation (Investments), TLH.

## Relationships
- Tax-Loss Harvesting is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Tax-Loss Harvesting is derived from Portfolio Rebalancing Management.
- Tax-Loss Harvesting depends on the Portfolio Management System capability.
- Tax-Loss Harvesting depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Tax-Loss Harvesting sells positions trading below cost basis to realise capital losses that offset realised gains, then maintains exposure by buying a correlated but not substantially identical security. The binding constraint is the US IRS wash-sale rule, which disallows the loss if the same or a substantially identical security is repurchased within 30 days before or after the sale; the capability tracks tax lots and the 61-day window to keep harvested losses deductible.
