# Enterprise Risk Management

**Definition.** Enterprise Risk Management is the top-level business capability that identifies, measures, monitors, and mitigates credit, market, operational, and liquidity risks across the enterprise. Enterprise Risk Management aligns with the BIAN risk management service domains and Basel framework expectations.
**Also known as:** Enterprise Risk.

## Relationships
- Enterprise Risk Management is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Enterprise Risk Management depends on the Data Warehousing capability.
- Enterprise Risk Management is related to Credit Risk Management.

## Details
Enterprise Risk Management establishes the risk appetite framework, three-lines-of-defense governance, aggregated risk reporting, and stress testing across risk types. Enterprise Risk Management consumes data from core, market, and transaction systems via enterprise data warehouses and analytics platforms, increasingly applying machine learning for early-warning indicators and BCBS 239 risk data aggregation compliance.
