# Regulatory Capital Management

**Definition.** Regulatory Capital Management is the business capability that calculates, optimizes, and reports regulatory capital adequacy under Basel and local prudential frameworks. It aligns with the BIAN Regulatory and Legal Compliance and Finance service domains.
**Also known as:** Capital Management.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Capital Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Regulatory Capital Management is derived from Finance Management.
- Regulatory Capital Management depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.

## Details
Regulatory Capital Management computes risk-weighted assets, CET1, Tier 1 and total capital ratios, leverage ratio, and capital planning and ICAAP under Basel III and IV. It depends on regulatory reporting engines and aggregated risk and finance data, with stress testing and capital optimization informing dividend and balance-sheet decisions.
