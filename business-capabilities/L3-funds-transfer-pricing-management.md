# Funds Transfer Pricing Management

**Definition.** Funds Transfer Pricing Management allocates the cost and value of funds between business units and the central treasury book, internalising liquidity and interest-rate-risk costs as a core ALM discipline.
**Also known as:** FTP Management, Internal Funds Pricing Capability, Matched-Maturity FTP.

## Relationships
- Funds Transfer Pricing Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Funds Transfer Pricing Management is derived from Asset Liability Management.
- Funds Transfer Pricing Management depends on the Asset Liability Management Engine capability.

## Details
Funds Transfer Pricing Management charges asset-originating units for the funds they use and credits liability-gathering units for funds they raise, using matched-maturity transfer rates plus a liquidity premium so that interest-rate and funding-liquidity risk are transferred to treasury rather than left in the business lines. This isolates customer-relationship margin from rate/term mismatch, makes business-unit profitability comparable, and centralises structural rate risk in the banking book for ALCO management.
