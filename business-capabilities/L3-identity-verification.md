# Identity Verification

**Definition.** Identity Verification is the L3 business capability that confirms a party is who they claim to be, combining document checks, biometric matching, and authoritative data source validation as part of onboarding and account access controls.
**Also known as:** ID&V, Identity Proofing.

## Relationships
- Identity Verification is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Identity Verification is derived from Customer Acquisition.
- Identity Verification depends on the Customer Identity capability.
- Identity Verification is related to Customer Due Diligence.

## Details
Identity Verification aligns to BIAN's Customer Reference Data Management and Party Authentication service domains. Typical sub-functions include document authentication, biometric and liveness checks, knowledge-based verification, and validation against authoritative registries. It is a key step in the Customer Onboarding and KYC Verification business processes. Modern deployments use intelligent document processing and CIAM platforms to support remote, real-time proofing.
