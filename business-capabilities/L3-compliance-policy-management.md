# Compliance Policy Management

**Definition.** Compliance Policy Management authors, approves, distributes and attests compliance policies, standards and procedures, satisfying the BSA "policies, procedures and internal controls" pillar.
**Also known as:** Policy & Standards Management, Compliance Procedures Management.

## Relationships
- Compliance Policy Management is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Compliance Policy Management is derived from Regulatory Compliance.
- Compliance Policy Management depends on the Document Management capability.
- Compliance Policy Management depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
The BSA/AML program's five pillars are: a system of internal controls (policies and procedures), independent testing, a designated BSA officer, training, and risk-based CDD. Compliance Policy Management governs the first pillar: maintaining board-approved AML/sanctions policies, mapping them to obligations, version-controlling procedures, and capturing employee attestations. Policies must be approved by the board or a designated committee and kept current as the FinCEN CDD Rule and other obligations evolve.
