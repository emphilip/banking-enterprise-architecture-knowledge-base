# Hardship & Forbearance

**Definition.** Hardship & Forbearance is the capability that assesses borrower financial difficulty and arranges temporary relief such as payment holidays and forbearance plans, mapping to the BIAN Delinquent Account Handling and Consumer Loan service domains.
**Also known as:** Financial Hardship Handling, Forbearance Management.

## Relationships
- Hardship & Forbearance is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Hardship & Forbearance is derived from Collections & Recovery.
- Hardship & Forbearance depends on the Workflow Orchestration capability.
- Hardship & Forbearance depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
The BIAN Delinquent Account Handling service domain accommodates temporary repayment relief, which Hardship & Forbearance arranges and tracks. A concrete banking specific: UK firms must treat customers in financial difficulty fairly under FCA CONC 7 and the Consumer Duty, offering forbearance such as reduced or deferred payments rather than escalating, while clearly explaining any impact on the borrower's credit file.
