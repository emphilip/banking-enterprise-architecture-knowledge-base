# Capital Planning

**Definition.** Capital Planning forecasts capital supply and demand, sets internal capital targets and produces the bank's capital plan under the Basel III capital adequacy framework, distinct from the Risk-domain ICAAP assessment.
**Also known as:** Capital Forecasting, Capital Plan Management, Capital Strategy.

## Relationships
- Capital Planning is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Capital Planning is derived from Regulatory Capital Management.
- Capital Planning depends on the Analytics Platform capability.

## Details
Capital Planning projects CET1 and total-capital ratios over the planning horizon against the Basel III minimum plus the capital conservation buffer and any countercyclical/G-SIB buffers, ensuring planned dividends, buybacks and balance-sheet growth keep the bank above its target operating level and management buffer. It integrates RWA forecasts, earnings projections and stress results, and feeds supervisory capital-plan submissions such as the Federal Reserve's CCAR.
