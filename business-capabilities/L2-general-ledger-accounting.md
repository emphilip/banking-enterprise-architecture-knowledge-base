# General Ledger Accounting

**Definition.** General Ledger Accounting is the business capability that records, classifies, and reconciles all financial transactions into the bank's general ledger. It corresponds to the BIAN Financial Statements and Position Keeping service domains.
**Also known as:** GL Accounting.

## Relationships
- General Ledger Accounting is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- General Ledger Accounting is derived from Finance Management.
- General Ledger Accounting depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
General Ledger Accounting maintains the chart of accounts, sub-ledger postings, intercompany entries, multi-GAAP and multi-currency books, and period-end close and reconciliation. Modern finance platforms such as Workday Financials and Oracle Fusion ERP supersede legacy ERP, enabling continuous accounting, rules-based sub-ledger engines, and real-time balances.
