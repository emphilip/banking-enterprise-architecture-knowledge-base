# EVE Sensitivity Analysis

**Definition.** EVE Sensitivity Analysis measures the change in economic value of equity across the prescribed BCBS 368 interest-rate shock scenarios.
**Also known as:** Economic Value Of Equity Analysis, EVE Shock Analysis, EVE Measurement.

## Relationships
- EVE Sensitivity Analysis is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- EVE Sensitivity Analysis is derived from Interest Rate Risk In The Banking Book.
- EVE Sensitivity Analysis depends on the Asset Liability Management Engine capability.

## Details
EVE Sensitivity Analysis discounts all banking-book cash flows to present value and reprices them under the six standardised interest-rate shock scenarios defined in BCBS d368 (parallel up/down, steepener, flattener, short up, short down), reporting the largest decline in economic value of equity. The supervisory outlier test flags banks whose maximum EVE decline exceeds 15% of Tier 1 capital. The capability captures repricing, optionality and behavioural assumptions so the value-based IRRBB measure is complete and auditable.
