# Trade Affirmation

**Definition.** Trade Affirmation matches and affirms institutional trade details with counterparties by the market cut-off ahead of settlement; it supports the BIAN Trade Confirmation Matching service domain and the T+1 cycle.
**Also known as:** Affirmation & Confirmation, Trade Matching.

## Relationships
- Trade Affirmation is defined in the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Trade Affirmation is derived from Trade Settlement.
- Trade Affirmation depends on the Order Management System capability.
- Trade Affirmation depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
The BIAN Trade Confirmation Matching service domain reconciles trade economics between broker, investment manager, and custodian. Under the US T+1 cycle effective May 2024, trades must be allocated, confirmed, and affirmed by 9:00 p.m. ET on trade date, typically via DTCC's central matching (CTM) and TradeSuite. Trade Affirmation matches block and allocation details and resolves mismatches before settlement instruction is released.
