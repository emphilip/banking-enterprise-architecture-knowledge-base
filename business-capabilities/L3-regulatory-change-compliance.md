# Regulatory Change Compliance

**Definition.** Regulatory Change Compliance horizon-scans, interprets and operationalises new and amended financial-crime and conduct obligations, aligning with the BIAN Regulatory Compliance service domain's requirement-interpretation role.
**Also known as:** Reg Change Tracking, Regulatory Change Interpretation.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Change Compliance is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Regulatory Change Compliance is derived from Regulatory Compliance.
- Regulatory Change Compliance depends on the Data Governance capability.
- Regulatory Change Compliance depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
The capability tracks rule changes from authorities such as the FCA, FinCEN/FFIEC and the EU (e.g. the AMLD6 transposition and the new EU AML Authority/AMLR single rulebook), interprets their impact on the firm, and assigns owners and deadlines. The FFIEC BSA/AML manual expects an institution's program to keep policies and internal controls current as laws change. Outputs feed obligation mapping, policy updates and control changes so the compliance program stays aligned with the governing law.
