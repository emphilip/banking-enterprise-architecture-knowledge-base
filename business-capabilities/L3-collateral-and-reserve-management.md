# Collateral & Reserve Management

**Definition.** Collateral & Reserve Management administers treasury collateral pools, central-bank reserves and high-quality liquid-asset buffers, aligning to the BIAN Reservation Administration and Treasury service domains.
**Also known as:** Treasury Collateral Management, Reserve Management, Liquidity Collateral Management.

## Relationships
- Collateral & Reserve Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Collateral & Reserve Management is derived from Liquidity Management.
- Collateral & Reserve Management depends on the Treasury Management System capability.

## Details
Collateral & Reserve Management maintains the inventory of pledgeable securities, central-bank reserve balances and the HQLA buffer the bank holds to meet the Basel III Liquidity Coverage Ratio (BCBS d238). It tracks asset eligibility, haircuts and encumbrance, pledges collateral to central-bank facilities, CCPs and repo counterparties, and manages margin calls so that unencumbered HQLA remains sufficient to cover stressed 30-day net outflows.
