# Relationship Management

**Definition.** Relationship Management is the L2 business capability for nurturing and growing customer relationships through needs analysis, next-best-action, and coordinated engagement, aligned to the BIAN Customer Relationship Management service domain.
**Also known as:** RM, Client Relationship.

## Relationships
- Relationship Management is defined in the Customer Management domain.
- Relationship Management is derived from Customer Management.
- Relationship Management depends on the CRM Platform capability.
- Relationship Management is related to Customer Segmentation.

## Details
Relationship Management maps to BIAN Customer Relationship Management and Customer Behavior Insights service domains. Typical sub-functions include relationship planning, opportunity management, needs assessment, and cross-sell and retention activities. It is commonly used for relationship-managed segments such as commercial, corporate, and private banking clients. CRM platforms realize this capability and increasingly embed generative AI for relationship insights.
