# SAR Management

**Definition.** SAR Management prepares, decisions, files and tracks Suspicious Activity Reports (STRs) and maintains confidentiality and timeliness per FATF Recommendations 20/21 and FinCEN SAR rules.
**Also known as:** STR Management, Suspicious Activity Reporting Management.

## Relationships
- SAR Management is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- SAR Management is derived from AML Monitoring.
- SAR Management depends on the Document Management capability.
- SAR Management depends on the Regulatory Reporting Engine capability.

## Details
FinCEN rules require a SAR to be filed within 30 calendar days of initial detection of facts that may constitute a basis for filing (extendable to 60 days if no suspect is identified), with a $5,000 transaction threshold for suspicious activity at banks. FATF R.20 requires prompt reporting to the FIU and R.21 protects reporters from liability and prohibits tipping off the customer. SAR Management governs narrative quality, the filing record, and continuing-activity reviews every 90 days where suspicious conduct persists.
