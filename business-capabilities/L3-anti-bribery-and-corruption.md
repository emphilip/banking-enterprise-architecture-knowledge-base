# Anti-Bribery & Corruption

**Definition.** Anti-Bribery & Corruption manages gifts, hospitality, conflicts and third-party diligence controls required by the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and the UK Bribery Act 2010.
**Also known as:** ABC Compliance, Bribery Prevention.

## Relationships
- Anti-Bribery & Corruption is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Anti-Bribery & Corruption is derived from Financial Crime Risk Compliance.
- Anti-Bribery & Corruption depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.
- Anti-Bribery & Corruption depends on the Document Management capability.

## Details
The UK Bribery Act 2010 creates a corporate offence of failing to prevent bribery (section 7), with the only defence being "adequate procedures"; it covers bribery of foreign and domestic officials and commercial bribery, with no facilitation-payment exemption. The US FCPA prohibits bribery of foreign officials and imposes books-and-records and internal-accounting-controls provisions. This capability operates the gifts and hospitality register, conflicts of interest declarations, facilitation-payment prohibition, and third-party integrity controls.
