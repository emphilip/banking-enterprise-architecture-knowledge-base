# Journal Management

**Definition.** Journal Management captures, validates and posts accounting journal entries to the general ledger, aligning to the BIAN Financial Accounting and Position Keeping service domains and APQC PCF 9.0 "Manage Financial Resources" general accounting activities.
**Also known as:** Journal Entry Management, GL Posting Management, Manual Journals.

## Relationships
- Journal Management is defined in the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Journal Management is derived from General Ledger Accounting.
- Journal Management depends on the General Ledger Engine capability.

## Details
Journal Management governs both system-generated sub-ledger postings and manual journals, enforcing that every entry is balanced (debits equal credits) before it hits the ledger. APQC PCF 9.3 "Perform general accounting and reporting" frames the activity, while SOX/ICFR controls require documented preparer-reviewer segregation and an immutable audit trail of who posted what and when. Modern universal-journal designs (single line-item table for actuals, plan and consolidation) replace periodic batch posting with continuous accounting.
