# Obligation Mapping

**Definition.** Obligation Mapping links regulatory requirements to internal policies, controls and owners so that change impact is traceable, supporting the risk-based program expected by the FFIEC BSA/AML manual.
**Also known as:** Requirement Mapping, Control Mapping.

## Relationships
- Obligation Mapping is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Obligation Mapping is derived from Regulatory Change Compliance.
- Obligation Mapping depends on the Data Governance capability.
- Obligation Mapping depends on the Governance Risk & Compliance Platform capability.

## Details
A defensible compliance program must demonstrate, for each obligation (e.g. the FinCEN CDD Rule, OFAC screening duties, FATF-aligned reporting), which policy, control and owner satisfies it. Obligation Mapping decomposes laws and regulations into atomic requirements and links each to the controlling artifact, so that when Regulatory Change Compliance flags a change, the affected controls and owners are identified immediately. This requirements-to-controls matrix underpins examiner walkthroughs and gap analysis, evidencing complete coverage of applicable obligations.
