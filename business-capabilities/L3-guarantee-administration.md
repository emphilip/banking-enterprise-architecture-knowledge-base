# Guarantee Administration

**Definition.** Guarantee Administration is the capability that manages guarantees, sureties and credit enhancements supporting a facility, mapping to the BIAN Guarantee service domain.
**Also known as:** Surety Management, Guarantee Servicing.

## Relationships
- Guarantee Administration is defined in the Lending & Credit domain.
- Guarantee Administration is derived from Collateral Management.
- Guarantee Administration depends on the Document Management capability.
- Guarantee Administration depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.

## Details
The BIAN Guarantee service domain administers third-party guarantees and sureties that mitigate credit risk on a facility. A concrete banking specific: a small-business loan may be backed by an SBA guarantee or a personal/corporate guarantee, and the bank must track guarantee limits, expiry and call conditions, recognising the credit-risk mitigation when calculating regulatory capital under Basel.
