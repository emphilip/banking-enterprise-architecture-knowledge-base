# Alert Prioritisation Scoring

**Definition.** Alert Prioritisation Scoring ranks alerts by risk to focus investigative effort and reduce false positives, supporting a risk-based AML program consistent with Wolfsberg Group effectiveness guidance.
**Also known as:** Alert Risk Scoring, Alert Ranking.

## Relationships
- Alert Prioritisation Scoring is defined in the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Alert Prioritisation Scoring is derived from Alert Management.
- Alert Prioritisation Scoring depends on the Machine Learning Platform capability.
- Alert Prioritisation Scoring depends on the Transaction Monitoring Platform capability.

## Details
The Wolfsberg Group's statement on effectiveness urges firms to direct resources toward the alerts most likely to reveal genuine financial crime rather than treating all alerts equally. Prioritisation scoring applies supervised and unsupervised models to features such as customer risk rating, scenario severity, prior SAR history and network exposure, producing a ranked queue so investigators work the highest-risk alerts first. This materially reduces the false-positive burden while preserving the ability to detect and report suspicious activity within mandated timeframes.
