# Regulatory Capital Adequacy

**Definition.** Regulatory Capital Adequacy runs the ICAAP, sets economic and Pillar 1/Pillar 2 capital requirements against the risk profile, and demonstrates solvency to supervisors, grounded in the Basel III/IV framework and the ECB ICAAP guide.
**Also known as:** ICAAP, Economic Capital Management, Capital Adequacy Assessment.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Capital Adequacy is defined in the Risk Management domain.
- Regulatory Capital Adequacy is derived from Enterprise Risk Management.
- Regulatory Capital Adequacy depends on the Risk Analytics Engine capability.
- Regulatory Capital Adequacy depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.

## Details
Regulatory Capital Adequacy assesses adequacy against the Basel III minimum ratios — 4.5% CET1, 6% Tier 1 and 8% total capital RWA ratios plus the 2.5% capital conservation buffer and a 3% leverage ratio. The ECB ICAAP guide requires a normative perspective (meeting regulatory ratios over a multi-year horizon) and an economic perspective (covering all material risks with internal capital). Pillar 2 (SREP) capital add-ons cover risks not fully captured under Pillar 1. Outputs link to Stress Testing Management for forward-looking solvency.
