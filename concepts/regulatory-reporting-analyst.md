# Regulatory Reporting Analyst

**Definition.** Regulatory Reporting Analyst is the compliance role that prepares, validates and submits regulatory returns — such as the Currency Transaction Report and prudential returns — to regulators within statutory deadlines. Regulatory Reporting Analyst owns the data-aggregation, compilation, validation and submission steps of the filing flow.
**Also known as:** Reg Reporting Analyst, Compliance Reporting Analyst.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Reporting Analyst is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Regulatory Reporting Analyst mentions Regulatory Filing.
- Regulatory Reporting Analyst mentions Identify Filing Obligation.
- Regulatory Reporting Analyst mentions Submit Regulatory Return.

## Details
Regulatory Reporting Analyst identifies which return is due, the regulator and the deadline (for example a CTR for currency transactions above $10,000), aggregates the underlying data from source systems, and compiles the return against the prescribed template or taxonomy (e.g. FinCEN Form 112). The analyst applies validation rules and four-eyes review, then e-files within the statutory deadline (15 days for the CTR, with no extension), captures the acknowledgement and retains the record for five years for audit.
