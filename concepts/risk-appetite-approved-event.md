# Risk Appetite Approved Event

**Definition.** Risk Appetite Approved Event is the business event signalling that the board or risk committee has formally approved the Risk Appetite Statement and its limits, authorising them to be embedded into business plans and monitoring.

**Also known as:** RAS Approved Event, Appetite Set Event.

## Relationships
- Risk Appetite Approved Event is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Appetite Approved Event causes Approve Risk Appetite.
- Risk Appetite Approved Event mentions Risk Appetite Statement.

## Details
Risk Appetite Approved Event is emitted at the end of the Risk Appetite Setting cycle once the board or its risk committee minutes formal approval of the Risk Appetite Statement and the cascaded limits and tolerances. The event marks the transition from draft to authoritative: it gates the embedding of approved limits into business plans, limit systems and ongoing monitoring, and establishes the appetite baseline against which exposures are later compared. Under the FSB Principles for an Effective Risk Appetite Framework, board sign-off is the control that gives the statement its authority, so this event carries governance significance and is typically logged with the approving body, version and effective date.
