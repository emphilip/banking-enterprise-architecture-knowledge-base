# FTP Curve

**Definition.** FTP Curve is the matched-maturity transfer-pricing curve, with liquidity-premium add-ons by tenor, used to charge and credit internal transfer rates to assets and liabilities. FTP Curve is the pricing backbone of funds transfer pricing in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Transfer Pricing Curve, Funding Curve.

## Details
FTP Curve specifies, by tenor, the internal transfer rate the bank charges asset originators for funds and credits liability gatherers for funding. It is built on a matched-maturity basis from market funding curves (senior unsecured debt yields or swap-plus-spread) and layered with a term liquidity premium and contingency-liquidity add-ons, with behavioural assumptions assigning effective tenors to non-maturity deposits and prepaying assets. The curve is approved by ALCO with a version and effective date, applied to assign FTP charges and credits, and used to attribute net interest margin between customer spread and funding and liquidity cost.
