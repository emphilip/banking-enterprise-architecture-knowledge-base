# Trial Balance

**Definition.** Trial Balance is the period-end listing of the ending balances of every general-ledger account, where total debits equal total credits, forming the basis for consolidation and financial statements. Trial Balance is a core record-to-report artifact in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Adjusted Trial Balance, TB.

## Relationships
- Trial Balance is related to the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Trial Balance mentions Run Consolidation.
- Trial Balance mentions Ledger Close & Reconciliation.

## Details
Trial Balance lists each general-ledger account with its closing debit or credit balance, summing to equal total debits and credits as an arithmetic proof of double-entry integrity. An unadjusted trial balance is taken after subledger close; the adjusted Trial Balance reflects period-end adjusting and accrual journals and cleared reconciliation breaks. It is organised by the chart of accounts (assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses) and per legal entity, then aggregated with elimination adjustments during consolidation to produce the consolidated position from which the primary financial statements are drawn.
