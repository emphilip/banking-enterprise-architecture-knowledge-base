# AML Compliance Officer

**Definition.** AML Compliance Officer is the first/second-line compliance role that performs customer due diligence and enhanced due diligence at onboarding, identifies beneficial owners and control persons, and assigns the customer risk rating under the bank's BSA/AML program. AML Compliance Officer owns the diligence and accept/EDD/reject decision in the onboarding flow.
**Also known as:** BSA Officer, CDD Officer, Onboarding AML Officer.

## Relationships
- AML Compliance Officer is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- AML Compliance Officer mentions Customer Due Diligence Onboarding.
- AML Compliance Officer mentions Rate Customer Risk.

## Details
AML Compliance Officer captures the Customer Identification Program (CIP) data set, identifies and verifies ultimate beneficial owners at the 25% ownership threshold and identifies control persons, then screens parties against sanctions, PEP and adverse-media lists. The officer synthesises screening, geography, product and behavioural risk into a customer risk score, triggers enhanced due diligence (source of wealth and source of funds) for high-risk or PEP customers, and records the accept/reject/conditional decision under four-eyes approval per FATF Recommendations 10 and 12.
