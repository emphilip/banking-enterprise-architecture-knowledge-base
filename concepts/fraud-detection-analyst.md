# Fraud Detection Analyst

**Definition.** Fraud Detection Analyst is the role operating real-time fraud detection, scoring transaction and event streams with rules and machine-learning models, tuning thresholds, and generating and routing alerts to investigation.

**Also known as:** Fraud Monitoring Analyst, Detection Analyst.

## Relationships
- Fraud Detection Analyst is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Fraud Detection Analyst mentions Fraud Detection.
- Fraud Detection Analyst mentions Screen Transaction Risk.

## Details
Fraud Detection Analyst sits at the front of the fraud operations chain. Fraud Detection Analyst ingests normalized real-time transaction and event feeds, applies rule sets and ML scoring to assess risk in real time, and raises a Fraud Alert when scores breach thresholds, deduplicating noise to control false positives. At the decision point the role may auto-block or hold high-risk activity or allow it to proceed, then prioritizes and routes the alert to the appropriate investigation queue. Fraud Detection Analyst monitors model performance, governs rules and thresholds, and balances customer friction against detection effectiveness, handing confirmed-suspect cases to investigators downstream.
