# Stress Testing Lead

**Definition.** Stress Testing Lead is the role that owns scenario design, model execution and capital and loss projection for the bank's stress-testing programme and the resulting capital plan submission to regulators.

**Also known as:** Stress Test Manager, Capital Stress Lead.

## Relationships
- Stress Testing Lead is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Stress Testing Lead mentions Stress Testing.
- Stress Testing Lead mentions Design Stress Scenario.

## Details
Stress Testing Lead coordinates the forward-looking assessment of capital resilience under adverse conditions. Stress Testing Lead designs coherent baseline, adverse and severely-adverse scenarios from supervisory and internal macro assumptions, ensures the models used are validated under Fed SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12 (conceptual soundness, ongoing monitoring and outcomes analysis), and runs them over portfolio data to project losses, revenues and capital ratios across the planning horizon. Stress Testing Lead assesses projected capital against minima and buffers, identifies management actions, obtains board sign-off of the capital plan, and submits results under CCAR/DFAST (or EBA in Europe). The role operates in the second line and depends on independent model validation for credibility.
