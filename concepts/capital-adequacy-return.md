# Capital Adequacy Return

**Definition.** Capital Adequacy Return is the prudential regulatory return (COREP / FINREP, FR Y-9C / FR Y-14) reporting risk-weighted assets, own funds and capital ratios to the supervisor. Capital Adequacy Return is the principal output of the prudential reporting cycle in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Prudential Return, COREP Return.

## Relationships
- Capital Adequacy Return is related to the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Capital Adequacy Return mentions Assemble Capital Return.
- Capital Adequacy Return mentions Return Assembly & Submission.

## Details
Capital Adequacy Return reports the components of regulatory capital and capital adequacy: Common Equity Tier 1, Additional Tier 1 and Tier 2 own funds; risk-weighted assets for credit, market, operational and counterparty-credit risk; the leverage ratio exposure measure; and the resulting CET1, Tier 1, total capital and leverage ratios against minimum and buffer requirements under Basel III / CRR. It is structured as templated schedules to the supervisor DPM / XBRL taxonomy (COREP own funds and FINREP financial reporting in the EU; FR Y-9C and FR Y-14 schedules in the US), validated against taxonomy rules and submitted with board attestation under CCAR.
