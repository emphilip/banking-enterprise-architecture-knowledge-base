# Risk Reporting Manager

**Definition.** Risk Reporting Manager is the role that owns risk data aggregation, limit monitoring and the production of board, committee and regulatory risk reports measuring the bank's profile against appetite under BCBS 239.

**Also known as:** Risk MI Manager, RDARR Lead.

## Relationships
- Risk Reporting Manager is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Reporting Manager mentions Risk Reporting.
- Risk Reporting Manager mentions Aggregate Risk Exposure.

## Details
Risk Reporting Manager runs the reporting cycle that aggregates risk data across systems and risk types, reconciles it to finance and source systems, and validates accuracy, completeness and lineage in line with BCBS 239 principles. Risk Reporting Manager compares exposures to approved limits and appetite, detects breaches and escalates them through governance, then compiles the Risk Report covering all material risk types and the profile versus appetite, and distributes it to the board and committees alongside regulatory returns. The role is accountable for timeliness, comprehensiveness and the data-quality controls (adaptability, reconciliation to the general ledger) that make the reported risk picture reliable.
