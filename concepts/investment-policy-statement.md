# Investment Policy Statement

**Definition.** Investment Policy Statement is the governing document that codifies a client's investment objectives, target asset allocation, constraints and rebalancing rules, and serves as the agreed mandate that portfolios are managed against in the wealth flow.

**Also known as:** IPS, Investment Mandate Statement.

## Relationships

- Investment Policy Statement mentions Financial Planning.
- Investment Policy Statement mentions Draft Investment Policy Statement.

## Details
Investment Policy Statement specifies the client's return objectives and risk tolerance, the time horizon and liquidity needs, the strategic target asset allocation with permitted ranges per asset class, eligible and excluded instruments, and any tax, ESG or concentration constraints. Investment Policy Statement also defines the benchmark, the rebalancing policy and tolerance bands, reporting frequency, and the discretion granted to the manager. Investment Policy Statement is drafted from the suitability report and target allocation, presented to and signed by the client, and then used as the reference baseline: drift detection measures holdings against its targets, pre-trade compliance checks orders against its limits, and performance reporting compares returns to its benchmark.
