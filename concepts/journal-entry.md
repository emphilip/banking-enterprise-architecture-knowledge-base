# Journal Entry

**Definition.** Journal Entry is the accounting record that posts debits and credits to the general ledger, including period-end adjusting, accrual and intercompany elimination journals. Journal Entry is the fundamental posting artifact of the record-to-report cycle in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Adjusting Journal, GL Journal.

## Relationships
- Journal Entry is related to the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Journal Entry mentions Post Closing Journals.
- Journal Entry mentions Period-End Adjustments.

## Details
Journal Entry records balanced debit and credit lines against general-ledger accounts, with an effective date, accounting period, source reference, narrative and supporting documentation. Types include recurring journals, manual adjusting journals (accruals, prepaids, depreciation, reclassifications), reversing journals and intercompany elimination journals. Each entry is subject to maker-checker approval and segregation-of-duties controls before posting, and feeds the trial balance. The journal audit trail evidences who raised, reviewed and approved a posting, supporting SOX / ICFR control over financial reporting.
