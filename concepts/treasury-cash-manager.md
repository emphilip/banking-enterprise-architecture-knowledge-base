# Treasury Cash Manager

**Definition.** Treasury Cash Manager is the treasury role responsible for positioning, forecasting and funding cash across accounts and currencies during cash management. Treasury Cash Manager decides and executes sweeps and funding transfers to keep balances at target.

**Also known as:** Cash Manager, Liquidity Manager.

## Relationships
- Treasury Cash Manager is related to the Payments domain.
- Treasury Cash Manager mentions Cash Management.
- Treasury Cash Manager mentions Determine Cash Position.
- Treasury Cash Manager mentions Forecast Cash Flow.
- Treasury Cash Manager mentions Execute Funding Transfer.

## Details
Treasury Cash Manager aggregates balances, computes the consolidated cash position, projects future inflows and outflows, and decides funding actions such as concentration, investment of surplus or borrowing to cover shortfalls. The role then releases sweep and funding payment instructions and monitors intraday liquidity against limits.
