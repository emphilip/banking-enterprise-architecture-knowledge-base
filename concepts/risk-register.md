# Risk Register

**Definition.** Risk Register is the master log of identified risks recording category, inherent and residual scores, owner, treatment and review dates, used to manage the risk-assessment lifecycle.

**Also known as:** Risk Log, Risk Inventory.

## Relationships
- Risk Register is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Register mentions Register Risk Item.
- Risk Register mentions Risk Identification & Assessment.

## Details
Risk Register records, for each risk: a unique identifier and description, the risk category from the taxonomy, the inherent risk score (likelihood by impact before controls), the controls and their assessed effectiveness, the resulting residual risk score, the comparison against appetite, the chosen treatment (accept, mitigate, transfer or avoid), the accountable owner, target dates and the next review date. Risk Register is the authoritative inventory feeding risk reporting and capital processes, and supports COSO ERM by making identification, assessment and remediation traceable. Good practice requires mandatory fields, version control, and disciplined refresh so that out-of-appetite residual risks are visibly escalated and remediation is tracked to closure rather than left static.
