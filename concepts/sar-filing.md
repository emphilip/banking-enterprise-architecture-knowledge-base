# SAR Filing

**Definition.** SAR Filing is the Suspicious Activity Report (e.g. FinCEN Form 111) filed with the financial intelligence unit documenting suspected money laundering or other financial crime. SAR Filing is the regulated output of the suspicious-activity reporting flow, signed off by the MLRO.
**Also known as:** Suspicious Activity Report, FinCEN Form 111.

## Relationships
- SAR Filing is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- SAR Filing mentions Lodge SAR Filing.
- SAR Filing mentions Suspicious Activity Reporting.

## Details
SAR Filing contains the subject information (filer, financial institution, and one or more subjects with identifiers), the suspicious-activity characterisation and category codes, the date range and dollar amounts, the involved accounts and instruments, and a free-text narrative describing the who/what/when/where/why and how of the suspected activity. It is filed via BSA E-Filing within 30 days of detection (up to 60 days where no subject is identified). SAR Filing is subject to strict tipping-off prohibitions and a five-year retention requirement, and continuing activity is reported in follow-up SARs.
