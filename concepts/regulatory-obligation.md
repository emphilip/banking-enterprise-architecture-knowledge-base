# Regulatory Obligation

**Definition.** Regulatory Obligation is a mapped internal requirement derived from an external regulation, linked to the policies, controls and owners responsible for compliance. Regulatory Obligation is the unit of the obligation library maintained through the regulatory change flow.
**Also known as:** Compliance Obligation, Regulatory Requirement.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Obligation is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Regulatory Obligation mentions Map Regulatory Obligation.
- Regulatory Obligation mentions Regulatory Change Management.

## Details
Regulatory Obligation records the source regulation and citation, the interpreted internal requirement, the affected business areas, the mapped policies and controls, the accountable owner, the implementation status and the attestation history. Obligations are created or amended when horizon scanning detects regulatory change, are assessed for impact and priority, and are traced through remediation to attestation. The obligation library provides the control mapping the bank relies on to demonstrate coverage and defensibility to regulators and to drive compliance monitoring and testing.
