# Reporting Deadline Reached Event

**Definition.** Reporting Deadline Reached Event is the business event marking the regulatory reference or remittance deadline that triggers prudential capital-return preparation and submission. Reporting Deadline Reached Event drives the regulatory reporting cycle in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Remittance Deadline Event, Reporting Cut-Off Event.

## Relationships
- Reporting Deadline Reached Event is related to the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Reporting Deadline Reached Event causes Source Regulatory Data.
- Reporting Deadline Reached Event mentions Regulatory Capital Reporting.

## Details
Reporting Deadline Reached Event fires when the supervisor reference date or remittance calendar deadline is reached for a prudential return such as COREP / FINREP or FR Y-9C / FR Y-14. The event sets the reporting reference date, fixes the applicable taxonomy version and signals the regulatory reporting team to source and reconcile finance and risk data feeds. Reporting Deadline Reached Event is the trigger that starts the Regulatory Capital Reporting process at its data-sourcing step, ahead of RWA and own-funds calculation, return assembly, taxonomy validation, attestation and submission.
