# Reg E Opt-In Record

**Definition.** Reg E Opt-In Record is the record of a consumer's affirmative opt-in to overdraft coverage of one-time debit-card and ATM transactions under Regulation E. Reg E Opt-In Record governs whether such items may be paid into overdraft in account servicing.

**Also known as:** Overdraft Opt-In Record, Reg E Consent.

## Relationships
- Reg E Opt-In Record is related to the Deposits & Accounts domain.
- Reg E Opt-In Record mentions Check Overdraft Opt-In.
- Reg E Opt-In Record mentions Overdraft Decisioning.

## Details
Reg E Opt-In Record evidences the consumer's affirmative consent, given after a segregated opt-in disclosure, to have one-time debit-card and ATM overdrafts paid for a fee. Without Reg E Opt-In Record those items must be declined at no charge rather than paid into overdraft. Reg E Opt-In Record is checked during overdraft decisioning, while checks and recurring ACH debits fall outside the opt-in requirement. The consumer may revoke Reg E Opt-In Record at any time.
