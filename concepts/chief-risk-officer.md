# Chief Risk Officer

**Definition.** Chief Risk Officer is the executive accountable for the enterprise risk framework, the bank's risk appetite and independent second-line oversight, owning the Risk Appetite Statement and presenting the aggregate risk picture to the board and risk committee.

**Also known as:** CRO, Head of Risk.

## Relationships
- Chief Risk Officer is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Chief Risk Officer mentions Risk Appetite Setting.
- Chief Risk Officer mentions Approve Risk Appetite.

## Details
Chief Risk Officer leads the second line of defence and is independent of the revenue-generating first line. Chief Risk Officer drives the Risk Appetite Framework: determining risk capacity, drafting qualitative appetite statements by risk type, cascading them into quantitative limits and tolerances, and securing board approval before the limits are embedded into business plans and monitoring. Chief Risk Officer also owns the firm-wide risk taxonomy, escalation of out-of-appetite breaches, and the relationship with prudential regulators. Under the Three Lines model and FSB Principles for an Effective Risk Appetite Framework, the role holds explicit accountability for aggregate risk-taking and reports directly to the board or its risk committee, separate from the CEO chain where required.
