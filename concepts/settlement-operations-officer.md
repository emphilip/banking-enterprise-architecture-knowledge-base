# Settlement Operations Officer

**Definition.** Settlement Operations Officer is the post-trade operations role that allocates executed fills across client accounts and settles securities trades on the T+1 cycle with custody reconciliation in the wealth flow.

**Also known as:** Securities Settlement Officer, Middle Office Operations.

## Relationships
- Settlement Operations Officer is related to the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Settlement Operations Officer mentions Trade Order Management.
- Settlement Operations Officer mentions Allocate Fills.

## Details
Settlement Operations Officer allocates block fills fairly across client accounts and sleeves, typically using average-price allocation, after the Trade Confirmation is issued. Settlement Operations Officer then settles the trade on the T+1 cycle, exchanging cash and securities and reconciling the trade against custody records. Settlement Operations Officer manages settlement instructions (SSIs), monitors for fails and drives fails management, and ensures trade-versus-custody reconciliation so internal positions match the custodian. Settlement Operations Officer applies allocation-fairness and four-eyes controls and escalates breaks for investigation before positions are finalised.
