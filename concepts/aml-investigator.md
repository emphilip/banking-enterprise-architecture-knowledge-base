# AML Investigator

**Definition.** AML Investigator is the financial-crime analyst who triages transaction-monitoring alerts, opens and works investigation cases, and analyses transactions and source of funds to determine whether reasonable grounds for suspicion exist. AML Investigator prepares the findings that the MLRO uses to decide on a SAR.
**Also known as:** AML Analyst, Transaction Monitoring Investigator.

## Relationships
- AML Investigator is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- AML Investigator mentions Suspicious Activity Reporting.
- AML Investigator mentions Triage AML Alert.

## Details
AML Investigator dedupes and prioritises alerts emitted by the transaction-monitoring platform, filters false positives against service-level agreements, and opens cases that assemble customer context and transaction history. The investigator maps activity to money-laundering typologies, performs source-of-funds checks, applies the reasonable-grounds standard, and escalates determinations of suspicion to the MLRO with documented evidence. Work is performed under tipping-off prohibitions and audit-trail controls.
