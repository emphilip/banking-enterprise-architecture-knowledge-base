# Fraud Investigator

**Definition.** Fraud Investigator is the role that triages routed alerts into cases, gathers evidence, decides the fraud disposition, recovers losses and files Suspicious Activity Reports where regulatory thresholds are met.

**Also known as:** Fraud Case Investigator, Financial Crime Investigator.

## Relationships
- Fraud Investigator is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Fraud Investigator mentions Fraud Investigation.
- Fraud Investigator mentions Investigate Fraud Case.

## Details
Fraud Investigator operates the case side of fraud operations. Fraud Investigator adjudicates a routed Fraud Alert to decide whether there is enough signal to open a case, opens a Fraud Case in the case-management system, and assembles account context such as several months of transaction history. The role investigates by gathering evidence and contacting the customer, then confirms or dismisses the fraud. On confirmed fraud, Fraud Investigator blocks or reverses funds and pursues recovery, files a SAR within the regulatory deadline where thresholds are met, and feeds confirmed outcomes back to tune detection rules and models. Investigation must meet evidence standards and a reasonable-investigation duty under applicable consumer-protection and AML rules.
