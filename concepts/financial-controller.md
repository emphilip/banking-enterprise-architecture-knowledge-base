# Financial Controller

**Definition.** Financial Controller is the accountable owner of the periodic financial close and of the statutory and consolidated financial statements, certifying the period close and the controls over financial reporting (ICFR). Financial Controller governs the record-to-report cycle in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Group Financial Controller, Head of Financial Control.

## Relationships
- Financial Controller is related to the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Financial Controller mentions Financial Close.
- Financial Controller mentions Certify Period Close.
- Financial Controller mentions Prepare Consolidated Statements.

## Details
Financial Controller leads the Financial Close process from period cut-off through to certification: overseeing subledger close, period-end adjusting journals, account reconciliation, intercompany elimination and consolidation. The role owns the production and review of the Consolidated Financial Statements under IFRS, enforces SOX / ICFR controls and segregation of duties over journal posting, evidences key controls and provides the controller sign-off that certifies the close. Financial Controller is the senior point of accountability between the general-ledger accounting team and the CFO for the integrity of reported financial results.
