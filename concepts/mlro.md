# MLRO

**Definition.** MLRO is the Money Laundering Reporting Officer, the named senior individual accountable for the bank's AML program, for deciding whether suspicious activity must be reported, and for filing suspicious activity reports with the financial intelligence unit and regulator. MLRO gives the final sign-off before a SAR is lodged.
**Also known as:** Money Laundering Reporting Officer, BSA/AML Compliance Officer.

## Relationships
- MLRO is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- MLRO mentions Suspicious Activity Reporting.
- MLRO mentions Lodge SAR Filing.

## Details
MLRO receives escalated suspicion determinations from AML Investigators, applies the reasonable-grounds standard, and decides whether to file or close. On a decision to file, MLRO signs off the SAR narrative for quality and lodges it via BSA E-Filing within the FinCEN 30-day deadline (up to 60 days where no subject is identified). MLRO confirms receipt, enforces tipping-off prohibitions and the five-year retention rule, and serves as the bank's primary liaison with the FIU and regulators. The role holds personal regulatory accountability for the AML program's effectiveness.
