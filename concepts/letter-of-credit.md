# Letter Of Credit

**Definition.** Letter Of Credit is a bank undertaking to pay the beneficiary against compliant documents under UCP 600 or ISP98 rules. Letter Of Credit is the core guarantee artifact produced and acted on in the trade-finance flow.

**Also known as:** Documentary Credit, LC.

## Relationships
- Letter Of Credit is related to the Lending & Credit domain.
- Letter Of Credit mentions Trade Finance Processing.
- Letter Of Credit mentions Documentary Credit Issuance.
- Letter Of Credit mentions Examine Trade Documents.

## Details
Letter Of Credit guarantees that the issuing bank will pay the beneficiary a stated amount provided the presented documents comply on their face with the credit terms, independent of the underlying sales contract. It specifies the applicant and beneficiary, amount and currency, expiry and place of presentation, required documents and any confirmation. Drafted and issued at Documentary Credit Issuance in the Trade Finance Processing flow under UCP 600 or ISP98, the credit is advised to the beneficiary, then drives the examination of presented documents within five banking days before honor or refusal.
