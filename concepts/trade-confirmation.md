# Trade Confirmation

**Definition.** Trade Confirmation is the document recording the economics of an executed trade, issued to and affirmed with the counterparty, that anchors allocation and settlement in the wealth flow.

**Also known as:** Trade Confirm, Contract Note.

## Relationships
- Trade Confirmation is related to the Wealth & Investments domain.
- Trade Confirmation mentions Trade Order Management.
- Trade Confirmation mentions Confirm Trade.

## Details
Trade Confirmation contains the trade date and settlement date, the security identifier, the side (buy/sell), the executed quantity and price, gross and net consideration, commissions, fees and taxes, the trade and settlement currency, the executing broker and counterparty, the settlement venue and place, and the account references. Trade Confirmation is issued when executed fills are confirmed and is affirmed/matched against the counterparty's record to detect economic discrepancies before settlement. Trade Confirmation drives the fair allocation of block fills across client accounts and the T+1 securities settlement, and it is reconciled trade-versus-custody so internal positions agree with the custodian. It serves as the legal record of trade terms.
