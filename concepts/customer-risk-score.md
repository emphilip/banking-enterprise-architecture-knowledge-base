# Customer Risk Score

**Definition.** Customer Risk Score is the risk-based score and tier assigned to a customer from screening, geography, product and behavioural factors during due diligence. Customer Risk Score drives the enhanced-due-diligence decision and the ongoing-monitoring cadence.
**Also known as:** CDD Risk Score, Customer Risk Tier.

## Relationships
- Customer Risk Score is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Customer Risk Score mentions Rate Customer Risk.
- Customer Risk Score mentions Customer Due Diligence Onboarding.

## Details
Customer Risk Score holds the composite numerical score, the assigned risk tier (e.g. low/medium/high), the contributing factor weights (jurisdiction risk, product and channel risk, occupation, PEP status, expected versus actual activity and adverse screening outcomes), the model version and the calibration date. A high tier or PEP flag triggers enhanced due diligence and senior-management approval, while the tier sets the review frequency for perpetual KYC and the segmentation thresholds used by transaction monitoring. The score is recalculated on trigger events and periodic review.
