# Stress Scenario

**Definition.** Stress Scenario is a defined macroeconomic and idiosyncratic scenario (baseline, adverse or severely adverse) used to project a bank's losses, revenues and capital under stressed conditions.

**Also known as:** Scenario Definition, Macro Stress Scenario.

## Relationships
- Stress Scenario is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Stress Scenario mentions Design Stress Scenario.
- Stress Scenario mentions Stress Testing.

## Details
Stress Scenario specifies a coherent set of forward-looking paths for macroeconomic and financial variables, typically including GDP growth, the unemployment rate, inflation, interest rates and yield curves, equity and house-price indices, exchange rates and credit spreads, projected over a multi-quarter horizon (nine quarters under CCAR). It distinguishes baseline, adverse and severely-adverse conditions and may add idiosyncratic shocks such as a counterparty default or an operational-loss event. Stress Scenario must be internally consistent and is sourced from supervisory scenarios (Federal Reserve, EBA) and internally designed assumptions; reverse stress testing instead starts from a failure outcome and derives the scenario that causes it. The scenario is the controlled input to projection models that produce stress-test results.
