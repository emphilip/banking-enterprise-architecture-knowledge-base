# CTR Filing

**Definition.** CTR Filing is the Currency Transaction Report (FinCEN Form 112) filed for currency transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single business day, submitted within 15 days. CTR Filing is a mandatory, threshold-driven output of the regulatory filing flow.
**Also known as:** Currency Transaction Report, FinCEN Form 112.

## Relationships
- CTR Filing is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- CTR Filing mentions Regulatory Filing.
- CTR Filing mentions Submit Regulatory Return.

## Details
CTR Filing reports cash-in and cash-out currency transactions aggregating above $10,000 in a single business day for one customer. It contains the person(s) and entities involved with full identification, the account numbers, the transaction date, the aggregated cash-in and cash-out amounts and the conducting financial institution. The report must be e-filed via BSA E-Filing within 15 calendar days of the transaction, with no extension permitted, and retained for five years. Multiple transactions are aggregated, and structuring to evade the threshold is itself reportable.
