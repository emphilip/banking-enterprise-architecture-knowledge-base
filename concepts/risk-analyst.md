# Risk Analyst

**Definition.** Risk Analyst is the practitioner who identifies, registers and assesses risks, scoring inherent and residual exposure and tracking treatment and remediation in the Risk Register across the risk-management lifecycle.

**Also known as:** Operational Risk Analyst, Risk Officer.

## Relationships
- Risk Analyst is related to the Risk Management domain.
- Risk Analyst mentions Risk Identification & Assessment.
- Risk Analyst mentions Identify Risk Event.

## Details
Risk Analyst typically sits in the first or second line and operates the COSO ERM identification-and-assessment cycle. Risk Analyst spots risks from business activities, loss events and emerging-risk scans, records them with category and owner, scores inherent risk on likelihood-by-impact scales, assesses control effectiveness to derive residual risk, and compares residual risk against the approved appetite. Where residual risk exceeds appetite, Risk Analyst recommends a treatment decision (accept, mitigate, transfer or avoid), assigns an owner and target date, and tracks remediation to closure. The role keeps the risk taxonomy complete, maintains data quality in the register, and feeds assessment outputs into risk reporting and capital processes.
