# Regulatory Change Manager

**Definition.** Regulatory Change Manager is the compliance role that owns end-to-end regulatory change: horizon-scanning emerging rules, mapping requirements to internal obligations, assessing impact, driving remediation and attesting that the change is embedded. Regulatory Change Manager keeps the obligation library current and defensible.
**Also known as:** Reg Change Manager, Compliance Change Lead.

## Relationships
- Regulatory Change Manager is related to the Compliance & Financial Crime domain.
- Regulatory Change Manager mentions Regulatory Change Management.
- Regulatory Change Manager mentions Map Regulatory Obligation.
- Regulatory Change Manager mentions Attest Change Compliance.

## Details
Regulatory Change Manager scans regulatory feeds and publications to detect emerging or finalised change early, maps each change to affected internal regulatory obligations, policies, controls and owners, and assesses business, operational, cost and timeline impact with a priority classification. The manager plans and tracks remediation, oversees control and policy implementation, and attests with full traceability that the change is embedded and operating — evidence the bank relies on to demonstrate compliance to regulators.
