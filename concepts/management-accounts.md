# Management Accounts

**Definition.** Management Accounts are the internal management-reporting pack of actuals versus budget and forecast, with variance commentary and segment KPIs, prepared for the CFO and board. Management Accounts are the principal decision-support artifact of the planning and analysis cycle in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Board MI Pack, Management Reporting Pack.

## Relationships
- Management Accounts is related to the Finance & Treasury domain.
- Management Accounts mentions Compile Management Accounts.
- Management Accounts mentions Variance & Insight Reporting.

## Details
Management Accounts present period and year-to-date actuals captured from the closed ledger against the approved budget and latest forecast, with quantified variances and management commentary explaining the drivers. The pack is structured by business segment, product and cost centre, carrying profit-and-loss, balance-sheet and cash views alongside KPIs and key operational metrics for performance steering. Unlike statutory statements, Management Accounts are internal, follow the management reporting hierarchy rather than IFRS presentation, and are assembled, reviewed for segment-KPI integrity and signed off by the CFO and board before distribution.
