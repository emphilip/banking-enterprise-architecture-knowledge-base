# Unclaimed Property Officer

**Definition.** Unclaimed Property Officer is the role that manages dormancy identification, dormancy notices and escheatment of unclaimed deposit balances to the state. Unclaimed Property Officer governs the abandoned-property lifecycle within deposit and account flows.

**Also known as:** Escheatment Officer, Dormancy Officer.

## Relationships
- Unclaimed Property Officer is related to the Deposits & Accounts domain.
- Unclaimed Property Officer mentions Dormancy Management.
- Unclaimed Property Officer mentions Issue Dormancy Notice.
- Unclaimed Property Officer mentions Escheat Dormant Account.

## Details
Unclaimed Property Officer reviews the dormancy report, attempts customer contact, and sends the Dormancy Notice before the statutory escheatment window. When funds remain unclaimed past the dormancy period, Unclaimed Property Officer reports and remits balances to the relevant state per escheatment and unclaimed-property rules, meeting state filing deadlines and record-retention obligations. Coordination with the Deposit Operations Clerk closes the underlying account once funds are remitted.
