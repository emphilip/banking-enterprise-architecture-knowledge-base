# Risk Appetite Statement

**Definition.** Risk Appetite Statement is the board-approved document stating the aggregate level and types of risk the bank is willing to accept to meet its objectives, expressed through qualitative statements and quantitative limits and tolerances.

**Also known as:** RAS, Risk Appetite Framework Statement.

## Relationships

- Risk Appetite Statement mentions Draft Appetite Statement.
- Risk Appetite Statement mentions Risk Appetite Setting.

## Details
Risk Appetite Statement sets, per risk type (credit, market, liquidity, operational, conduct, reputational), both qualitative appetite statements and quantitative measures such as limits, tolerances and triggers, anchored to the bank's risk capacity and strategy. Under the FSB Principles for an Effective Risk Appetite Framework, the statement covers risks that are hard to quantify (for example conduct and reputation), links to the capital and liquidity plans, and is cascaded into business-line and legal-entity limits. Risk Appetite Statement requires board approval, is reviewed at least annually, and provides the baseline against which exposures are measured, breaches escalated and the bank's risk profile reported. It defines who may take risk, how much, and where escalation is required.
