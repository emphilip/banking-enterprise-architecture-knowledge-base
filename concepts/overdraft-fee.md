# Overdraft Fee

**Definition.** Overdraft Fee is the charge assessed when an item is paid into overdraft, distinct from an NSF return fee charged when an item is returned unpaid. Overdraft Fee is the output charge of the overdraft-servicing flow.

**Also known as:** OD Fee, Insufficient Funds Fee.

## Relationships
- Overdraft Fee is related to the Deposits & Accounts domain.
- Overdraft Fee mentions Apply Overdraft Fee.
- Overdraft Fee mentions Overdraft Fee & Notice.

## Details
Overdraft Fee is applied per the disclosed fee schedule when the decision is to pay an item that exceeds available balance, whereas a returned item attracts an NSF fee instead. Overdraft Fee may be subject to per-day caps, de-minimis thresholds and sustained-overdraft rules, and assessment requires the disclosures and, for one-time debit and ATM items, a valid Reg E Opt-In Record. Overdraft Fee is reported to the customer in the outcome notice and on the next Bank Account Statement.
