# Consolidated Financial Statements

**Definition.** Consolidated Financial Statements are the group financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash-flow statement and statement of changes in equity) presenting the parent and its subsidiaries as a single economic entity after intercompany elimination. Consolidated Financial Statements are the primary external output of the close in the finance and treasury function.

**Also known as:** Group Accounts, Consolidated Accounts.

## Details
Consolidated Financial Statements aggregate the trial balances of all group entities, applying currency translation, intercompany eliminations and non-controlling-interest adjustments so the group is presented as one entity under IFRS 10 / IAS 27. The set comprises the consolidated statement of financial position, statement of profit or loss and other comprehensive income, statement of cash flows and statement of changes in equity, plus accompanying notes and disclosures. The statements are prepared from the consolidated position, reviewed against IFRS presentation and disclosure requirements, and certified by the controller before external publication.
