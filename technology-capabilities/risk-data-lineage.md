# Risk Data Lineage

**Definition.** Risk Data Lineage is the technology capability that captures end-to-end lineage of how risk metrics are sourced, transformed and aggregated from source systems to final reports and dashboards — the BCBS 239 complete-lineage traceability evidence.
**Also known as:** Data Lineage (Risk), Risk Lineage Tracing.

## Relationships
- Risk Data Lineage is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Risk Data Lineage is derived from Risk Data Aggregation.
- Risk Data Lineage depends on the Data Governance capability.
- Risk Data Lineage is related to Risk Data Warehouse.

## Details
Risk Data Lineage records column-level and transformation-level provenance so that every reported risk number can be traced back through aggregation logic and intermediate stores to its originating source systems. This satisfies the BCBS 239 expectation that banks evidence complete lineage and reconcile risk data to source, supporting impact analysis, control attestations and supervisory review, as delivered by lineage platforms such as Solidatus.
