# Deal Management

**Definition.** Deal Management is the technology capability that runs the capture, confirmation, settlement and accounting lifecycle for treasury financial instruments — money-market, FX, debt/investments and derivatives — with position keeping and counterparty/limit control.
**Also known as:** Treasury Deal Capture, Front-Office Dealing, Treasury Instrument Management, Trade & Position Management.

## Relationships
- Deal Management is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Deal Management is derived from Treasury Management System.
- Deal Management is related to Subledger Accounting.

## Details
Deal Management captures front-office trades across instrument types, validates them against counterparty and instrument limits, and drives the back-office lifecycle: confirmation matching, settlement instruction generation, fixings, coupon/maturity events and unwind. It maintains real-time positions and mark-to-market valuations and generates accounting entries that flow to Subledger Accounting, giving an auditable bridge from dealing to the books.
