# Liquidity Forecasting Engine

**Definition.** Liquidity Forecasting Engine is the technology capability that builds short- and medium-term cash-flow forecasts from actuals, scheduled flows, working-capital and AI-driven predictions, with variance analysis and scenario what-ifs to manage liquidity and funding.
**Also known as:** Cash Forecasting Engine, AI Cash Forecasting, Liquidity Planning Service.

## Relationships
- Liquidity Forecasting Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Liquidity Forecasting Engine is derived from Treasury Management System.
- Liquidity Forecasting Engine is related to Cash Positioning Service.
- Liquidity Forecasting Engine is related to Asset Liability Management Engine.

## Details
Liquidity Forecasting Engine blends deterministic inputs (AP/AR schedules, debt and investment maturities, payroll, tax) with machine-learning models trained on historical bank flows to project cash by entity, currency and horizon. Variance analysis compares forecast against realised positions to tune accuracy, and scenario what-ifs stress funding needs. Output informs concentration, borrowing and investment decisions and feeds longer-horizon balance-sheet planning in the Asset Liability Management Engine.
