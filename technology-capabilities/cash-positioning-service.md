# Cash Positioning Service

**Definition.** Cash Positioning Service is the technology capability that aggregates balances and transactions across all banks, accounts, entities and currencies to give a real-time global cash position and drive same-day funding and concentration decisions.
**Also known as:** Cash Visibility Service, Cash Position Engine, Global Cash Management.

## Relationships
- Cash Positioning Service is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Cash Positioning Service is derived from Treasury Management System.
- Cash Positioning Service is related to Bank Connectivity Hub.

## Details
Cash Positioning Service consumes prior-day and intraday bank statements (MT940/MT942, camt.052/.053) and internal transaction feeds to build a worksheet of opening balances, known flows and projected end-of-day positions by bank, account, currency and entity. Treasurers use it to identify surpluses and shortfalls, target concentration sweeps and decide same-day funding or investment, drawing connectivity from the Bank Connectivity Hub.
