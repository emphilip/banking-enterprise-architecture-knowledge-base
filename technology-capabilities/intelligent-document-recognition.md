# Intelligent Document Recognition

**Definition.** Intelligent Document Recognition applies optical character recognition and machine learning to classify documents and extract structured data fields during capture.
**Also known as:** Intelligent Document Recognition Service, OCR & Extraction Service, Document Field Extraction.

## Relationships
- Intelligent Document Recognition is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Intelligent Document Recognition is derived from Content Capture Service.
- Intelligent Document Recognition depends on Content Repository.

## Details
Intelligent Document Recognition extends basic capture with intelligent document processing: OCR converts images to text, then machine-learning models classify the document type and locate and extract key fields such as names, amounts, dates, and identifiers, even on unstructured or semi-structured layouts. Confidence scores route low-certainty extractions to human review, and the models improve as corrected examples accumulate. This removes manual keying from high-volume banking documents like loan packets, statements, and KYC evidence, populating metadata and downstream systems automatically. As a sub-capability of capture it feeds classified, data-rich documents into the content repository and onward content workflows.
