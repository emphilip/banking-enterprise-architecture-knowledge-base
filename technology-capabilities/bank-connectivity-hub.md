# Bank Connectivity Hub

**Definition.** Bank Connectivity Hub is the technology capability that provides standardised connectivity to banks, ERPs, payment and market systems (SWIFT, host-to-host, APIs, EBICS) with statement (MT940/camt) ingestion and treasury payment dispatch and tracking.
**Also known as:** Bank Connectivity Service, SWIFT Service Bureau, Treasury Connectivity Gateway, Multi-Bank Connectivity.

## Relationships
- Bank Connectivity Hub is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Bank Connectivity Hub is derived from Treasury Management System.
- Bank Connectivity Hub is related to Payment Orchestration.

## Details
Bank Connectivity Hub abstracts the many channels and formats a treasury must support — SWIFT FIN/FileAct via a service bureau, host-to-host SFTP, regional protocols such as EBICS, and modern bank APIs — into a single managed gateway. It normalises inbound statements (MT940/942, camt.05x) for cash positioning and dispatches outbound treasury payments (pain.001, MT101) with acknowledgement and status tracking, sparing the bank from maintaining per-bank integrations.
