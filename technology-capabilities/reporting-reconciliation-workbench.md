# Reporting Reconciliation Workbench

**Definition.** Reporting Reconciliation Workbench is the technology sub-capability that reconciles reported figures back to source ledgers and risk data, manages adjustments and provides an audit trail for sign-off of regulatory returns.
**Also known as:** Reconciliation Workbench, Reg Reconciliation Service, Figure Reconciliation, Reporting Sign-Off.

## Relationships
- Reporting Reconciliation Workbench is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Reporting Reconciliation Workbench is derived from Regulatory Reporting Engine.
- Reporting Reconciliation Workbench depends on Regulatory Report Calculation Engine.
- Reporting Reconciliation Workbench is related to Risk Data Lineage.

## Details
Reporting Reconciliation Workbench ties reported figures back to the general ledger and risk systems, evidencing that returns agree with the books and records. It manages manual adjustments and overlays with maker-checker control, captures justification, and holds the formal sign-off audit trail expected by supervisors. Drill-down through the calculation engine to granular source records, combined with lineage from the risk data lineage capability, supports BCBS 239 traceability and explains every movement between reporting periods.
