# General Ledger Engine

**Definition.** General Ledger Engine is the technology capability that records double-entry accounting postings, maintains the chart of accounts, and produces trial balances and financial statements for the bank.
**Also known as:** GL System.

## Relationships
- General Ledger Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- General Ledger Engine depends on the Core Banking Processing capability.
- General Ledger Engine mentions SAP ECC.
- General Ledger Engine mentions Workday Financials.
- General Ledger Engine mentions Oracle Fusion ERP.
- General Ledger Engine is related to Regulatory Reporting Engine.

## Details
General Ledger Engine consolidates sub-ledger postings from core, payments, and cards into the bank's official books and feeds financial and regulatory reporting. Legacy realization is typically SAP ECC running on premise, while modern cloud ERPs such as Workday Financials and Oracle Fusion ERP provide real-time, multi-entity ledgers with continuous close. It is the accounting backbone of the finance reference architecture.
