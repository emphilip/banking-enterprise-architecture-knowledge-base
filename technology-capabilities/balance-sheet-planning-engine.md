# Balance Sheet Planning Engine

**Definition.** Balance Sheet Planning Engine is the technology capability that projects and simulates the future balance sheet and earnings under business and rate scenarios — multi-year NII and EVE forecasting, new-business volume modelling and capital/liquidity what-if planning for ALCO.
**Also known as:** Balance Sheet Management Engine, NII Simulation Engine, ALM Scenario Engine.

## Relationships
- Balance Sheet Planning Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Balance Sheet Planning Engine is derived from Asset Liability Management Engine.
- Balance Sheet Planning Engine is related to Stress Testing Engine.
- Balance Sheet Planning Engine is related to Liquidity Forecasting Engine.

## Details
Balance Sheet Planning Engine rolls the current book forward, layering modelled new-business volumes, pricing and runoff to produce a multi-year projected balance sheet and income statement. It simulates NII and EVE across rate and macro scenarios, evaluates funding and capital/liquidity ratios under each, and supports what-if and optimisation analysis for ALCO. Scenarios are shared with the enterprise Stress Testing Engine so balance-sheet, capital and liquidity views stay consistent.
