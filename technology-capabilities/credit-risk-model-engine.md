# Credit Risk Model Engine

**Definition.** Credit Risk Model Engine is the technology capability that computes credit risk parameters and outcomes — PD/LGD/EAD, expected credit loss (ECL/IFRS 9 and CECL), credit portfolio loss distributions and counterparty credit exposure and PFE — for regulatory and economic capital.
**Also known as:** Credit Risk Engine, ECL Engine, Credit Portfolio Engine.

## Relationships
- Credit Risk Model Engine is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Credit Risk Model Engine is derived from Risk Analytics Engine.
- Credit Risk Model Engine depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.
- Credit Risk Model Engine is related to Economic Capital Engine.

## Details
Credit Risk Model Engine estimates the three Basel risk parameters — probability of default (PD), loss given default (LGD) and exposure at default (EAD) — and rolls them into expected credit loss under IFRS 9 staging and CECL lifetime-loss requirements. It generates credit portfolio loss distributions using factor/Monte Carlo simulation to derive unexpected loss, concentration and economic capital, as delivered by SAS Credit Risk Management and Moody's RiskFrontier.
