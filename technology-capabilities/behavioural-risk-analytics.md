# Behavioural Risk Analytics

**Definition.** Behavioural Risk Analytics is the machine-learning and anomaly-detection technology sub-capability that profiles customer and peer behaviour and scores deviations to surface money-laundering risk beyond static rules.
**Also known as:** AML Machine Learning, Anomaly Detection Service, Behavioural Profiling, Unsupervised Detection.

## Relationships
- Behavioural Risk Analytics is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Behavioural Risk Analytics is derived from Transaction Monitoring Platform.
- Behavioural Risk Analytics depends on Machine Learning Platform.
- Behavioural Risk Analytics is related to Behavioural Analytics.

## Details
Behavioural Risk Analytics adds supervised and unsupervised machine learning on top of rules-based detection, as positioned by Napier AI and SAS AML: it builds dynamic behavioural baselines per customer and peer group and flags anomalous deviations that fixed thresholds miss. Models run on the shared machine-learning platform with feature pipelines over transaction and profile data, and outputs feed alert scoring to cut false positives while catching novel typologies. Explainability and challenger-model comparison support model governance and regulatory scrutiny.
