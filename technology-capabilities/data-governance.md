# Data Governance

**Definition.** Data Governance is the technology capability that catalogs data assets, manages lineage, quality, ownership, and access policies, and enforces regulatory data controls such as BCBS 239 across the bank.
**Also known as:** Data Catalog, Data Governance and Catalog.

## Relationships
- Data Governance is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Data Governance is related to Master Data Management.
- Data Governance is related to Data Warehousing.
- Data Governance mentions Snowflake.

## Details
Data Governance provides the metadata, lineage, and policy layer that makes warehoused and streamed data trustworthy and auditable. It supports regulatory compliance by evidencing data provenance and quality for reporting. Modern catalogs are cloud-native and integrate with lakehouse and streaming platforms to automate classification, masking, and access control. It is foundational to the Data & Analytics reference architecture.
