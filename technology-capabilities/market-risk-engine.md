# Market Risk Engine

**Definition.** Market Risk Engine is the technology capability that computes market risk metrics — parametric, historical and Monte Carlo VaR, expected shortfall, sensitivities (Greeks), P&L explain and FRTB-SA/IMA capital — across asset classes with full or Taylor-based revaluation.
**Also known as:** Market Risk VaR Engine, VaR Engine, FRTB Engine.

## Relationships
- Market Risk Engine is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Market Risk Engine is derived from Risk Analytics Engine.
- Market Risk Engine depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.
- Market Risk Engine is related to Stress Testing Engine.

## Details
Market Risk Engine values trading-book positions across full revaluation and Taylor/sensitivity-based grids to produce one-day and ten-day VaR and expected shortfall at multiple confidence levels, with P&L attribution (PLA) tests and backtesting for IMA eligibility under FRTB. It computes the FRTB standardised approach (SA) sensitivities-based method as well as internal models approach (IMA) capital, as delivered by Murex MX.3 Risk, Numerix Oneview and Quantifi.
