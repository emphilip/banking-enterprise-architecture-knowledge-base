# Scheme Connectivity Gateway

**Definition.** Scheme Connectivity Gateway is the technology capability providing connectivity adapters and gateways that link the bank to clearing and settlement networks and rails such as SWIFT, Fedwire, CHIPS, FedNow, RTP, ACH, SEPA, TARGET2, CHAPS and Faster Payments.
**Also known as:** Rail Connectivity, Network Adapters, Scheme Connectivity.

## Relationships
- Scheme Connectivity Gateway is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Scheme Connectivity Gateway is derived from Payment Orchestration.
- Scheme Connectivity Gateway depends on Payment Message Transformer.
- Scheme Connectivity Gateway is related to Payment Routing Engine.

## Details
Scheme Connectivity Gateway maintains the certified adapters, protocol handlers and gateways that connect the payment hub to domestic and international clearing and settlement networks. It abstracts rail-specific session, security and message-exchange details, and is exemplified by the connectivity layers of Volante US Payments, Form3 and FIS Open Payment Framework.
