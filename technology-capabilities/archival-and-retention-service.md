# Archival & Retention Service

**Definition.** Archival & Retention Service preserves and disposes of content according to retention and legal-hold policies, governing the long-term lifecycle of stored information.
**Also known as:** Archival Service, Retention Management, Preservation Service.

## Relationships
- Archival & Retention Service is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Archival & Retention Service is derived from Document Management.
- Archival & Retention Service is related to Records Management Service.
- Archival & Retention Service is related to Content Repository.

## Details
Archival & Retention Service manages the final phases of the content lifecycle: it migrates inactive content to lower-cost preservation storage, retains items for the period mandated by regulation or policy, and then performs defensible disposal once retention expires. Legal holds suspend disposition for content subject to litigation or investigation, overriding the normal schedule. Write-once-read-many (WORM) and immutability controls satisfy evidential requirements such as SEC 17a-4. Operating on records and documents in the repository, the service complements records management, which declares and classifies records, by enforcing where and how long content physically persists and when it is destroyed.
