# Customer Authentication

**Definition.** Customer Authentication is the customer identity technology sub-capability providing adaptive, multi-factor and passwordless authentication that governs customer login and step-up across digital channels.
**Also known as:** Adaptive Authentication, MFA Service.

## Relationships
- Customer Authentication is defined in the Security & Identity domain.
- Customer Authentication is derived from Customer Identity.
- Customer Authentication depends on Threat Detection.
- Customer Authentication is related to Digital Channel Platform.

## Details
Customer Authentication verifies customers at login and high-risk events using multiple factors, risk signals and passwordless methods such as FIDO passkeys, applying step-up challenges when context warrants. It consumes device, behavioural and threat-intelligence signals to make adaptive decisions and is a core service of consumer identity and access management for banking channels.
