# Passwordless Authentication

**Definition.** Passwordless Authentication is the customer identity technology sub-capability providing a FIDO2 and passkey public-key credential service that enables phishing-resistant, password-free customer login and transaction approval.
**Also known as:** Passkey Service, FIDO2 Service.

## Relationships
- Passwordless Authentication is defined in the Security & Identity domain.
- Passwordless Authentication is derived from Customer Authentication.
- Passwordless Authentication is related to Threat Detection.

## Details
Passwordless Authentication issues and verifies FIDO2 public-key credentials bound to a device, letting customers authenticate with biometrics or a device PIN instead of a shared secret. Because credentials never leave the device and are origin-bound, it resists phishing and credential replay, and it underpins step-up and transaction approval as a specialised method within customer authentication.
