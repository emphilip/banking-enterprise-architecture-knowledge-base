# Computer Telephony Integration

**Definition.** Computer Telephony Integration is the technology sub-capability providing middleware that links telephony events to business applications, including call control, screen pop, click-to-dial and context passing to the desktop and CRM.
**Also known as:** CTI, Telephony Integration, Call Control Service.

## Relationships
- Computer Telephony Integration is defined in the Channels & Engagement domain.
- Computer Telephony Integration is derived from Contact Center Platform.
- Computer Telephony Integration is related to CRM Platform.

## Details
Computer Telephony Integration bridges the voice platform and business apps: it exposes call-control APIs (answer, transfer, conference, hold), publishes call events with attached data (ANI/DNIS, IVR-collected keys), enables click-to-dial, and pushes caller context to the agent desktop so a screen pop appears as the call connects. It carries customer identifiers into the CRM so interaction history and case context are pre-loaded. Genesys and Amazon Connect provide CTI adapters that embed soft-phone control directly in CRM and desktop apps.
