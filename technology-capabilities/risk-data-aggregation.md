# Risk Data Aggregation

**Definition.** Risk Data Aggregation is the BCBS 239-aligned technology capability that aggregates, reconciles and governs risk data across business lines into accurate, complete, timely and adaptable risk exposures, with end-to-end data lineage feeding risk computation and risk reporting.
**Also known as:** Risk Data Platform, BCBS 239 Platform, Risk Data Aggregation and Reporting.

## Details
Risk Data Aggregation implements the four BCBS 239 risk-data principles — accuracy and integrity, completeness, timeliness and adaptability — by sourcing positions, exposures, reference and market data into a reconciled risk repository and capturing complete data lineage from source systems to final reports. Legacy platforms such as Moody's RiskAuthority centralize Basel I/II/III credit, market, operational, concentration and liquidity risk data, while modern entity-resolution platforms like Quantexa unify fragmented internal and external data into a single contextual view. The capability provides the drill-downable, traceable exposures consumed by limits, capital and board risk reporting.
