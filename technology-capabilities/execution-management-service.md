# Execution Management Service

**Definition.** Execution Management Service is the trader-facing technology capability that consumes market data, manages execution strategies and algorithms, supports one-click and program trading, and monitors live orders and fills for best execution.
**Also known as:** EMS, Execution Workbench, Trade Execution Service.

## Relationships
- Execution Management Service is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Execution Management Service is derived from Order Management System.
- Execution Management Service depends on Order Routing Engine.
- Execution Management Service is related to Brokerage & Trading.

## Details
Execution Management Service is the trader workbench (EMS) that consumes real-time market data, manages algorithmic and program-trading strategies, supports list and one-click trading, and monitors working orders and fills in flight. It works with the Order Routing Engine over FIX to slice and route child orders and gather transaction-cost analysis (TCA) to evidence best execution. Integrated OMS/EMS platforms such as Bloomberg AIM and Charles River IMS, and multi-asset tools like Limina, realize the execution workspace across equities, fixed income and derivatives.
