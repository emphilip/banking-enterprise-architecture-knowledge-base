# Consolidation Engine

**Definition.** Consolidation Engine is the technology capability that performs group consolidation: data collection from entities, currency translation, intercompany elimination, ownership and minority-interest calculation and multi-GAAP/management views to produce consolidated financial statements.
**Also known as:** Group Consolidation Engine, Financial Consolidation Service, Consol Engine.

## Relationships
- Consolidation Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Consolidation Engine is derived from Financial Consolidation Platform.
- Consolidation Engine is related to Intercompany Accounting Service.
- Consolidation Engine is related to Period Close Manager.

## Details
Consolidation Engine collects submitted trial balances, translates each entity's local currency to the group currency (CTA on equity, average rates on the income statement), and applies the consolidation method per ownership — full consolidation with non-controlling interest, equity method or proportional. It eliminates intercompany balances, transactions and unrealised profit using matched trading-partner data, and supports parallel IFRS, local-GAAP and management views per IFRS 10.
