# Regulatory Reporting Engine

**Definition.** Regulatory Reporting Engine is the technology capability that aggregates, calculates, and formats prudential, financial, and transaction reports for submission to regulators such as the Fed, PRA, and EBA.
**Also known as:** RegTech Reporting.

- Regulatory Reporting Engine mentions Suade.

## Details
Regulatory Reporting Engine ingests ledger and risk data, applies regulatory calculation logic, and produces validated submissions and disclosures. Legacy platforms such as AxiomSL and Wolters Kluwer OneSumX are heavyweight and batch-oriented, while modern providers like Regnology and Suade use granular, data-driven and API-first models aligned to BCBS 239. It draws on warehoused and general-ledger data within the Data & Analytics domain.
