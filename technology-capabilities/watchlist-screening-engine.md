# Watchlist Screening Engine

**Definition.** Watchlist Screening Engine is the name/entity matching technology sub-capability that screens customers, counterparties and payments against sanctions, PEP, adverse-media and internal watchlists with fuzzy matching and hit disposition.
**Also known as:** Name Screening Engine, Sanctions Matching Engine, List Screening Engine, Filter Engine.

## Relationships
- Watchlist Screening Engine is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Watchlist Screening Engine is derived from Transaction Monitoring Platform.
- Watchlist Screening Engine depends on Watchlist Data Management.
- Watchlist Screening Engine is related to Sanctions Screening.

## Details
Watchlist Screening Engine, as in LexisNexis Bridger Insight XG and NICE Actimize Watchlist Filtering, applies fuzzy and phonetic matching algorithms — edit distance, transliteration, alias and name-order handling — to compare party and payment names against curated reference lists. Configurable matching thresholds, secondary identifiers (date of birth, country) and good-guy / whitelist suppression tune the precision/recall trade-off, and matches flow to a hit-disposition workflow for analyst review. It is the batch and customer-list screening capability of the FinCrime stack, distinct from inline in-flight payment filters.
