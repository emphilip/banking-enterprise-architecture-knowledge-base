# Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine

**Definition.** Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine is the technology capability that drives risk-and-control self-assessment (RCSA) workflows — inherent risk, control effectiveness, residual and target risk, KRIs and scenario analysis — across the operational-risk taxonomy.
**Also known as:** RCSA Engine, Risk Register Engine, Control Assessment Service.

## Relationships
- Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine is derived from Governance Risk & Compliance Platform.
- Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine depends on the Workflow Orchestration capability.
- Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine is related to Loss Event Register.

## Details
Risk Control Self-Assessment Engine structures the RCSA cycle: business units rate inherent risk against the operational-risk taxonomy, assess control design and operating effectiveness, and derive residual risk against target risk and appetite. It links key risk indicators (KRIs) and scenario analysis to the risk register and routes assessments, attestations and issues through workflow, as delivered by ServiceNow IRM and IBM OpenPages.
