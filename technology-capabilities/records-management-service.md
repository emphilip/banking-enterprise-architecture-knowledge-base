# Records Management Service

**Definition.** Records Management Service classifies records, enforces retention schedules, and maintains reliable audit trails for compliant recordkeeping.
**Also known as:** Records Management, Recordkeeping Service, RM Service.

## Relationships
- Records Management Service is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Records Management Service is derived from Document Management.
- Records Management Service depends on Content Repository.
- Records Management Service is related to Archival & Retention Service.

## Details
Records Management Service governs content that has been declared a record: a fixed, evidential document that must be retained, protected from alteration, and disposed of on schedule. It applies a file plan and retention categories, locks records against editing, and tracks a complete audit trail of access and disposition for examiners. Records management aligns with standards such as DoD 5015.2 and ISO 15489 and underpins regulatory obligations like SEC 17a-4 in banking. It works on content held in the repository and hands long-term preservation and defensible disposal to the archival and retention capability, distinguishing record declaration and classification from physical storage lifecycle.
