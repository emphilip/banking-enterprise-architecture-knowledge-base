# E-Signature Integration

**Definition.** E-Signature Integration embeds electronic and digital signature capture into document workflows to execute legally binding agreements.
**Also known as:** Electronic Signature Service, E-Sign Integration, Digital Signing Service.

## Relationships
- E-Signature Integration is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- E-Signature Integration is derived from Document Management.
- E-Signature Integration is related to Content Workflow Engine.
- E-Signature Integration is related to Content Repository.

## Details
E-Signature Integration connects ECM and workflow to signing services so customers and staff can sign agreements electronically, with the executed document and its evidence returned to the repository. Electronic signatures capture intent and consent, while digital signatures additionally bind the document with a certificate-based cryptographic seal that detects tampering. Compliance with frameworks such as the U.S. ESIGN Act, UETA, and the EU eIDAS regulation gives the resulting documents legal standing, supported by a tamper-evident audit certificate recording signer identity, timestamps, and IP. In banking this enables paperless account opening, loan agreements, and disclosures within an automated content workflow.
