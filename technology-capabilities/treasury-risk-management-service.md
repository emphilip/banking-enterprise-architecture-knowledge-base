# Treasury Risk Management Service

**Definition.** Treasury Risk Management Service is the technology capability that measures and manages treasury market exposures — FX, interest-rate and commodity — with exposure capture, hedge management and hedge-accounting support, plus counterparty and limit monitoring.
**Also known as:** FX & Risk Management Service, Hedge Management Service, Treasury Exposure Management.

## Relationships
- Treasury Risk Management Service is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Treasury Risk Management Service is derived from Treasury Management System.
- Treasury Risk Management Service is related to Deal Management.
- Treasury Risk Management Service is related to Risk Analytics Engine.

## Details
Treasury Risk Management Service consolidates forecast and balance-sheet exposures by currency, rate index and commodity, recommends and books hedges, and links derivatives to hedged items for IFRS 9 / ASC 815 hedge accounting (designation, effectiveness testing and reclassification). It monitors counterparty and settlement limits and tracks mark-to-market and VaR-style sensitivities on the hedge book, escalating limit breaches.
