# Economic Capital Engine

**Definition.** Economic Capital Engine is the technology capability that aggregates risk across credit, market and operational books to compute economic and regulatory capital, capital allocation, RAROC and risk-based pricing, and concentration analysis via simulation.
**Also known as:** Capital Engine, Economic Capital Calculator, RAROC Engine.

## Relationships
- Economic Capital Engine is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Economic Capital Engine is derived from Risk Analytics Engine.
- Economic Capital Engine is related to Credit Risk Model Engine.
- Economic Capital Engine is related to Stress Testing Engine.

## Details
Economic Capital Engine runs Monte Carlo simulation over the enterprise loss distribution to derive economic capital at a target solvency confidence level, then allocates that capital to business units, products and counterparties to compute risk-adjusted return on capital (RAROC) and support risk-based pricing. It performs single-name and sector concentration analysis and capital planning, as delivered by Moody's RiskFrontier and Moody's Analytics strategic capital planning solutions.
