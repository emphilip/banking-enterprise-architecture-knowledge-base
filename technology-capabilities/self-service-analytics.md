# Self-Service Analytics

**Definition.** Self-Service Analytics enables business users to explore and analyse data independently through governed drag-and-drop querying and guided workflows.
**Also known as:** Self-Serve Analytics, Ad-Hoc Exploration, Citizen Analytics.

## Relationships
- Self-Service Analytics is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Self-Service Analytics is derived from Analytics Platform.
- Self-Service Analytics depends on Semantic Layer Service.
- Self-Service Analytics is related to Natural Language Query Service.

## Details
Self-Service Analytics gives non-technical users curated datasets and visual, no-code tooling so they can build their own analyses without filing a request to a central analytics team. The key to safe self-service is a governed foundation: a semantic layer supplies vetted metrics, certified data, and enforced permissions so users explore freely while definitions stay consistent and sensitive data stays protected. Capabilities include drag-and-drop field selection, guided exploration, and shareable workbooks. This reduces the reporting backlog and shifts analysts from building bespoke reports to curating governed, reusable models.
