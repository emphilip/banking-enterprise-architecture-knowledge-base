# Pre-Trade Compliance Engine

**Definition.** Pre-Trade Compliance Engine is the technology capability that evaluates orders before routing against client mandates, regulatory limits and internal risk rules such as restricted lists, concentration and suitability, blocking or warning on breaches across the investment lifecycle.
**Also known as:** Pre-Trade Compliance, Investment Guideline Monitor, Mandate Compliance Engine.

## Relationships
- Pre-Trade Compliance Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Pre-Trade Compliance Engine is derived from Order Routing Engine.
- Pre-Trade Compliance Engine is related to Brokerage & Trading.
- Pre-Trade Compliance Engine is related to Regulatory Compliance.

## Details
Pre-Trade Compliance Engine runs investment guideline checks before an order is released to routing, testing each proposed trade against client mandates and IPS constraints, regulatory limits (e.g. UCITS, 40-Act), restricted and watch lists, and internal concentration and risk rules. Breaches are hard-blocked or flagged for override with audit trail. Platforms such as Charles River IMS and Bloomberg buy-side embed pre-trade and post-trade compliance directly in the OMS so the Order Routing Engine only forwards compliant orders, supporting continuous intraday monitoring.
