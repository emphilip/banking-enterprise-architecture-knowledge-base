# Portfolio Accounting Engine

**Definition.** Portfolio Accounting Engine is the technology capability that maintains the investment book-of-record: positions, lots, cash, accruals, trade and corporate-action posting, NAV and multi-currency valuation, producing the accounting truth for portfolios and accounts.
**Also known as:** Investment Accounting Engine, Portfolio Bookkeeping, Position & Cash Engine.

## Relationships
- Portfolio Accounting Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Portfolio Accounting Engine is derived from Portfolio Management System.
- Portfolio Accounting Engine is related to Portfolio Management.

## Details
Portfolio Accounting Engine is the IBOR ledger underneath the Portfolio Management System: it posts trades, income, fees and corporate actions to tax lots, accrues interest and dividends, and revalues holdings to compute NAV across multiple currencies and books. It supports trade-date and settlement-date accounting and reconciles to custodians. Platforms such as SS&C Advent Geneva, SS&C investment accounting and FIS trust accounting realize this engine, increasingly shifting from overnight batch to a real-time, intraday book-of-record that feeds performance, attribution and reporting downstream.
