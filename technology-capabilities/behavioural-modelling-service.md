# Behavioural Modelling Service

**Definition.** Behavioural Modelling Service is the technology capability that models customer behaviour driving balance-sheet risk — non-maturity deposit decay and rate sensitivity, loan prepayment, pipeline and early-redemption assumptions — including macro-linked behavioural models for stress.
**Also known as:** Behavioral Modeling Service, NMD Modelling Service, Prepayment Modelling Engine.

## Relationships
- Behavioural Modelling Service is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Behavioural Modelling Service is derived from Asset Liability Management Engine.
- Behavioural Modelling Service is related to IRRBB Analytics Engine.

## Details
Behavioural Modelling Service estimates the effective repricing and maturity profile of products whose contractual terms differ from observed behaviour. Non-maturity deposit (NMD) models split balances into stable core and volatile portions and assign decay profiles and pass-through betas; prepayment and early-redemption models project loan cash flows under rate paths. Macro-linked variants tie assumptions to scenario variables so the same behaviour feeds both IRRBB measurement and stress testing.
