# Risk Analytics Engine

**Definition.** Risk Analytics Engine is the high-performance computation technology capability that quantifies financial risk — value-at-risk (VaR), expected shortfall, credit exposure and PFE, CVA/XVA, expected and unexpected loss, and economic and regulatory capital — across asset classes using analytical, historical and Monte Carlo methods in near-real-time and batch.
**Also known as:** Risk Engine, Risk Computation Platform, Quant Risk Engine.

## Details
Risk Analytics Engine computes market risk metrics (parametric, historical and Monte Carlo VaR and expected shortfall), credit exposure and potential future exposure, valuation adjustments (CVA/DVA/FVA), and economic and regulatory capital. Legacy on-prem suites such as SAS Risk Management for Banking, Oracle OFSAA, Moody's RiskFrontier, IBM Algorithmics and FIS Adaptiv deliver simulation engines, economic scenario generators and capital analytics, while cloud-ready platforms like Murex MX.3 Risk, MSCI RiskMetrics and Numerix Oneview add elastic scalability, FRTB-SA/IMA and SA-CCR. The capability typically runs large Monte Carlo simulations across full or Taylor-based revaluation grids to derive tail-risk measures and capital charges.
