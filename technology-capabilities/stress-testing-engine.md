# Stress Testing Engine

**Definition.** Stress Testing Engine is the technology capability that runs enterprise stress tests and scenario analysis — CCAR/DFAST, EBA, reverse stress and what-if — applying macroeconomic and idiosyncratic scenarios to compute capital, loss and liquidity impacts.
**Also known as:** Scenario Analysis Engine, Enterprise Stress Testing.

## Relationships
- Stress Testing Engine is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Stress Testing Engine is derived from Risk Analytics Engine.
- Stress Testing Engine depends on the Risk Data Aggregation capability.
- Stress Testing Engine is related to Economic Capital Engine.

## Details
Stress Testing Engine translates macroeconomic and idiosyncratic scenarios into shocked risk-factor paths, then revalues positions and projects expected losses, pre-provision net revenue, capital ratios and liquidity over the supervisory horizon for CCAR/DFAST and EBA exercises. It supports reverse stress testing — solving for the scenario that breaches a capital or liquidity threshold — and what-if analysis, as delivered by the FIS Enterprise Risk Suite and Quantifi.
