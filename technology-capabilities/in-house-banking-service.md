# In-House Banking Service

**Definition.** In-House Banking Service is the technology capability that operates an internal bank for the group: intercompany loans and deposits, internal current accounts, payments-on-behalf-of (POBO) and collections-on-behalf-of (COBO), and cash-pooling with interest apportionment.
**Also known as:** IHB Service, Payment Factory, POBO/COBO Service, Internal Bank.

## Relationships
- In-House Banking Service is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- In-House Banking Service is derived from Treasury Management System.
- In-House Banking Service is related to Bank Connectivity Hub.

## Details
In-House Banking Service maintains internal current accounts for group entities and centralises external banking through a payment factory, executing POBO/COBO so subsidiaries transact via the in-house bank rather than external accounts. It runs notional and physical cash pools, books intercompany loans/deposits, and apportions interest across participants, reducing external accounts, bank fees and idle balances while concentrating liquidity.
