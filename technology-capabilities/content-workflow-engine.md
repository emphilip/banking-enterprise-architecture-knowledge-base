# Content Workflow Engine

**Definition.** Content Workflow Engine automates document-centric business processes using electronic forms, routing, reviews, and approvals over managed content.
**Also known as:** Content Workflow, Document Workflow, Process Automation (ECM).

## Relationships
- Content Workflow Engine is defined in the Data & Analytics domain.
- Content Workflow Engine is derived from Document Management.
- Content Workflow Engine depends on Content Repository.
- Content Workflow Engine is related to E-Signature Integration.

## Details
Content Workflow Engine drives the lifecycle of documents through human and system steps: a captured loan file or onboarding pack is routed to reviewers, escalated on timeouts, sent for approval, and updated with status as it moves. It combines electronic forms, business rules, task queues, and notifications so document-heavy banking processes run without manual hand-offs and with a complete audit of each decision. The engine acts on content stored in the repository and frequently invokes electronic signature steps to capture binding approvals within the flow. By codifying review and approval paths, it enforces segregation of duties and consistent controls across high-volume content processes.
