# Decision Rules Engine

**Definition.** Decision Rules Engine is the technology capability that executes underwriting and product-offering rules (policy rules, cut-offs, strategy trees) to produce approve/decline/refer decisions and offer terms.
**Also known as:** Underwriting Rules Engine, Policy Rules Engine.

## Relationships
- Decision Rules Engine is defined in the Core Processing domain.
- Decision Rules Engine is derived from Credit Decisioning Engine.
- Decision Rules Engine depends on Credit Scoring Service.

## Details
Decision Rules Engine runs credit policy rules, cut-offs and strategy trees over scores and applicant data to yield approve, decline or refer outcomes and to set offer terms. Provenir and Experian PowerCurve Strategy Management implement this as configurable strategy execution, consuming credit scores so policy logic and risk appetite are applied consistently across every origination decision.
