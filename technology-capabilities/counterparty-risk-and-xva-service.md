# Counterparty Risk & XVA Service

**Definition.** Counterparty Risk & XVA Service is the technology capability that calculates daily counterparty credit exposure, potential future exposure (PFE/SA-CCR/MPoR) and valuation adjustments (CVA/DVA/FVA — XVA) feeding limits and capital.
**Also known as:** XVA Engine, Counterparty Credit Risk Service, PFE Engine.

## Relationships
- Counterparty Risk & XVA Service is defined in the AI & Automation domain.
- Counterparty Risk & XVA Service is derived from Market Risk Engine.
- Counterparty Risk & XVA Service is related to Credit Risk Model Engine.

## Details
Counterparty Risk & XVA Service simulates netting-set exposure profiles over the margin period of risk (MPoR) to produce expected exposure and potential future exposure (PFE) percentile profiles, then computes the regulatory SA-CCR exposure for counterparty credit risk capital. It prices the valuation adjustments — credit (CVA), debit (DVA) and funding (FVA) — that flow into fair value and CVA capital, as delivered by the FIS XVA data services.
