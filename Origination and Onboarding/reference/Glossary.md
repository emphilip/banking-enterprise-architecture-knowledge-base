# Glossary

Terms used throughout the onboarding and origination library, defined for an average Canadian retail banking context.

## Application & Decisioning

**Applied product** — The single product an application session is for (term loan, short-term loan, or credit card), fixed by the applicant's entry point rather than selected mid-flow. One application = one product.

**Pre-qualification** — A preliminary eligibility decision based on applicant-declared data and a **soft inquiry**; produces an indicative amount or range without affecting the credit score. See [[Pre-Qualification Flow]].

**Adjudication** — The final credit decision after verification, typically involving a **hard inquiry** for credit cards, returning an approved amount range or credit limit. See [[Adjudication and Underwriting]].

**Soft inquiry** — A credit-bureau check that does not affect the applicant's credit score and is not visible to other lenders; used for pre-qualification.

**Hard inquiry** — A bureau check recorded on the credit file and potentially affecting the score; requires prior express consent and, in these flows, an explicit acknowledgement immediately beforehand on the card path.

**Decision engine** — The centralized service that evaluates applications against credit policy and returns structured outcomes; the front end renders decisions but never implements credit logic. See [[Integration and Decisioning Patterns]].

**Straight-through processing (STP)** — An application completing from intake to funded account with no human intervention; the design goal of the automated paths.

**Manual review** — The human-adjudicated fallback whenever automation can't complete (document proof, IDV failure, repeated linking errors); terminal for the self-serve session. See [[Manual Review Flow]].

**Counter-offer** — An alternative product or amount offered after a decline. Not generated by the front end in these flows; declines route to generic alternatives messaging.

## Verification

**Financial data aggregator** — A third-party service (e.g., Flinks, Plaid in the Canadian market) that, with consent, retrieves account and transaction data from the applicant's bank to verify income and account ownership. See [[Income Verification Flow]].

**IDV (identity verification) provider** — A specialist vendor performing document-and-biometric identity checks (ID front/back capture plus selfie video liveness). See [[Identity Verification Flow]].

**KYC (Know Your Customer)** — The regulatory obligation to identify and verify clients before establishing a relationship; in Canada, governed by the PCMLTFA. See [[AML KYC and Compliance]].

**Carrier-data verification (PICRA-style consent)** — Consent permitting the bank to verify applicant identity attributes against mobile-carrier records; gates entry to the verified application in the source flows.

**OTP (one-time passcode)** — A short-lived numeric code (6 digits here) sent to the applicant's phone to verify possession; limited attempts before escalation to an assisted channel.

**Unverified income** — Manually declared income pending document review; badged distinctly from aggregator-verified income until cleared.

**Provenance** — The recorded source of a data element (aggregator-verified, manually declared, carrier-confirmed), retained so adjudication can weight evidence appropriately.

## Funding & Repayment

**PAD (pre-authorized debit)** — Standing authorization for the bank to withdraw payments from the customer's account, governed by Payments Canada **Rule H1**; requires a signed PAD agreement.

**e-Transfer (Interac e-Transfer)** — Email-addressed near-real-time payment rail; used for loan disbursement (1–2 hours in the source), with the destination email locked post-verification as a fraud control.

**EFT (electronic funds transfer)** — Batch direct-deposit rail used for slower disbursement (1–2 business days) to a transit/institution/account-numbered bank account.

**Transit number / institution number** — Canadian account routing identifiers: a 5-digit branch (transit) number and a 3-digit financial-institution number.

**Primary account** — When multiple accounts are linked via the aggregator, the account with the highest net deposits; designated (immutably) as the PAD account.

**Embedded card capture** — A vendor-hosted component for entering debit-card details so the bank never handles the PAN, keeping the journey out of most **PCI-DSS** scope.

## Products & Pricing

**Term loan / installment loan** — A loan repaid in scheduled installments over a term; configured by amount, frequency, and term within the approved envelope.

**Short-term loan / single-payment loan** — A small-dollar loan repaid in one or few payments, priced as a fee per $100 borrowed and subject to the federal **$14 per $100** cap (see [[Canadian Regulatory Context]]).

**Creditor insurance (loan protection)** — Optional insurance covering loan payments on defined events; must be expressly elected, with premiums disclosed, and a decline may trigger a single rebuttal presentation.

**Balance protection** — The credit-card analogue of creditor insurance; optional, with express election and (in the source) a signed agreement governing opt-out.

**APR (annual percentage rate)** — The annualized total cost of borrowing including non-interest charges; capped federally at the **criminal interest rate of 35% APR** (since Jan 1, 2025).

**Cost of borrowing** — The regulated disclosure of total interest and charges; presented pre-agreement and in worked examples (cost per $100, total repayable).

**Cooling-off period** — A statutory window after signing during which the borrower may cancel without penalty; duration varies by province and product.

## Regulatory & Compliance (Canada)

**FINTRAC** — Canada's financial-intelligence unit administering the **PCMLTFA** (*Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act*); source of client-identification, reporting, and record-keeping obligations.

**FCAC** — Financial Consumer Agency of Canada; supervises federally regulated institutions' market conduct, including cost-of-borrowing disclosure and express consent for optional products.

**PIPEDA** — Federal private-sector privacy law governing collection, use, and disclosure of personal information; Québec's Law 25 imposes additional provincial requirements.

**CASL** — Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation; the basis for the optional, never-required marketing consent checkbox.

**Rule H1** — Payments Canada's rule governing pre-authorized debit agreements (mandatory PAD agreement contents and payor authorization).

**EDD (enhanced due diligence)** — Heightened verification and monitoring for higher-risk clients (e.g., PEPs); a capability under [[AML KYC and Compliance]].

**PEP/HIO** — Politically exposed person / head of international organization; categories requiring screening and EDD under the PCMLTFA.

**E-signature** — Electronic execution of agreements; validity and formality requirements vary by province (the source notes BC, NL, and MB variations), making signing rules province-keyed configuration.

**High-cost credit regime** — Provincial licensing/disclosure regimes (BC, AB, MB, QC) applying to credit above defined rate thresholds, adding cancellation rights and disclosure duties.

## Library Conventions

**Capability ID** — Identifiers of the form `ONB-XXX-nn`: `ONB` (Onboarding & Origination L0), a three-letter L1 group (APP, ADJ, AKC, ASF, CCC, ACT), and a two-digit L2 capability number. See [[Onboarding and Origination Capability Model]].

**MOC (map of content)** — The index note linking the library together: [[00 - Onboarding and Origination Index]].
